# Corporate Event Outreach Template
**Tribal Cowboy LLC — B2B Sales Outreach**

---

## Who to Reach

Target the person who controls the event or employee experience budget. That is rarely the CEO.

| Role | Why They're the Right Contact | How to Find Them |
|---|---|---|
| **HR Manager / HR Director** | Owns employee appreciation events, company culture initiatives, benefits adjacent experiences | LinkedIn search: "[Company] HR Manager" or "People Operations" |
| **Executive Assistant** | Books leadership team events, off-sites, and executive-facing experiences | LinkedIn search: "[Company] Executive Assistant" — look for EA to CEO/President |
| **Events Director / Events Manager** | Owns the calendar of company events at mid-to-large companies | LinkedIn search: "[Company] Events" |
| **Office Manager** | At smaller companies (under 50 employees), they often own the event budget entirely | LinkedIn search: "[Company] Office Manager" |

Do not cold-pitch the CEO. Do not cold-pitch Sales or Marketing unless the company is small enough that those people also run events.

---

## Outreach Channels

| Channel | Use When | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn DM** | You've identified the right contact and they're active on LinkedIn | Keep it short — 3–4 sentences max. LinkedIn is not email. |
| **Email** | You have a direct email or can find it via company website | More room to tell the story. Still keep it under 200 words. |
| **Venue referrals** | A venue that hosts corporate events recommends TC to their clients | Warm intro — respond faster and more personally than cold outreach |
| **Event vendor referrals** | Caterers, photographers, planners who work corporate events | Ask for referrals proactively, especially after strong events |

## Subject Line Options

Concise, benefit-forward, not cute. Decision-makers get 80 emails a day.

1. `Team event idea — Tribal Cowboy (North Idaho horse experience)`
2. `Something different for your next company event — Clydesdale wagon rides + horse photography`
3. `Employee appreciation idea for [Company] — Tribal Cowboy LLC`
4. `Corporate event inquiry — Tribal Cowboy, Athol ID`

Avoid: "Exciting opportunity," "Partnership proposal," "Quick question" — all overused and flag as vendor spam.

## What to Include in Every Corporate Outreach

- TC's credibility signals: Indigenous-owned, real horses (not rented), established operation, instant photo delivery tech
- What the event actually looks like: format, duration, group size, what's managed vs. not
- A reason it works for corporate specifically: team photos for Slack/Teams/LinkedIn, employee appreciation, off-site alternative
- Easy CTA: "Can I send a one-pager?" or "Do you have upcoming events where this might fit?" — not "Book now"

## Tone Notes

This is professional but human. TC is not a vendor trying to get on a preferred list. TC is a premium experience that solves a real problem for event planners who are sick of the same options.

- Professional enough for a corporate inbox
- Human enough that it doesn't read like a CRM sequence
- Confident — TC's offer is strong. Write it that way.
- Not pushy — the CTA is always low-commitment
