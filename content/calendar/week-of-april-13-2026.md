## WEEKLY STRATEGY

It's mid-April. Spring booking season is open and summer dates are filling. This week's content does two things: shows the horses being real (personality, prep, behind the scenes) and makes it easy for someone scrolling to picture booking. Every post works without event footage — all filmable at the barn or to camera.

Mix of pillars: 2 animal personality, 1 work/behind-the-scenes, 1 education, 1 booking/promo.

## MONDAY — Instagram Reel + TikTok

**Pillar:** The Animals
**Format:** Short clip (15-20 sec), trending audio
**Concept:** "Millie thinks she's a lap dog"

Film Millie doing something big-horse-small-brain — nudging you for treats, blocking a gate, standing too close, trying to follow you inside. The joke is a 1,800-lb Clydesdale who thinks she's a golden retriever.

**HOOK (text on screen):** "She's 1,800 lbs and has zero concept of personal space."

**BODY:** 3-4 quick cuts of Millie being Millie. Let the audio do the work. No voiceover needed. Text overlay at the end: "Book Millie for your next event — link in bio"

**CAPTION:**
Millie has never met a boundary she respected. 1,800 lbs of horse who thinks she fits in your lap. She's available for your festival, corporate event, or birthday party this summer. Dates are going fast.

**HASHTAGS:**
#TribalCowboy #ClydesdaleHorse #HorseTok #NorthIdaho #HorsesOfInstagram #ClydesdaleTok #EquestrianLife #IdahoHorses

**FILMING NOTES:** Shoot at the barn. Morning light is best. Get low angles to emphasize her size. Let her come to you — don't stage it, just keep the camera rolling.

## WEDNESDAY — Instagram Reel + TikTok

**Pillar:** The Work
**Format:** Voiceover + b-roll (30-45 sec)
**Concept:** "What it actually takes to bring horses to your event"

Behind-the-scenes of event prep: loading the trailer, grooming, packing gear, checking tack, loading the photo equipment. Show the labor. This is the content that builds trust with event planners — they see the professionalism before they ever email you.

**HOOK (voiceover, first 2 seconds):** "People see the horses at the event. They don't see the 4am alarm."

**BODY (voiceover beats, not a script — hit these points naturally):**
- Grooming starts early. Every horse gets bathed, brushed, and braided.
- Equipment check: wagon hitch, photo gear, QR delivery setup, branded overlays loaded.
- Load the trailer. Drive to the site. Set up before anyone arrives.
- By the time the first guest walks up, it looks effortless. It's not.

**CTA (text on screen):** "Want this at your event? Link in bio."

**CAPTION:**
The setup starts hours before anyone shows up. Every horse groomed, every piece of gear checked, every branded overlay loaded and tested. By the time the first guest walks up, it looks like magic. It's not. It's 4am and a trailer full of Clydesdales.

**HASHTAGS:**
#TribalCowboy #BehindTheScenes #HorseLife #EventSetup #NorthIdaho #ClydesdaleHorse #HorseTok #EquestrianLife

**FILMING NOTES:** Film the actual morning routine — no need to stage it. Use time-lapse for grooming and loading. Steady phone shots. Natural light.

**POST:** 12pm–2pm. This is the kind of content event planners save and share. Optimize for saves.

## THURSDAY — Instagram Story (3-4 slides)

**Pillar:** Education
**Format:** Text + photo stories
**Concept:** "3 things to know before you book horses for your event"

Quick, useful, saveable. This is for the event planner or parent who's thinking about it but hasn't reached out yet.

**Slide 1:** "Thinking about booking horses for your event? Here's what to know first." (photo of horses at property)

**Slide 2:** "1. You need flat outdoor space. We bring everything else — the horses, the handlers, the gear, the photography. But we need a level area for the animals. A parking lot, a field, a park — all work."

**Slide 3:** "2. Book 4-8 weeks out for peak season. Summer dates go fast. Spring still has openings. The earlier you reach out, the more options you have."

**Slide 4:** "3. Every guest goes home with branded photos. We set up a QR code at your event. Guests scan it and get their photos — with your event name, logo, and date — on their phone before they leave. It's included in every package."

**Final slide:** "Check your date → link in bio" with a tap-forward arrow
