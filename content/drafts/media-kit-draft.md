## Who We Are

Tribal Cowboy LLC is an Indigenous-owned equine experience business in Athol, Idaho, founded and operated by Stacie — a Nisenan Maidu woman who has been working with horses in North Idaho long before it became a content category. We bring real Clydesdales and ponies to corporate events, family celebrations, school programs, and community gatherings across North Idaho and Eastern Washington — and every event we run is paired with instant photo delivery instant branded photo delivery, a technology differentiator no other equine vendor in this region offers. The business is owner-operated: one person, one herd, one standard, done right every time.

## Our Audience

TC's audience is concentrated in North Idaho, Eastern Washington, and the broader Pacific Northwest — a region where outdoor life, western heritage, and land-based experience are not aspirational aesthetics but daily realities. These are people who spend money on experiences rather than accumulating things: families booking something worth showing up for, HR coordinators searching for a team event that doesn't feel recycled, and outdoor-oriented adults who follow horse content because they live adjacent to that life.

The demographic profile skews toward adults 25–50, with strong female representation in the booking-decision role. The household income signal is above average — TC's own customers are people who pay for quality equine experiences, which means they are already primed to invest in what a brand partner is selling. The equestrian community segment is particularly high-trust and high-engagement; when a horse person follows an account, they are following with real attention, not passive scrolling. The Indigenous community and allied audience brings an additional layer of intentional followership — people who are here because they found something worth supporting.

What brands rarely find in niche outdoor and equine accounts is an audience that is also an active buyer. TC's followers are TC's customers. That means a brand placing a product in TC's content is reaching people who already demonstrate the willingness to pay for real experiences.

## Brand Values and Non-Negotiables

**What TC stands for in a partnership:**

TC is a working operation with a real identity. Brand partnerships that align with that identity are ones that fit TC's visual world (outdoor, western, equine, Pacific Northwest), respect the premium positioning of the business, and are comfortable being associated with an Indigenous-owned creator who is not going to neutralize her identity for brand convenience.

TC believes in disclosure. Sponsored content is labeled. That's not a compromise — it is a baseline.

TC's animals are not props. Any content partnership that reduces the horses to backdrops or asks TC to produce content that diminishes their role is not a fit.

**What TC will not do:**

- Gifting-only partnerships with no cash compensation, regardless of product value above a reasonable threshold
- Partnerships with brands whose public record, values, or visual identity conflicts with TC's premium positioning
- Content that requires TC to misrepresent how a product is used or how the operation works
- Exclusivity agreements in TC's core service category (equine experiences) at rates below the appropriate premium
- Rushed multi-piece deliverables — quality takes the time it takes
- Any content that asks TC to use Indigenous identity as a brand decoration rather than allowing it to be present as it naturally is
