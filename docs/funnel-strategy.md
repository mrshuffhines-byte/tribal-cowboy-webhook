# Tribal Cowboy — Funnel Strategy

*How strangers become followers, followers become buyers, and buyers become advocates. No CRM required.*

---

## Funnel Overview

Tribal Cowboy's funnel has six stages. Most buyers don't move through all six in one visit — they enter at different points and move at different speeds. The content system's job is to serve every stage so nobody stalls for lack of information, trust, or a clear next step.

```
DISCOVER → TRUST → CONSIDER → INQUIRE → BOOK → ADVOCATE
```

Each stage requires different content, a different platform priority, and a different type of CTA. The biggest mistake most small businesses make is posting only Discover content (entertaining, no ask) or only Book content (always selling). The funnel requires both, plus everything in between.

---

## Stage 1: DISCOVER

**Goal:** Get in front of someone who doesn't know Tribal Cowboy exists.

**Who's here:** Cold audience. Strangers scrolling. Someone whose friend tagged them. Someone searching #NorthIdaho or #HorseParty.

**What content belongs here:**
- Pillar 1 (The Animals) — high engagement, shareable, no ask required
- Pillar 7 (North Idaho Life) — regional discovery, hashtag-driven
- Pillar 6 (Education) — searchable, saveable, attracts new followers
- Pillar 2 (The Moments) — emotional, shareable, earns follows

**Platform priority:** TikTok first (organic reach, new audience), Instagram Reels second, Facebook shares third.

**CTAs appropriate here:** Soft or none. "Follow for more" is acceptable. No booking asks at this stage.

**How the website fits:** It doesn't yet. People at this stage are not going to the website. The Instagram profile and TikTok bio do the orientation work.

**How creator content accelerates it:** Sponsored posts, collaborations with regional creators, and UGC from guests all expand Discover reach beyond the organic follower base.

**Signals that this stage is working:** Follower growth, reel/video views from non-followers, saves on new posts, comments from new accounts.

---

## Stage 2: TRUST

**Goal:** Turn a new follower into someone who consistently pays attention.

**Who's here:** Recent followers. Someone who liked one post and is now browsing the profile. Someone who followed months ago and is warming up.

**What content belongs here:**
- Pillar 3 (The Work) — shows commitment, earns respect, builds credibility
- Pillar 4 (Indigenous Identity) — depth and distinctiveness, builds personal loyalty
- Pillar 1 (The Animals) — ongoing relationship with the animals as characters
- Pillar 7 (North Idaho Life) — place as community, regional belonging

**Platform priority:** Instagram (grid + Stories for ongoing relationship), TikTok (content worth following for), Facebook (community posts and behind-the-scenes).

**CTAs appropriate here:** None in most posts. The goal is continued engagement, not conversion. Let trust accumulate.

**How the website fits:** Some Trust-stage followers will visit the website out of curiosity — the About page and Instagram-linked bio should feel consistent with what they've seen on social.

**How creator content accelerates it:** Consistent content quality signals a professional operation. Inconsistency (gaps, tonal shifts, quality drops) breaks trust faster than anything else.

**Signals that this stage is working:** Repeat engagement from the same accounts, saves, profile visits, Story replies, comments that reference previous posts ("I was waiting for this").

---

## Stage 3: CONSIDER

**Goal:** Move someone from passive follower to active evaluation — they're thinking about booking.

**Who's here:** Followers who have been watching for weeks or months. A family mom who keeps saving posts. A corporate planner who's been circling. Someone whose kid's birthday is coming up.

**What content belongs here:**
- Pillar 2 (The Moments) — social proof through real guest reactions
- Pillar 6 (Education) — removes uncertainty about how it works
- Pillar 5 (Events and Booking) — availability information that makes it concrete
- Pillar 8 (Brand Authority) — credibility signals that confirm the decision is safe

**Platform priority:** Instagram (Stories with links, carousels with detail), Facebook (longer explanatory posts, shared in community groups), website (they will visit the website at this stage).

**CTAs appropriate here:** Soft booking CTAs are appropriate. "Check dates at www.tribalcowboy.com" or "DM us to check availability." Not "book now" — they're not ready for urgency.

**How the website fits:** This is when the website becomes critical. The Consider-stage visitor will read the services page, look for pricing, and look for evidence that this business is real and reliable. If the website is outdated, unclear, or missing pricing, they stall here.

**How creator content accelerates it:** Testimonials, guest-tagged content, and UGC all serve as third-party validation that moves Consider-stage visitors forward faster than first-party content.

**Signals that this stage is working:** Profile link clicks, website visits from social, DMs asking questions, email inquiries that reference social posts.

---

## Stage 4: INQUIRE

**Goal:** Get a direct message, email, or contact form submission.

**Who's here:** Someone with intent. They have a date in mind, a budget in mind, and they need one more piece of information or a human response to move forward.

**What content belongs here:** This stage happens off the feed — in DMs, email, and the contact form on the website. Content supports it by making the inquiry step frictionless.

**Platform priority:** Instagram DMs for families, email for corporate buyers and executive assistants. Website contact form as a backup.

**CTAs appropriate here:** Direct. "Email info@tribalcowboy.com" or "DM us with your date." One action, not two.

**Lead nurture logic (what to do after an inquiry comes in):**
1. Respond within 24 hours. Same day preferred. Corporate inquiries should receive a reply within 4 business hours.
2. Confirm what they're looking for: event type, date, guest count, location.
3. Confirm availability or offer alternatives.
4. Send a clear summary of what's included and the price. Not a long PDF — a few sentences or a clean bullet list.
5. Follow up once if they go quiet. One follow-up, not three.
6. If they book: confirm in writing, send any prep info they need.
7. If they don't book: that's fine. Don't chase.

**How the website fits:** The inquiry form on the website should be simple — name, email, event type, preferred date, and a message box. Nothing else. Every additional field reduces completion rate.

**Signals that this stage is working:** Inquiries received, response rate, inquiry-to-booking conversion.

---

## Stage 5: BOOK

**Goal:** Confirm the booking, collect deposit if applicable, prepare the guest.

**Who's here:** A committed buyer.

**Content at this stage:** Pre-event communication (what to expect, what to wear, what to bring). This is not social content — it is direct communication via email.

**Platform priority:** Email. DM as a supplement for families who prefer it.

**How the website fits:** If there's an online booking tool, this is where it earns its value. If booking is manual (email/DM), confirm everything in writing and keep it simple.

**Signals that this stage is working:** Deposits received, events confirmed, no-show rate is low, repeat bookings from previous guests.

---

## Stage 6: ADVOCATE

**Goal:** Turn a guest into someone who brings new people into the funnel.

**Who's here:** A happy customer after the event.

**What content belongs here:**
- Guest-tagged posts — re-share (with permission) on Stories
- Direct ask for a review or referral (one time, after the event, done warmly — not a mass email)
- instant photo delivery delivers shareable content automatically — every guest who gets their photos is a potential advocate because they have photos worth sharing

**Platform priority:** Instagram Stories (re-shares of guest content), Facebook (where local shares and recommendations happen in community groups).

**CTAs appropriate here:** "Tag us if you share your photos" — said once, before the event ends.

**How creator content accelerates it:** If guests leave with branded, high-quality photos, they are far more likely to share them. The branded overlay on every photo is passive advertising.

**Signals that this stage is working:** Tagged posts from guests, referral inquiries ("my friend booked you last summer"), positive comments under posts from former guests.

## Corporate Buyer Funnel Path

Corporate buyers move differently. They skip the long trust-building phase because they are searching for a solution, not following content for enjoyment.

```
SEARCH → EVALUATE → INQUIRE → APPROVE → BOOK
```

**Search:** Google, venue referrals, colleague recommendations. They arrive at the website first, not social.

**Evaluate:** Website review (services, pricing, credentials), then Instagram review (does this look professional? is there corporate event content?). They need both to feel confident.

**Inquire:** Email is the preferred channel. They need a professional, specific response — not a DM reply.

**Approve:** Corporate buyers often need internal approval. Give them what they need to present: a clear summary of what's included, starting price, and contact information they can forward.

**Book:** Once approved, corporate bookings move quickly. Make the confirmation process easy.

**What this means for content:** Maintain a visible corporate event presence on Instagram and a dedicated corporate page on the website. Corporate buyers will check both. If neither exists, they move on.

## Warm Engagement Rules vs. Hard Sell Rules

### Warm Engagement (any post, any stage)
- Respond to every comment within 24 hours
- Reply to Story replies — even one-word replies deserve acknowledgment
- If someone asks a question in comments, answer it publicly (everyone else has the same question)
- Like and respond to tags from guests
- Never sell in a comment reply — if someone expresses interest, move it to DM

### Hard Sell (only in Pillar 5 posts)
- Clear pricing (at minimum "starting at")
- Clear availability (specific dates or timeframes)
- One direct CTA (URL or email, not both)
- No urgency language
- No follow-up comments on non-booking posts that push toward a sale

## How to Move People from Follower to Inquiry Without Sounding Pushy

The answer is patience and specificity — not tactics.

1. Post booking content on a predictable cadence so followers know to expect it. They'll notice when the "May availability" post drops.
2. When availability is real and specific, say it plainly: "We have three Saturdays open in June." Specificity creates urgency without pressure language.
3. Respond to engagement on non-booking posts warmly. The relationship is what converts — not a closing line.
4. Use Stories for soft booking prompts — they disappear in 24 hours and don't dominate the feed.
5. Never follow up on a comment or DM with "did you decide yet?" One warm follow-up is acceptable after an inquiry goes cold. One.

## How to Track Funnel Health Without Analytics Tools

You don't need a dashboard. Watch for these signals weekly:

| Stage | Healthy Signal | Warning Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Discover | New followers from non-followers (check who's commenting/following) | Follower count flat or declining |
| Trust | Repeat engagement from same accounts | Only likes, no saves or comments |
| Consider | DMs asking questions about booking | Profile link clicks not resulting in inquiries |
| Inquire | Inquiry messages received | Silence for 2+ weeks after a booking post |
| Book | Events confirmed, deposit received | Inquiries that go quiet after first response |
| Advocate | Guest-tagged posts, referral mentions | No organic re-shares after events |

One signal in the wrong direction is noise. Two weeks of the same warning signal in the same stage is a problem worth addressing.
