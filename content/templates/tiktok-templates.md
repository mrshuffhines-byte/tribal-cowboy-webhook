# TikTok Post Templates — Tribal Cowboy

TikTok lives or dies by the hook. First line = everything.

---

## Hook Formulas That Work for Tribal Cowboy

- "POV: You hired horses for your kid's birthday party"
- "Things nobody tells you about running a horse business in North Idaho"
- "We showed up to a corporate event with a 1,800-lb Clydesdale"
- "Watch what happens when a toddler meets a draft horse for the first time"
- "I'm Indigenous-owned and I run this [show the horses]"
- "What a $[price] pony party actually looks like"

---

## Template 1: POV / Moment Video

**Format:** Short clip (15–30 sec) of a real moment, caption tells the story.

```
HOOK: POV: [relatable situation]

BODY: [what's happening on screen]

CLOSE: [the payoff or punchline]

CAPTION: [1-2 lines + tags]
```

**Script example:**
```
Hook (on screen text): POV: A 6-year-old meets a Clydesdale for the first time

[show: kid walking up, stopping, wide eyes]

[show: kid whispers something to parent, then turns back to the horse]

Close (on screen text): She asked if she could live here. We said we'd check.

Caption: This is basically our whole business model. 🐴
#HorseTok #TribalCowboy #NorthIdaho #ClydesdaleHorse #PonyParty
```

## Template 2: Day-in-the-Life / Behind the Scenes

**Format:** Quick montage of a day — morning through event. Text overlays.

```
HOOK: [Time] — [what's happening]

BEAT 2: [Next moment]

BEAT 3: [Next moment]

CLOSE: [The contrast — the work vs. the result]

Caption: [1-2 lines + tags]
```

**Script example:**
```
Text: 4:30am — loading the trailer

Text: 8am — on the road
Text: 10am — kids are already losing their minds

Caption: Nobody sees the 4am. They just see the magic.

```

## Template 3: Talking Head / Explainer

**Format:** Stacie or someone from the team talking directly to camera.

```
HOOK (spoken): "[Surprising statement or question]"

BODY (spoken): [2-3 sentences explaining something real]

CLOSE (spoken): "[Callback or punchline]"

Caption: [1-2 lines + tags]
```

**Script example:**
```
Hook: "People always ask why the horses aren't scared of crowds."

Body: "Clydesdales were bred to work in the middle of chaos — battlefields, coal yards, city streets. Millie has been doing events since she was three. A birthday party with 50 kids is basically a relaxing Tuesday for her."

Close: "Meanwhile I'm the one who needs a nap."

Caption: Millie energy is real.
#HorseTok #ClydesdaleHorse #TribalCowboy #HorseFacts #NorthIdaho
```

## TikTok Best Practices for Tribal Cowboy

- **Hook in 0-2 seconds** — don't save the good stuff
- **Vertical video only** — 9:16 ratio
- **Captions on screen** — many people watch without sound
- **15–45 seconds** is the sweet spot for this content type
- **Don't over-edit** — raw and real outperforms polished for this brand
- **Trending audio** works, but don't force it — use silence or ambient horse sounds when that's more honest
- **Comment back** — TikTok rewards engagement in the first hour
