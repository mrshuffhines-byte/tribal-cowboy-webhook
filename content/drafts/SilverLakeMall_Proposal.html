Space Requirements

Full three-zone setup requires a minimum 40' × 60' footprint — outdoor, covered, or a combination. Pony loop needs level ground. Clydesdale photo area: 15' × 20'. Photo delivery station works indoors or outdoors.

Tribal Cowboy will advise on optimal layout for Silver Lake's specific footprint — parking lot, entry corridor, or event lawn. We have operated in all three.

Setup & Teardown

Tribal Cowboy handles all setup and teardown. Arrival 2 hours before open. Teardown complete within 90 minutes of close. Animals arrive in Tribal Cowboy's own trailer and are managed exclusively by our handlers. No mall staff involvement required.

Staffing

Every activation includes a minimum of 2 animal handlers, 1 photo delivery operator, and 1 guest flow coordinator. Staffing scales to event size. All Tribal Cowboy personnel are uniformed and branded throughout the event.

Our animals — not rented stock. Every activation follows our daily ranch protocols: no work above 90°F · 45-min max per pony · current vet certificates · SAVE Veterinary on-call · diaper bags on all animals · all animals pre-conditioned to crowds and music before deployment.

Common Questions
What Decision-Makers Ask
Straight Answers to the Hard Questions
1. What if a child gets hurt?
No rider is ever on a pony unsupervised. Lead-line only — a handler holds the pony and controls all movement at every step. The enclosure, fencing, and rider weight limit (80 lbs max) are additional layers. National emergency room data shows 98.2% of equestrian injuries involve independent riding or being thrown — both of which are structurally impossible in our format. Tribal Cowboy carries $2M per-occurrence general liability insurance, and the Certificate of Insurance names Silver Lake Mall as additionally insured. An Emergency Action Plan is on file with your team before gates open.
2. What does the mall actually have to do?
Approve the event, designate a space, and promote it through your existing channels (email, social, signage). That's it. Tribal Cowboy handles all setup, teardown, animal handling, guest flow management, photo operations, permitting coordination, and post-event cleanup. No mall staff involvement is required on event day — not for animal handling, not for crowd management, not for photo delivery. You host the event. We run it.
3. How do you handle waste and sanitation in a retail environment?
All working animals wear diaper bags. The entire enclosed footprint is laid with ground cover or appropriate flooring that Tribal Cowboy brings and removes. Any waste is collected and removed by our team — no mall custodial involvement required. Setup and breakdown includes full cleanup of the activation area. We have operated in indoor/adjacent spaces before. This has never been an issue.
4. What if the weather doesn't cooperate or turnout is low?
The activation is designed for outdoor, covered, or combination footprints — we advise on optimal placement for Silver Lake's space during planning. For weather, we build a hold protocol into the Emergency Action Plan: activation pauses, animals are secured in the trailer, and resumes when conditions allow. If turnout is lower than projected, the fixed base fee is the mall's only cost — there's no performance penalty. A smaller first event still validates the model, generates content, and builds the audience for the next one.
5. What if parking lot or load-in logistics are complicated?
Tribal Cowboy arrives with our own truck and trailer — we need a designated unloading lane and access to the activation footprint, ideally accessible without crossing active pedestrian traffic. We scout the location in advance and advise on the cleanest approach. Our trailer fits in a standard commercial vehicle parking bay. If a vendor staging area exists, we use it. If not, we coordinate directly with your facilities team to identify the lowest-disruption load-in route. This is handled before event day — not improvised on arrival.
