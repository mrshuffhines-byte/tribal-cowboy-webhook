## What Trends to Track

TC does not need to track every trend. These are the five categories that matter.

### 1. TikTok Format Shifts
How TikTok presents content is changing constantly. Format trends affect reach before they affect taste. Track:
- Video length trends (are shorter or longer videos performing better this month?)
- Text-on-screen styles (subtitle style, overlay style, talking-head vs. B-roll trends)
- Sound trends relevant to TC's world (country, western, Indigenous, ambient nature)
- Series formats (part 1/2/3 structure, "POV" framing, "tell me without telling me")
- Hook structures (what first-line patterns are performing in TC's adjacent content)

**Where to check:** TikTok Creative Center (free), scroll TC's FYP with fresh eyes weekly.

### 2. Western Lifestyle Content Trends
The western lifestyle content space is large and evolving. What's happening in TC's content neighborhood:
- What ranch, farm, and horse accounts are posting that's getting traction
- Aesthetic shifts (is the tone getting more rustic, more premium, more humor-forward?)
- What content from draft horse or Clydesdale accounts is being shared widely
- Pony party and children's event content patterns

**Where to check:** @horsesofinstagram saves, TikTok #HorseTok, Instagram Explore in equine content.

### 3. Creator Economy Shifts
This affects TC's monetization strategy directly:
- Which creator platforms are growing or declining (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts)
- Changes to creator fund eligibility or payout structures
- New brand partnership platforms launching
- What content formats brands are currently spending budget on (UGC is up, static posts are down — track shifts)
- Indigenous creator space: new programs, new platforms, new conversations

**Where to check:** Creator Economy newsletter, Later Blog, Social Media Today.

### 4. Luxury and Premium Consumer Behavior
TC is positioning as a premium brand. What premium consumers are responding to:
- Experiential spending trends (post-pandemic experiential preference is strong — track changes)
- What premium outdoor and western brands are marketing (signals what buyers want to see)
- Event industry premium positioning: what are upscale event vendors doing differently?

**Where to check:** Skift (travel/events industry), Instagram Explore in luxury outdoor and western categories.

### 5. Sponsorship and Brand Partnership Trends
This directly affects creator income:
- Which brand categories are increasing creator marketing spend
- New Indigenous creator partnership programs launching
- UGC platform changes (Billo, Backstage, Fiverr Pro — how demand is shifting)
- Regional tourism board content partnerships (these have budget cycles — track when they open)

**Where to check:** Later Blog, influencer marketing publications, direct observation of which brands are running creator campaigns.

## The Four-Question Trend Filter

Before acting on any trend, run it through all four questions. All four must have a "yes" answer or the trend gets skipped.

### Q1: Is it authentic to TC?
Could Stacie do this trend in a way that still sounds like herself, with content she already has or could naturally create? If it requires TC to pretend to be something it is not, the answer is no.

**Example yes:** A "day in my life as an Indigenous horse business owner" format — authentic.
**Example no:** A trending corporate-humor format about office life — nothing to do with TC.

### Q2: Does it build authority?
Does participating in this trend make TC look more credible, more interesting, or more distinctive in the equine or premium event space? Or does it make TC look like it is chasing attention?

**Example yes:** A "what nobody tells you about running Clydesdale events" trend — builds expertise.
**Example no:** A viral lip sync trend — adds no authority even if it gets views.

### Q3: Does it support monetization?
Would brand partners, corporate clients, or premium inquiry leads look at this content and feel TC is a serious, bookable operation? Or would it undermine TC's positioning?

**Example yes:** the photo delivery system in action at a real event — directly monetization-adjacent.
**Example no:** Prank or chaos content — funny but actively damages premium positioning.

### Q4: Can it be done without looking cheap?
Some trends require production quality TC may not always have access to. If executing this trend would produce content that looks rushed or underfunded, skip it and wait for a trend that fits.

**Example yes:** Voiceover B-roll trends — TC has B-roll of horses and events always available.
**Example no:** Heavily edited green-screen or meme format trends — requires production time TC cannot always spare.

## Trend Categories: Always Pursue vs. Always Avoid

### Always Pursue

**Authenticity and behind-the-scenes formats**
"Day in the life," "what my actual job looks like," "things I didn't expect about running [X]" — these play to TC's genuine operation and require no fabrication.

**Animal personality content**
The internet has never gotten tired of animals with personality. Trends that celebrate horse quirks, pony chaos, and herd dynamics are always available to TC and always perform.

**Indigenous creator and cultural identity conversations**
When larger conversations about Indigenous creators, Indigenous business ownership, or Indigenous land and culture come into the cultural moment, TC has a real, earned place in that conversation. Engage with care and authenticity — not opportunistically.

**Seasonal and regional content**
North Idaho spring, summer, fall, and winter are all distinct and beautiful. Seasonal format trends ("spring is here and that means...") are easy to adapt with TC's real surroundings.

**Educational content formats**
"Did you know" structures, myth-busting formats, and explanation trends fit TC's education content pillar and build authority with new audiences.

**Business owner transparency formats**
"What I charge for," "the real cost of running a horse event business," "what people think this job is vs. what it actually is" — high relevance, high authenticity, high creator income signal.

### Always Avoid

**Trends that require TC to perform a personality TC does not have**
If a trend requires being sarcastic, cynical, or ironic in ways that contradict TC's warm voice, skip it. The brand has a voice. Trends work when they fit the voice, not the other way around.

**Controversy-adjacent trends**
Anything adjacent to culture wars, political controversy, or divisive social debates. TC's audience includes people across a wide political spectrum in North Idaho. The Indigenous identity is handled with dignity — it is not a political statement.

**Shock or cringe formats**
Anything that relies on embarrassment, gross-out humor, or "fails" that make TC look unprofessional. One bad video can undo months of brand building.

**Trends built around platform-specific audio that expires in 48 hours**
If the entire trend depends on a specific sound clip that will be replaced by next week's trending sound, it is not worth the production time unless TC can create the content in under 30 minutes.

**Content that mimics bigger creators or brands**
TC has a distinctive identity. Mimicking what Yellowstone, large western brands, or celebrity horse accounts do signals that TC is following, not leading. Reference and adapt — never copy.

## Platform-Native Format Trends

### Instagram

**Reels:** Performance currently rewards 15–30 second videos with strong visual hooks in the first second. Text overlay captions (not relying purely on audio) perform well for accounts building authority. Series content with "follow for part 2" structuring.

**Carousels:** Still performing well for educational and story-style content. Swipe-to-reveal format. First slide must be compelling enough to earn the swipe.

**Stories:** Polls, question boxes, and countdown stickers drive engagement signals. Behind-the-scenes stories keep warm audiences warm between feed posts.

**Collabs:** Instagram Collab posts (co-authored with another account) are underused in TC's space and drive reach to both audiences. Pursue 1–2 per month with aligned accounts.

### TikTok

**POV formats:** "POV: You just booked a Clydesdale wagon ride for your company retreat" — high shareability, easy to produce.

**Text-on-screen with B-roll:** Hook text in first frame, story unspools with text, B-roll carries it visually. This format has staying power because it requires no talking-head production.

**Stitches and Duets:** Engaging with other equine or western creators via Stitch drives reach to their audiences. Use sparingly and only when TC has a genuine addition to make.

**Series content:** Three-part or five-part series ("How TC runs a corporate event: Part 1") drives follows and return visits.

## How to Translate External Trends into TC Content Without Losing Identity

This is the skill. It is not about copying trends — it is about finding where the trend overlaps with something TC already has.

### The Translation Method
1. Identify the trend's underlying appeal (humor, surprise, relatability, aspiration, education)
2. Find a TC equivalent that delivers the same appeal from TC's real world
3. Adapt the format (hook structure, pacing, visual approach) without copying the content
4. Write in TC's voice, not the trend creator's voice

### Example
**Trend:** "Things I wish I'd known before starting this business" in talking-head format with text.
**TC translation:** "Things nobody tells you about running a horse experience business in North Idaho" — same structure, entirely TC's content, spoken in Stacie's actual voice about her real experience.

**Trend:** Aesthetic B-roll with lo-fi audio and text overlays showing "the life."
**TC translation:** B-roll of Duke being harnessed at 6am with text overlay: "This part doesn't make it on the booking page." Same format, TC's real morning.

## Weekly Trend Check Checklist (5 Minutes Maximum)

Run this every Monday morning.

- [ ] Open TikTok FYP — scroll for 5 minutes with fresh eyes. What format is showing up more than once in TC's topic area?
- [ ] Check TikTok Creative Center — any new trending sounds or formats in lifestyle/animal categories?
- [ ] Check Instagram Reels tab — what format do the top Reels in TC's space use this week?
- [ ] Glance at one or two competitive accounts (equine experience businesses, western lifestyle creators) — what are they posting?
- [ ] Note anything that passes the four-question filter in the content queue

Total time: 5 minutes. Do not spiral into a 45-minute research session. The weekly check is a signal scan, not a deep dive.

## Red Flags — Trends That Hurt Premium Positioning

These are warning signs. If a trend requires any of these, do not participate.

- Posting content that looks rushed or low-quality just to hit a trend window
- Content where TC is the butt of the joke in a way that reads as incompetent
- Trends that make TC look like a party rental company rather than a premium experience operator
- Any trend requiring TC to mock its own clientele or services
- Trends built around negativity or complaint energy — even "funny" complaint content can read as unprofessional
- Copying another creator's format so closely it looks derivative
- Sound trends with explicit, offensive, or politically charged audio

## Trend-to-Content Pipeline

When a trend passes the four-question filter, this is how it moves from idea to post.

### Step 1: Log it (5 minutes)
Write one line in the content queue: "Trend: [description]. Format: [video/carousel/text]. My TC version: [one sentence]. Status: draft."

### Step 2: Draft it (15–20 minutes)
Write the caption or script. Pull existing B-roll if video. Identify the hook. Do not over-produce. The trend window is short — good enough and timely beats perfect and late.

### Step 3: Post it on TikTok first (10 minutes)
TikTok Business first — it has the fastest trend cycle. Instagram can follow within 24 hours.

### Step 4: Evaluate it (2 minutes — one week later)
Did it perform above TC's average? Did it attract relevant comments, DMs, or followers? Note in the weekly Friday review.

**Total time from trend spotted to live post: under 24 hours. If it takes longer, the window has likely passed.**
