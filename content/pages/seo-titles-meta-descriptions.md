## How to Update in Squarespace

For each page: **Pages panel → Click the page → Settings (gear icon) → SEO tab**

Two fields:
- **SEO Title** (also called "Page Title" or "Title Override") — this is what shows in the browser tab and in Google search results. Keep under 60 characters.
- **SEO Description** (also called "Meta Description") — the snippet under the title in Google results. Keep between 140–160 characters.

## COMMON ISSUES TO FIX

1. **"— Tribal Cowboy" appears twice** in at least 5 page titles. Squarespace appends the site title automatically. If you've also typed "— Tribal Cowboy" in the SEO title field, you get the double. Fix: Only type the page-specific part in the SEO Title Override field, and let Squarespace append the site title — OR go to **Settings → SEO → Site Title Format** and set it to not append, then include "Tribal Cowboy" manually in each title.

2. **Zero meta descriptions** across the entire site. Google is pulling random page text as your snippet, which usually looks bad. Adding these gives you control over what people see in search results.

3. **Keyword strategy:** Every title and description includes "North Idaho" because that's where your customers search from. Secondary terms like "Clydesdale," "same-day photo delivery," "Indigenous-owned," and specific service names help you rank for long-tail searches.
