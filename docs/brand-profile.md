# Tribal Cowboy — Brand Profile

*Source of truth for all positioning decisions. Reference this before writing anything public-facing.*

---

## Business Identity

Tribal Cowboy LLC is an Indigenous-owned equine experience business operating out of Athol, Idaho, owned and run by Stacie, a Nisenan Maidu woman. The business brings horses — a Clydesdale herd of four (Belle, Jill, Abby, and Millie — including two mother-daughter pairs), a mini herd of six (Monster Unicorn, Master Chief, Reba, Dolly, Lainey, and Minimus), and Stacie's personal horses (Jolene, and Rowdy and Nash currently in training) — into events, schools, and private experiences across North Idaho and Eastern Washington. The Clydesdale herd includes Belle, a 22-year-old senior matriarch, and her daughter Millie (5); and Jill (13) and her daughter Abby (7), both bred by the same Canadian breeder. These two mother-daughter pairs in the same active herd are a distinction no other North Idaho equine business shares. Services include candid-style horse photography with instant facial recognition delivery via instant photo delivery, Clydesdale wagon rides, pony parties, school and community programs rooted in Indigenous culture and horsemanship, and corporate team events. Tribal Cowboy is owner-operated. Stacie is the face, the driver, the photographer, and the brand.

## Core Differentiators

**1. instant photo delivery Facial Recognition Delivery**
Guests receive their photos instantly using facial recognition — no QR codes to hunt for, no galleries to scroll through. This is a technology differentiator no other pony party or horse experience vendor in North Idaho offers.

**2. Real Clydesdales — Owned, Not Rented**
Belle, Jill, Abby, and Millie are not rentals. They are Tribal Cowboy's Clydesdales — a four-mare herd that includes two mother-daughter pairs (Belle & Millie, Jill & Abby). The mini herd (Monster Unicorn, Master Chief, Reba, Dolly, Lainey, Minimus) also belongs to Stacie. Events are not assembled from borrowed animals with unknown temperaments. This matters for safety, consistency, and experience quality. Belle has been working since 2004. Stacie knows every animal in this herd — their quirks, their bad days, and what they need before they show it.

**3. Indigenous Ownership — Nisenan Maidu Identity**
Tribal Cowboy is owned by a Nisenan Maidu woman. That is not a marketing category. It is who Stacie is. It gives the business a perspective and an identity that no competitor can replicate, and it makes the school program and cultural education components substantive rather than decorative.

**4. North Idaho — Regional Rarity**
A working Clydesdale, professional photography, and instant delivery at a family or corporate event does not exist anywhere else in this region at this level. Distance from major markets is a feature — Tribal Cowboy serves a geography that is otherwise underserved.

**5. Custom Branded Overlays**
Photography is delivered with event-specific branded overlays: event name, company logo, date. This turns guest photos into event keepsakes and marketing assets simultaneously.

**6. Creator-Led Brand Authenticity**
Stacie is the brand. The content is hers — her land, her animals, her perspective. That creator authenticity attracts an audience and a sponsor category that polished agency-run accounts cannot reach.

**7. Full-Service Corporate Experience**
Corporate events are not an afterthought. Tribal Cowboy offers a complete team experience: horses on-site, branded photography, wagon rides, and documentation — all in one booking. No vendor coordination required.

## Competitor Context

There is no direct competitor in North Idaho doing what Tribal Cowboy does at this level. The relevant comparison points are:

- **Pony party vendors (regional):** Lower price, lower quality, rented animals, no photography component, no Indigenous ownership, no Clydesdale.
- **Horse photographers (regional):** Shoot horses in fields; do not bring horses to events; no live experience component.
- **Corporate event vendors (regional):** Offer catering, entertainment, team games — nothing involving live animals at this quality level.
- **Clydesdale farms (national):** Budweiser, large heritage draft horse farms — these are the reference point for Clydesdale experiences, but none are accessible as a private event service in North Idaho.

The result: Tribal Cowboy sits in a category it largely defines for this region. The competitive threat is not other horse businesses — it is the absence of awareness that this exists at all.
