# Platform Rules — Tribal Cowboy LLC

## Overview

TC operates across five distinct surfaces: Instagram, Facebook, TikTok Business, TikTok Personal, and the Website. Each has its own rules, purpose, posting norms, and audience behavior. Content that works on one platform does not automatically work on another. Cross-posting is allowed in some cases but always requires platform-specific adaptation. This file governs how TC shows up on each surface.

---

## Instagram (@TribalCowboy)

### Purpose
Primary brand authority channel. The visual record of TC's operation, events, and animals. The first place a potential booking client checks when evaluating TC. The hub that drives traffic to the website and to DMs.

### Posting Frequency
- Feed posts: 3–5 per week
- Stories: Daily when possible, minimum 4 days per week
- Reels: 2–3 per week (Reels receive the most algorithmic reach)
- Going below 3 posts/week consistently will reduce reach and signal inactivity

### Format Priorities (in order)
1. Reels (vertical video, 15–90 seconds) — highest reach
2. Carousel posts (3–10 images) — highest saves and dwell time
3. Single image — strong for announcement-style content
4. Stories — daily touchpoint, not indexed by algorithm

### What to Never Post on Instagram
- Hard-sell content more than 1 in 5 posts
- Long-form text posts with no visual anchor
- Reposted memes or unrelated trending content
- Price lists in post captions (move to website or DM)
- Anything that looks like stock photography or generic content
- Guest faces without consent clearance
- Overly filtered or heavily edited content that doesn't look like North Idaho

### Tone for Instagram
Warm, visual, a little story. Captions tell the moment that the image or video shows. Short to medium length — most reads happen in the first two lines before the "more" break. Put the most important line first.

### CTA Rules for Instagram
- Maximum one CTA per post
- Approved CTAs: "DM us to check dates," "link in bio," "www.tribalcowboy.com," "send us a message"
- Never use: "Book now before it's too late," "limited slots available," or any pressure language
- Story CTAs can use swipe-up/link sticker when destination is relevant
- Do not end every post with a CTA — let some posts just be the content

### Hashtag Rules for Instagram
- Use 5–10 per post from the hashtag bank in CLAUDE.md
- Mix core TC tags (#TribalCowboy, #NorthIdaho) with service-specific and reach tags
- Never use more than 10 — it reads as spam to Instagram's algorithm
- Rotate reach tags to avoid looking like a bot
- Put hashtags at the end of caption or in first comment

## Facebook (Tribal Cowboy LLC Page)

### Purpose
Community and events channel. The place where local North Idaho and Eastern Washington audiences discover TC through shares, event listings, and community groups. Slightly older demographic than Instagram. Good for longer-form event announcements, behind-the-scenes, and community engagement.

### Posting Frequency
- 3–5 posts per week
- Event listings: Every time TC has a public event or booking availability announcement
- Stories: Optional, repurpose Instagram stories when relevant

### Format Priorities (in order)
1. Video (Reels/short video) — Facebook's algorithm favors video
2. Link posts with preview (when driving website traffic)
3. Photo posts
4. Text-only posts (for community moments, occasional longer reflection)
5. Event listings (for any public booking period or event)

### What to Never Post on Facebook
- Hard-sell content more than 1 in 5 posts
- Content that requires Instagram-native features to make sense
- Hashtag-heavy posts (Facebook users don't engage with hashtag culture the same way)
- Overly casual or "Gen Z" tone — the Facebook audience for TC skews family/community-oriented
- Duplicate Instagram posts without adaptation
- Unrelated viral content or memes

### Tone for Facebook
Slightly longer, community-forward. Good for event details, booking windows, behind-the-scenes stories. Can use full paragraphs where appropriate. Speaks to the local community — neighbors, parents, business owners.

### CTA Rules for Facebook
- Event posts: Clear link to website for booking or inquiry
- Announcement posts: "Message us" or "email info@tribalcowboy.com"
- Never use countdown urgency language
- Facebook allows longer CTA context than Instagram — use it when needed

### Hashtag Rules for Facebook
- Use sparingly: 2–3 maximum per post
- Core tags only: #NorthIdahoEvents, #TribalCowboy, #IdahoHorses
- Facebook users generally do not click or follow hashtags the way Instagram users do

- Mini horse "for sale" content underperforms (~266 views) — wrong intent signal for this account

### Purpose
Premium brand authority, corporate credibility, and discovery channel for event buyers and high-value audiences. Every Business TikTok video should be something a corporate event planner, HR manager, or premium client could see and take TC seriously.

### Posting Frequency
- 3–5 posts per week
- Consistency matters more than volume — a reliable 3/week outperforms irregular 7/week
- Series episodes should post on a consistent schedule (same day each week)

### Format Priorities
1. Short-form vertical video with text overlay (7–30 seconds for hooks, 45–90 seconds for depth)
2. Series content — recurring formats that bring audiences back
3. Event recaps with educational angle
4. photo delivery demos and "how it works" explainers

### What to Never Post on Business TikTok
- Stacie's personal opinions on politics, culture, or social issues
- Raw unedited footage without a clear narrative
- Content that would confuse a corporate buyer about TC's professionalism
- Content mixing Business and Personal TikTok energy
- Sound-only trends with no visual substance
- Content featuring guest faces without consent
- Booking pressure or urgency content

### Tone for Business TikTok
Polished but real. Confident. Capable. Authoritative about equine experiences and events. TC speaks as the expert on this specific thing — Clydesdales at corporate events, instant photo delivery, North Idaho equine experiences. The hook carries the first 3 seconds. The video earns watch time from there.

### CTA Rules for Business TikTok
- One CTA per video maximum
- Approved: "Link in bio," "www.tribalcowboy.com," "DM for event inquiries"
- CTAs belong at the end — never interrupt the video content with a booking pitch
- Do not add a CTA to every video — build the audience first

### Hashtag Rules for Business TikTok
- 3–5 hashtags maximum
- From TikTok hashtag bank in CLAUDE.md: #HorseTok, #NorthIdaho, #TribalCowboy, #ClydesdaleTok, #PonyParty
- One niche-specific hashtag, one location hashtag, one broader reach hashtag per video
- Keyword-rich video description matters more for TikTok SEO than hashtags alone

## Website (www.tribalcowboy.com)

### Purpose
Authority hub. Conversion center. Trust engine. Every social channel should be pointing people here for booking, inquiry, deeper information, and proof. The site must be able to sell TC to someone who has never seen TC's social media — and to convert social followers who are ready to book.

### Update Frequency
- Service pages: Review quarterly, update when services or pricing change
- Blog or news: Publish when there's something genuinely worth saying — not on a forced schedule
- Landing pages: Create before any campaign launches, not after

### Format Priorities
1. Service pages with complete information and clear CTAs
2. About page with Stacie's story
3. Gallery organized by service type
4. Corporate events page (dedicated, separately optimized)
5. Contact/Booking page (friction-free)
6. Media/Press/Creator Partnerships page

### What to Never Put on the Website
- Urgency or countdown timers
- Pop-up forms that interrupt the browsing experience
- Generic stock photography (real TC photos only)
- Pricing without context or explanation
- Corporate-speak copy that doesn't sound like TC
- Broken links or dead-end pages with no next step

### Tone for Website
Professional and warm. More structured than social — visitors are here to make a decision, not to be entertained. Every page should answer: "What is this service, who is it for, what do I get, how do I ask about it?"

### CTA Rules for Website
- Every page: One primary CTA
- Service pages: "Inquire about [Service Name]" → contact form
- Home page: Primary CTA to most-booked service or current season priority
- Never: "Contact us for more information" alone — always specify what kind of inquiry

### Cross-Posting Rules

**Instagram → TikTok Business:** Reels can cross-post if exported without watermark. Re-evaluate caption for TikTok SEO and hook.
**TikTok Business → Instagram Reels:** Strong TikTok performers can post to IG Reels. Remove watermark, check aspect ratio, adapt caption for Instagram tone.
**Instagram → Facebook:** Most Instagram posts can adapt for Facebook. Remove excess hashtags. Expand caption where more context helps. Use Facebook-native event listings instead of post CTAs for booking pushes.
**TikTok Business → Facebook:** Short educational TikToks can be posted natively to Facebook. Adapt description to Facebook tone.
**TikTok Personal ↔ Business:** Never directly cross-post. Moments can be re-captured with different framing for the appropriate account.
**Social → Website:** Strong social content (testimonials, event recaps, viral moments) should be integrated into relevant website pages as social proof. Screenshot + embed or write-up.
**Website → Social:** Service pages are the destination, not the content. Never post a website page URL as the main content of a social post without context.
