# Facebook Post Templates — Tribal Cowboy

Facebook audiences are local, community-minded, and a bit older than Instagram/TikTok. Posts here can be a little longer and more detailed. Great for events, announcements, and community connection.

---

## Template 1: Event Announcement

```

[What it includes — 2-4 concrete details]

[Who it's for]

[Simple booking CTA with link]

[1-2 hashtags max]
```

**Example:**
```
Pony parties for summer 2026 are now available.

Here's what's included: our pony herd on-site, a licensed horse handler, on-the-spot photos with custom Tribal Cowboy branded overlays, and instant photo delivery so guests find their pictures the same day — no chasing down a photographer.

We serve North Idaho and Eastern Washington. Great for birthdays, end-of-year school parties, and family reunions.

Check availability at www.tribalcowboy.com or email info@tribalcowboy.com.

#TribalCowboy #NorthIdahoEvents
```

## Facebook Best Practices for Tribal Cowboy

- **Local groups matter** — share event posts to North Idaho community groups when relevant
- **Photos always outperform text** — even one good horse photo changes everything
- **Tag your location** — Athol, Idaho on event posts helps local reach
- **Keep hashtags to 1-3** — Facebook isn't Instagram, more hashtags don't help
- **Respond to comments promptly** — Facebook algorithm rewards conversation
- **Events feature** — create Facebook Events for public bookings and community programs
- **Long-form is okay** — a 3-paragraph post reads fine on Facebook, would be too long on Instagram
