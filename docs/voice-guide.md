# Tribal Cowboy — Brand Voice Guide

*Every word written for Tribal Cowboy goes through this filter. No exceptions.*

---

## Voice Overview

Tribal Cowboy sounds like a person who knows horses, knows this land, and doesn't need to sell you on either. The voice is warm but not effusive, specific but not technical, dry without being distant. Stacie is the voice. Write like she's talking — not like an agency drafted something on her behalf.

The single test for any piece of writing: Would a real person in North Idaho read this and feel like they know her? If the answer is yes, keep it. If it sounds like a marketing department, cut it.

---

## Tone by Platform

### Instagram
Warm. Visual. A small story in a caption. The image carries weight, and the caption adds one more layer — the thing you don't see in the photo. Keep it under 150 words unless the story earns more. End with one question or a soft CTA, never both.

### Facebook
Slightly longer. Community-forward. Facebook is where you explain something — an event, a program, a behind-the-scenes moment. It's also where you talk to parents planning a party and corporate admins looking for event ideas. More context is appropriate here. Still no corporate language.

### TikTok (Business)
Hook in the first line. No throat-clearing. Speak directly to the person watching. Write like you're talking to the camera. Use line breaks frequently — walls of text don't work on TikTok. End with something to do: follow, comment, visit.

### TikTok (Personal / Creator)
More raw. Less produced. This is where Stacie's perspective as a Nisenan Maidu woman, a horse owner, and a business builder comes through directly. First person. Present tense. Opinions allowed.

---

## Tone by Audience Type

### Families and Parents
Warm, specific, reassuring. They want to know the horses are safe and that their kid will love it. Show the moment — don't describe the "experience." Use one specific detail about an animal. Don't over-explain the logistics in the caption.

### Corporate Event Planners
Confident, efficient, credentialed. They are booking on behalf of someone else and need to justify the choice. Be specific about what's included, how it works, and who else has done it. Skip the "memorable experience" language — they've read that a thousand times.

### Executive Assistants
Clear and easy to act on. They need one clear next step. Give them the CTA, the contact, and a reason to reach out. Don't make them dig.

### Sponsors and Brand Partners
Show, don't pitch. The content itself is the pitch. Brand authority posts should make it obvious that Tribal Cowboy is a credible, visually consistent, engaged-audience brand. Save the formal pitch language for outreach emails.

### Tourism and Venue Partners
Regional identity matters here. North Idaho. Athol. The land. The Clydesdale. Lean into what makes this place and this experience specific. They're looking for something that enhances a destination, not something generic.

### General Followers
Talk to them like neighbors. They may never book — that's fine. They share content, they tag friends, they build the audience that attracts sponsors. Give them something worth their attention.

## Sentence Structure Rules

1. **Short sentences.** If a sentence has more than two clauses, split it.
2. **Active voice.** "Stacie hauled the wagon at 6am" not "The wagon was hauled by Stacie at 6am."
3. **Lead with the specific.** "Millie ate half a carrot and sneezed on the birthday girl" not "Our horses always create memorable moments."
4. **No throat-clearing.** Start with the thing, not a preamble.
5. **One idea per sentence.** If you're explaining and selling in the same sentence, split it.
6. **Line breaks matter on social.** Every 1–2 sentences, break. Do not post a wall of text.
7. **Commas are not periods.** If a list runs more than three items in a caption, reconsider the structure.
8. **Numbers over words.** "1,800 lbs" not "eighteen hundred pounds." "6am" not "six in the morning."
9. **Avoid -ing openings.** "Starting with..." "Creating..." "Using..." — find a stronger entry.
10. **End with intention.** The last line of a caption is the most-remembered. Make it count.

## Caption Formulas by Post Type

### Animal Personality Post
```
[Specific animal name] + [one specific thing they did or do]
[One observation that tells you something about the animal]
[Optional: one human parallel or dry comparison]
[Hashtags]
```

### Event Recap Post
```
[Location + date, plain]
[One specific moment — the most human thing that happened]
[What guests left with, concretely]
[Soft CTA — check dates, DM, link in bio]
[Hashtags]
```

### Work / Behind the Scenes Post
```
[Time of day + what's happening]
[Why this step matters — briefly]
[What it costs / what it makes possible]
[No CTA needed — let the effort speak]
[Hashtags]
```

### Booking / Availability Post
```
[Plain statement of availability]
[What's included, two to three specifics]
[One CTA — one URL or one email address]
[Hashtags — fewer than usual, max 5]
```

### Indigenous Identity / Culture Post
```
[Specific thing Stacie is observing, doing, or thinking]
[Context — what this means without over-explaining]
[No call to action. Let it stand.]
[Hashtags — include #NiseanMaidu #IndigenousOwned]
```

## Strong vs. Weak Caption Examples

### Post Type: Animal Personality (Millie or Abby (your Clydesdales))

**Weak:**
> Millie is our beautiful Clydesdale who loves meeting new people. He's great with kids and makes every event special. Book him for your next party and create memories your family will treasure forever.

**Strong:**
> Millie weighs 1,800 lbs and moves like he knows it.
> He's also extremely opinionated about carrots and will absolutely turn his head away from the baby ones.
> He showed up to a corporate event last fall and had three grown adults reconsidering their life choices.
> Link in bio to check spring availability.

---

### Post Type: Event Recap (Pony Party)

**Weak:**
> What an amazing day! We had so much fun at this pony party bringing smiles and joy to the children. Tribal Cowboy loves creating magical experiences for families across North Idaho. DM us to book your unique experience today!

**Strong:**
> The birthday girl walked up to Biscuit and stopped three feet away.
> Just stood there.
> Then: "Is she real?"
> She is very real. And she will absolutely steal your snacks if you're not watching.
> Pony parties start at $XXX. Check open dates at www.tribalcowboy.com.

---

### Post Type: Corporate Event

**Weak:**
> Tribal Cowboy is excited to offer unique corporate team building experiences in North Idaho! Our horses seamlessly integrate into your event to create a truly unforgettable day that leverages the power of nature and connection. Contact us to learn more!

**Strong:**
> Brought Millie out to a team event in Spokane last month.
> Forty people who spend all day in a conference room, suddenly standing in a field next to a Clydesdale.
> Nobody knew where to look.
> That awkward moment when your employees actually talk to each other — that's the whole point.
> Corporate events start at $XXX for two hours. Email info@tribalcowboy.com to check dates.

## CTA Language Rules

### Allowed
- "Check dates at www.tribalcowboy.com"
- "DM us to check availability"
- "Email info@tribalcowboy.com"
- "Link in bio"
- "We have [X] weekends open in [month]"
- "Reach out if you're planning something"

### Not Allowed
- "Book now before it's too late"
- "Limited spots — act fast"
- "Don't miss out"
- "Reserve your spot today"
- "Click the link to begin your journey"
- "Contact us to learn more about our offerings"
- "We'd love to help make your event special"
