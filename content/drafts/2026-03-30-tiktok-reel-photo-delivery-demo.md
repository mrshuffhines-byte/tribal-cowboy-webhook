## Filming Details

### What You Need
- Your phone (to film AND to demo the QR scan — see trick below)
- A second phone OR a tripod with your main phone recording while you demo on a second device
- Your photo delivery QR code (print one or pull it up on a laptop screen)
- A few sample photos already in the system with a branded overlay visible

### Camera Setup
- **Tripod or propped phone** about 3–4 feet away, vertical (9:16)
- Shoot at the barn or near the paddock — horses in the background sell the vibe without saying a word
- Natural light, face the sun or open shade. Avoid direct overhead midday if you can

### The Two-Phone Trick
This is the easiest way to film the screen demo:
1. **Phone A** (on tripod): records the whole thing
2. **Phone B** (in your hand): this is the one you scan the QR code with and show to the camera
3. When you get to the demo section, hold Phone B up toward Phone A so the screen is visible
4. Go slow. Viewers need 2–3 seconds on each screen to actually read it
5. Tilt Phone B slightly toward the camera to reduce glare — avoid filming straight-on if there's overhead light

### If You Only Have One Phone
- Film the talking parts first (hook + closer) on the tripod
- Then screen-record the QR scan and photo delivery on the same phone
- Edit them together — the screen recording drops in as a cutaway between your talking clips
- Most editing apps (CapCut, InShot) make this easy

### Audio
- Talk directly to camera, normal volume. No background music needed for the first version
- If you add music later, keep it low — the voice is the content here
- Use auto-captions (TikTok has them built in, or use CapCut). Most people watch on mute first

### Shot List (in filming order)
1. **Hook** — you on camera, phone in hand, barn behind you. One take, say the line
2. **QR scan close-up** — Phone B scanning the code. Hold steady 3 seconds
3. **Photos loading** — Phone B screen showing SpotMyPhotos results appearing
4. **Scroll through photos** — slow scroll, let viewers see the branded overlays
5. **Closer** — back on camera, relaxed delivery. Can do 2–3 takes and pick the best
6. **End card** — either hold for a beat after the closer (add text in editing) or film a quick sign-off

### Editing Notes
- Cut tight. No dead air between sections
- Add text overlay on the hook: "How instant photo delivery works"
- Add text overlay on the QR scan: "Scan → Find → Done"
- End card text: "INSTANT PHOTO DELIVERY" + website
- Total runtime target: 45–60 seconds. Under 60 is better for TikTok reach
