## Service 1: Horse Photography Props

### Full Description
Tribal Cowboy brings horses on-site to serve as live photography props for events. Guests interact with the horses — standing beside them, holding reins, letting kids sit up for a moment — while a candid-style photo session captures the moments. TC uses facial recognition technology so every guest finds their photos instantly via a QR scan — no email list, no waiting, no lost photos. Every image comes with a custom branded overlay featuring the event name, logo, and date. Photos are professionally delivered and feel like real candid portraits, not forced photo ops.

### What Makes It Premium/Distinctive
- TC is the only equine event operator in North Idaho using facial recognition to deliver every guest's photos instantly — this is a genuine technology differentiator
- Custom branded overlays mean every photo that leaves the event markets the host — corporate buyers immediately understand this value
- Candid-style photography with live horses produces images guests actually want and share — not stiff posed shots
- TC owns its herd — horses are not rented, are handled by Stacie's own team, are known animals with documented temperaments

### Best Audience
- Corporate events — branded photos are a measurable ROI deliverable for event planners
- Weddings and upscale private events
- Brand activations and experiential marketing events
- Large family gatherings and reunions
- Any event where the host wants guests to remember and share the event

### How to Present Without Sounding Cheap
Do not describe this as a "photo booth with horses." Frame it as a professional branded photography experience with live animals and instant delivery technology. The horses are not backdrops — they are participants. The instant photo delivery system is not a gimmick — it is professional event technology.

Say: "Every guest at your event gets a branded, professionally composed photo with the horses, delivered instantly to their phone. No photographer follow-up, no lost images."
Not: "Come take pictures with our horses!"

### Inquiry Path

DM: Instagram or Facebook → qualify event type, date, headcount → proposal
Email: info@tribalcowboy.com
Corporate: Via corporate events inquiry (separate dedicated page)

### Seasonal Notes
- **Spring:** Mother's Day, graduation parties, end-of-school events — families booking for May/June
- **Summer:** Peak season — weddings, corporate events, family reunions, birthday parties
- **Fall:** Harvest festivals, company fall events — October is strong
- **Winter:** Holiday corporate parties — late November through December

### Corporate Framing
"TC's horse photography experience includes branded overlays with your company name and logo on every photo, instant delivery via facial recognition, and full on-site management by TC's team. Your guests leave with a photo that markets your company every time they share it."

### Creator/Content Angle
photo delivery demo videos perform exceptionally well — the moment of a guest scanning and seeing their photo appear is a genuine wow moment. This content drives both brand deal interest (tech/photography brands) and corporate inquiry. Prioritize capturing this moment at every event.

## Service 2: Clydesdale Wagon Rides

### Full Description
Tribal Cowboy's working Clydesdales pull authentic heritage wagons for rides at events, festivals, and private experiences. This is not a carnival ride — it is a genuine draft horse experience with animals that have real scale (1,800+ pounds), real personality, and real history as working horses. Rides are managed by Stacie and her team. Can be integrated into larger events or booked as a standalone experience.

### What Makes It Premium/Distinctive
- TC owns its Clydesdale herd — Duke and the team are known, temperament-documented, trained for public interaction
- The scale of a real Clydesdale pulling a wagon is unlike anything most guests have encountered — it creates a moment that stands out in memory
- Authentic heritage experience — not a pony ride on a track, not a mechanical attraction
- Stacie's knowledge and presence as the driver and narrator adds genuine educational and cultural depth

### Best Audience
- Heritage and history events
- Harvest festivals and fall fairs
- Wedding ceremonies and receptions (arrivals, processionals)
- Corporate events where the goal is "nothing else like this"
- North Idaho tourism and outdoor experience offerings
- School and community programs

### How to Present Without Sounding Cheap
Do not describe this as a "ride." Frame it as a heritage horse experience. The Clydesdales are not props — they are the point. The wagon is not the main event — the relationship between these ancient draft horses and the guests who have never been that close to something that large is the main event.

Say: "Duke is 1,800 pounds and the gentlest horse we own. A Clydesdale pulling a wagon through an event stops everything."
Not: "Wagon rides available for your event!"

### Inquiry Path
Website: www.tribalcowboy.com → Clydesdale Wagon Rides page → Contact

Wedding inquiries: Specific consultation on timing, route, and ceremony integration

### Seasonal Notes
- **Spring:** Light bookings — early season, smaller events
- **Summer:** High demand — festivals, outdoor events, company retreats
- **Fall:** Harvest festivals, apple festivals, fall company events — the visual of Clydesdales in fall foliage is strong content
- **Winter:** Holiday wagon rides — December nostalgia events, tree lighting ceremonies, Christmas markets

### Corporate Framing
"The wagon ride experience is the moment your event is remembered for. Most corporate event attendees have never stood next to a 1,800-pound horse, let alone ridden behind one. TC handles logistics, safety, and animal management entirely."

### Creator/Content Angle
Clydesdale content has among the highest organic reach in the equine/horse content category on TikTok and Instagram. The scale is visually arresting. Build content series around Duke specifically — his personality, his history, his daily life. Every wagon ride event should be captured for content.
