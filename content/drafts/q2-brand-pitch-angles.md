## Category 1: Equine Feed and Supplement Brands

### Why This Is a Strong Fit Right Now

Spring is when horse owners are actively making feed and supplement decisions — transitioning from winter hay to fresh pasture, assessing condition after a hard season, and adjusting programs for the work ahead. TC's herd is visible, active, and posting regularly. An April partnership puts TC in front of brands right when their marketing budgets are focused on the spring horse care calendar.

### Target Brands

- **Purina Animal Nutrition** — Market leader, runs consistent creator programs, has worked with ranch and farm accounts at the regional level
- **Nutrena** (Cargill) — Active on Instagram, known for partnering with working horse operations rather than purely pleasure-horse accounts
- **Standlee Premium Western Forage** — Idaho-headquartered, regional alignment, forage-focused which fits TC's Clydesdale draft horse angle

### Pitch Angle

TC is a working Indigenous-owned horse operation in Athol, Idaho — not a hobbyist account. The herd includes working Clydesdales and ponies that are fed, exercised, and maintained for public-facing events, which means the animals are visible, documented, and photographed regularly. A Clydesdale's feed program reads differently than a backyard pleasure horse's. Draft horse nutrition is an underserved content category that Purina, Nutrena, and Standlee have not fully addressed with creator content. TC can close that gap with authentic behind-the-barn content that shows real feeding, real condition, and real results — not a posed product shot.

### Subject Line

`Draft horse nutrition content — working Clydesdale herd, North Idaho`

### What TC Offers

- 2–4 sponsored feed/supplement posts per month (Instagram + TikTok)
- Behind-the-scenes UGC: feeding routines, hay delivery, supplement mixing, body condition check-ins
- Event integration: brand name visible at TC corporate and private events
- Story and Reel format — real daily labor, not glamour shots
- Seasonal angle: spring conditioning series (April through June)

### What to Ask For

- Floor: product supply (3–6 months of herd feed/supplements) + $500–$800 per sponsored post
- Preferred structure: monthly flat rate ($1,500–$2,500/month) covering 2 posts + ongoing product supply
- Minimum: product-only deal acceptable for first partnership if the brand is a strong fit and leads to a paid relationship

### One Thing to Never Say to This Brand Category

Never say "my horses love this product" — feed brands have had enough of unverifiable influencer claims. Lead with observable outcomes: condition scores, energy levels, hoof quality, coat change over time. Show, don't tell.

## Category 2: Western Lifestyle Apparel Brands

### Why This Is a Strong Fit Right Now

Spring/summer collections are already shot and publishing — but social content supporting those collections runs through April, May, and June. Ariat's spring boot and workwear campaign, Wrangler's ranch lifestyle content, and Cinch's western event content are all active right now. TC's content calendar is full of on-horse, at-event, and early-morning ranch content that drops naturally into the aesthetic these brands need — without looking like an ad.

### Target Brands

- **Ariat** — The highest-profile western boot and workwear brand. Known for supporting working horse people, not just show ring competitors. Active creator program.
- **Wrangler** — The most recognizable name in western apparel. Has been deliberately expanding into Indigenous and western lifestyle storytelling. TC's identity is a genuine fit.
- **Cinch** — More rodeo-adjacent but expanding into ranch lifestyle. Smaller creator program than Ariat = easier to get into.

### Pitch Angle

Stacie is not a show horse trainer or a barrel racer. She is an Indigenous woman running a working equine operation that does real events with real animals in a real landscape. She wears the clothes at 6am when she's hauling, at noon when she's managing a 30-person corporate event, and at sunset when the horses are put away. That is a different story than a posed photo at a horse show — and it is the story western apparel brands cannot get enough of right now, especially with Wrangler's push into Indigenous and ranch creator content.

### Subject Line

`Western ranch content — Indigenous-owned working horse operation, North Idaho`

### What TC Offers

- Seasonal apparel content integrated naturally into daily ranch and event work
- Event-day apparel visibility: TC is on-site at corporate and private events with high photo and video production
- TikTok content: "day in the life" format — boots, work clothes, real dirt
- Reel content: morning routine, event setup, end-of-day barn work
- Possible: Wrangler and Ariat both have programs for Indigenous creators — pitch that specifically to Wrangler

### What to Ask For

- Floor: gifted apparel (2–3 items per season) + $600–$1,000 per sponsored post
- Preferred structure: seasonal partnership ($2,000–$4,000/season) covering 4–6 posts across Instagram and TikTok
- Ariat specifically: pitch for inclusion in their Pro Team or ambassador program — that route includes product and event support, not just per-post fees

### One Thing to Never Say to This Brand Category

Never pitch "lifestyle content." These brands receive hundreds of pitches that say exactly that. Pitch the specificity: a working Clydesdale, an Indigenous-owned operation, North Idaho. The story is the pitch.

## Category 3: Photography and Imaging Technology Brands

### Why This Is a Strong Fit Right Now

Event photography technology is growing fast — every brand in this space is looking for authentic use cases that show the product doing real work in real conditions. TC is already running instant photo delivery at corporate events and generating content around the delivery experience. A brand deal with a photography or imaging brand (Moment lenses, Joby stabilizers, or a deeper instant photo delivery integration) makes the technology story more visible and turns TC's events into a proof-of-concept the brand can use.

### Target Brands

- **instant photo delivery** — TC already uses this platform. A deeper partnership — co-marketing, case study content, paid ambassador deal — is the most natural next step and has the highest likelihood of conversion.
- **Moment** — Lens and mobile photography accessory brand. Skews toward creators who shoot genuine content in real environments. TC's event photography angle fits their "pro mobile" storytelling.
- **Joby** — Stabilizer and mount brand used heavily by creators who shoot at events. TC's event content — from wagons, from horse level, from crowd level — is exactly their use case.

### Pitch Angle

instant photo delivery pitch (deepened partnership): TC is currently one of the only equine event operations in the country using instant photo delivery for branded corporate photo delivery. Every event generates a real-world case study — group size, scan rate, branded delivery stats. TC is building an audience that includes event planners, HR directors, and corporate buyers who have never heard of instant photo delivery. A co-marketing relationship turns TC's corporate content into a instant photo delivery lead generation channel.

Moment/Joby pitch: TC's events are photographically complex — fast-moving horses, outdoor lighting, candid guest interaction, early morning setup — and TC shoots them with mobile gear. The documentation of how TC captures event content, behind-the-scenes production, and horse photography technique is a real content category that Moment and Joby's audiences follow.

### Subject Line

instant photo delivery: `Co-marketing proposal — TC corporate events, branded delivery content`
Moment/Joby: `Mobile photography at working horse events — creator partnership inquiry`

### What TC Offers

**Instant photo delivery:**
- Recurring case study content: post-event stats (photos delivered, scan rate), video showing the scan-to-delivery moment
- Corporate buyer audience: TC's Instagram and LinkedIn reach is increasingly corporate-facing — this technology content targets the right audience
- Speaking/feature opportunity: TC can be a case study for instant photo delivery marketing materials

**Moment/Joby:**
- UGC: "how TC shoots events with mobile gear" — tutorials, behind-the-scenes, gear-in-use shots
- Sponsored posts: gear featured naturally in event setup and production content
- Reel/TikTok: fast-paced event day content showing the gear in real conditions

### What to Ask For

- instant photo delivery: paid ambassador deal or revenue share — flat monthly rate ($500–$1,000/month) for consistent co-marketing content. Negotiate: free platform access, feature placement on instant photo delivery marketing materials.
- Moment: gifted gear + $400–$700 per sponsored post. Preferred: quarterly partnership.
- Joby: gifted stabilizer/mount + $400–$600 per sponsored post.

### One Thing to Never Say to This Brand Category

Never pitch "content creation" as a generic service. These brands want proof. Lead with TC's existing instant photo delivery usage stats or specific event photography examples. The pitch is "here is what we already do" — not "here is what we could do."

## Category 4: North Idaho / Pacific Northwest Outdoor and Travel Brands

### Why This Is a Strong Fit Right Now

Visit Idaho and Visit North Idaho are actively running spring campaign content through April and May — the regional tourism push is in full swing before summer travel decisions are made. Washington Tourism Alliance is similarly active. TC's operation is a rare, authentic North Idaho experience that is not a ski resort or a lake boat — it fills a storytelling gap these tourism boards actively need filled.

### Target Brands

- **Visit North Idaho** — Regional tourism organization for the Idaho Panhandle. TC is a natural feature: Indigenous-owned, horse-based, photo-friendly, working ranch in Athol.
- **Visit Idaho** — State-level tourism organization. Indigenous-owned businesses and authentic rural experiences are a priority content category for them.
- **Washington Tourism Alliance** — TC serves Eastern Washington and is a North Idaho adjacent offering. The Alliance covers rural and outdoor experiences that cross state lines.

### Pitch Angle

Tourism boards need content that reads as genuinely local — not a resort-packaged experience, not a replica of something they've seen in Montana or Wyoming. TC is an Indigenous-owned working Clydesdale operation running public events in North Idaho. It is visually strong, culturally specific, and does not exist anywhere else in the region. For Visit Idaho's Indigenous-owned business spotlight, for Visit North Idaho's outdoor and rural experience content, and for Washington Tourism Alliance's cross-border appeal, TC is the kind of feature that earns editorial coverage — not paid placement.

### Subject Line

`Tribal Cowboy — Indigenous-owned equine experience, Athol ID | Feature/partnership inquiry`

### What TC Offers

- Featured listing on Visit North Idaho and Visit Idaho event and experience directories
- Co-created content: TC hosts a Visit Idaho or Visit North Idaho photographer/videographer at an event for editorial coverage
- Social media tags and location features in TC's regular content
- Quote and interview participation for regional tourism publications
- Spring/summer event calendar coordination — TC's public events feed into the tourism board's event listings

### What to Ask For

- Visit North Idaho / Visit Idaho: preferred vendor listing + co-created editorial feature (1–2 published pieces per year). Negotiate: paid content creation fee if TC is producing original content for their platforms ($500–$1,500 per asset).
- Washington Tourism Alliance: cross-border business listing, inclusion in rural experience campaign content.
- Long-term goal: feature in Visit Idaho's annual travel guide or Indigenous tourism spotlight.

### One Thing to Never Say to This Brand Category

Never describe TC as a "tourist attraction." Tourism boards want authentic local businesses — not attractions built for tourists. The pitch is that TC exists as a working operation and happens to be open to visitors and event bookings. That distinction matters to how these organizations categorize and feature you.
