Why Facebook looks low
Instagram posts shared to Facebook are treated as cross-posts by the algorithm — Facebook actively reduces their reach because the content wasn't made natively. Your 246 followers aren't seeing the posts the way your 820 Instagram followers are. This is a known Meta issue, not a you issue.
What actually works on Facebook
Facebook
Post natively at least 2x a week
  • Write the post directly in Facebook, not pushed from Instagram
  • Facebook favors text-heavy posts with a question or local angle — "Anyone else in North Idaho looking for something to do with the kids this Saturday?"
  • Events drive massive organic reach on Facebook — create an Event for every booking that has a public component
  • Facebook Groups are your best friend — post in North Idaho family groups, CdA events groups, Spokane parenting groups
  • Go Live for 5 minutes with the horses — Facebook still boosts Live video more than any other format
  • Share your Instagram Reels to Facebook manually as a native upload, not through the auto-share toggle

What works on TikTok
TikTok
Hook in the first 2 seconds — always
  • Start with the horse on screen, not with you talking — movement stops the scroll
  • Text overlay hooks: "POV: you brought a Clydesdale to a kids birthday party" or "What 1,800 lbs of horse looks like next to a 4-year-old"
  • TikTok pushes horse content hard — #HorseTok has billions of views. You belong there.
  • Keep it under 30 seconds for maximum reach — show one moment, don't try to fit everything
  • Reply to comments with a video — TikTok boosts video replies heavily
  • Post 3-5x per week if you can. Consistency matters more than production quality.
  • Indigenous creator content performs well — lean into your story authentically

Platform Tips
Instagram
Your strongest platform — keep the momentum
  • Reels outperform static posts 3:1 — even a shaky 15-second clip of Millie counts
  • Stories every day keeps you at the top of the feed — behind-the-scenes, polls, Q&A
  • Carousels get saved more than any other format — use them for education posts
  • Caption the first line as if it's the only line people will read — hook first, story second
  • Tag your location on every post — North Idaho, Athol, Coeur d'Alene, Spokane
  • Respond to every comment within 2 hours of posting — Instagram rewards early engagement
Facebook
Don't just cross-post — post natively
  • 2x native posts per week minimum — written directly in Facebook, not pushed from Instagram
  • Create a Facebook Event for anything bookable or public-facing — Events get massive free reach
  • Post in local groups: North Idaho Family Fun, CdA Events, Spokane Kids Activities
  • Facebook Live with the horses — even 5 minutes gets boosted more than any other format
  • Ask a local question in the caption: "What's your go-to family activity in North Idaho this spring?"
  • Upload Reels directly to Facebook as a native video — don't rely on the auto-share from Instagram
TikTok
Biggest growth potential right now
  • Start every video with the horse moving — don't start with you talking
  • Text hook on screen: "POV: 1,800 lbs of Clydesdale just showed up to a birthday party"
  • #HorseTok has billions of views — you belong in that space
  • Post 3-5x per week — volume and consistency beat perfection on TikTok
  • Reply to comments with video replies — TikTok boosts these heavily
  • Keep it under 30 seconds for max reach — one moment, one reaction, done
