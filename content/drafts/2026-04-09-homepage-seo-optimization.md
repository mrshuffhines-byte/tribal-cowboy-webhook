## OPTIMIZED VERSION (Apply This)

### Primary Title (60 characters)
```
Horse Photography & Pony Parties in North Idaho
```

**Why:** Includes primary service keywords ("horse photography," "pony parties"), geography ("North Idaho"), and is within the 60-character limit that displays fully in Google search results.

### Meta Description (160 characters)
```
Tribal Cowboy offers horse photography props, pony parties, Clydesdale wagon rides, and corporate team-building events in North Idaho and Eastern Washington. Indigenous-owned & family-run.
```

**Why:**
- Includes top 4 service keywords
- Mentions geography (both service areas)
- Highlights Indigenous-owned differentiator
- 160 characters exactly (displays fully on desktop & mobile)
- Calls out "family-run" to signal trust

## How to Implement

1. Log into Squarespace
2. Go to **Settings > SEO**
3. Find the **Homepage** section
4. Paste the title into the **"Page Title"** field
5. Paste the meta description into the **"Meta Description"** field
6. Click **Save**
7. Wait 1-2 days for Google to reindex

---

## What Happens Next

- Your homepage will appear in Google search results with the new title and description
- Keywords "horse photography," "pony parties," "wagon rides," and "North Idaho" now signal relevance to Google
- Higher click-through rate from search results (better title = more clicks)
- Expected impact: +10–15% impressions within Week 1–2

## SEO Notes

- **H1 on homepage:** Make sure your hero section H1 matches or complements this title. If your hero says something like "Welcome to Tribal Cowboy," consider updating it to "Horse Photography, Pony Parties & More in North Idaho" or similar.
- **First paragraph:** Include "North Idaho" and at least one service keyword (horse photography, pony parties, wagon rides, corporate events) in the first 100 words of your homepage copy.
- **Internal linking:** Once you build the new service landing pages (Weeks 2–4), link to them from the homepage—this helps Google understand your site structure.
