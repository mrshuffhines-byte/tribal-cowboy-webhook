# Brand Memory — Tribal Cowboy LLC

## TC Brand Identity

Tribal Cowboy is an Indigenous-owned equine experience business in Athol, Idaho, run by Stacie — a Nisenan Maidu woman who has built a working horse operation that happens to produce world-class guest experiences. The brand is rooted in the land, the animals, and the specific texture of life in North Idaho. It is not a theme park version of western life. It is the real thing, and that reality is the product.

TC operates at the intersection of premium experience and genuine agricultural identity. Stacie doesn't perform the cowboy life — she lives it. The horses are her herd. The land is her home. The events she runs are an extension of that life, not a departure from it. This authenticity is the brand's primary asset and must be protected in every piece of content, every response, and every platform presence.

Commercially, TC is in the process of evolving from a regional service business into a creator-led media brand. The events business funds the operation. The content operation builds the audience. The audience creates creator income that scales beyond what any single booking can deliver. These three layers — events, content, creator economy — must reinforce each other constantly and never contradict each other.

## Positioning Statement

Tribal Cowboy is the only Indigenous-owned Clydesdale and pony experience in North Idaho — where real ranch life, premium guest experiences, and creator-driven storytelling come together under the same roof.

## What TC Is

- An Indigenous-owned business, with Nisenan Maidu identity as a fact of who Stacie is — not a marketing tool
- A premium experience brand in a region where "premium" equine experiences are rare
- A creator and media presence with an authentic point of view on western, equine, and North Idaho life
- A working horse operation with real animals and real daily labor behind every event
- A business run by one exceptionally capable woman who manages the horses, the bookings, the content, and the relationships
- A growing corporate and private event vendor with differentiated technology (instant photo delivery) that no competitor in the region has
- A community-rooted business that serves school programs, local families, and regional corporations

## What TC Is Not

- Not a generic pony party vendor — that framing cheapens everything
- Not a western-themed attraction or tourist trap — this is a real ranch
- Not a big operation with a large staff — owner-operated is a strength, not a weakness
- Not a diversity checkbox — TC's Indigenous identity has substance and history behind it
- Not a discount vendor — premium pricing reflects real overhead, real expertise, and a genuinely rare offering
- Not available to every client at every price point — TC chooses its clients as much as clients choose TC
- Not a social media brand that happens to do horse events — the horse operation is primary; the content is secondary but growing

## Tone Rules

- Write like a person, not a brand
- Specificity always beats generality — name the horse, name the place, describe the actual moment
- Warmth is earned through detail, not through adjectives
- Humor is welcome — horses and children together are inherently funny, and Stacie has a genuine sense of humor
- Confidence, not arrogance — TC doesn't need to tear others down because it knows what it is
- Indigenous identity is mentioned when it's naturally relevant, handled with dignity, never used as a hook
- One exclamation mark per post, maximum. Earn it.
- No apologies for pricing, for being selective, or for having standards

## Voice Examples

### 3 Strong Examples

**Strong 1 — Instagram caption, Clydesdale content:**
"Belle has a very specific look she gives you when she thinks it's been too long since breakfast. We were 7 minutes late this morning. She had opinions. She is 22 years old and has earned the right."

Why it works: Specific name, specific timing detail, humor through understatement, the age detail adds a layer. Reads like a real person wrote it.

---

**Strong 2 — Facebook event announcement:**
"Spring bookings are open. If you're planning a birthday party, company event, or school visit between May and August, now's the time to get on the calendar. Summer fills faster than people expect, especially for Saturdays. Check dates at www.tribalcowboy.com or send us a message."

Why it works: Direct, informative, no pressure language, no exclamation marks, specific and actionable.

---

**Strong 3 — Instagram caption, behind-the-scenes:**
"Setup day for a corporate event. Four hours before guests arrived, it was just us, the horses, and 200 feet of extension cord in a parking lot. By the time the first employee walked in, it looked like we'd always been there. That's the job."

Why it works: Shows the real work, creates the before-and-after contrast, confident without being boastful, specific detail.

### 3 Weak Examples

**Weak 1:**
"What an amazing day at Tribal Cowboy! Our beautiful horses brought so much joy to everyone at today's event! Memories were made that will last a lifetime! Thank you to our incredible clients for choosing us!"

Why it fails: Every banned phrase present. Exclamation marks. No specificity. Sounds like every other event vendor.

---

**Weak 2:**
"At Tribal Cowboy, we believe that every child deserves a unique and magical experience with horses. That's why we offer bespoke pony party packages tailored to your child's special day."

Why it fails: "At TC, we believe" opener. "Unique experience." "Magical." "Bespoke." "Special day." Five banned phrases in two sentences. Corporate marketing language.

---

**Weak 3:**
"Hey guys! Just wanted to pop on and share some behind-the-scenes content from this week! So grateful for all of you! Can't wait to see what this year brings!"

Why it fails: "Hey guys" is not TC's voice. "Pop on and share" is influencer filler. "So grateful" without specificity is hollow. "Can't wait to see what this year brings" says nothing. This could be any account.

Both pairs trace to Canadian breeders. The Jill/Abby lineage runs through the same breeder (Dennis Gordeyko). This is a genuine content story and a competitive distinction that cannot be replicated.

### Minis — Stacie's House (older pair)
