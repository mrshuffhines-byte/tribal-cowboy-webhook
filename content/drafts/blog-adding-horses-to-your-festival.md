## ARTICLE

You're planning a festival, a farmers market, or a community event and you want something that draws families in and keeps them there. Bounce houses are fine. Face painting is fine. But a Clydesdale standing in the middle of your event? That stops people in their tracks.

Here's what it actually looks like to add horses to your event, what you need to plan for, and what your guests walk away with.

### What We Bring

We show up with the herd — Clydesdales, ponies, or both, depending on what fits your event. Trained handlers manage all animal interactions and crowd flow. A professional photographer captures guests with the horses. And every single guest gets branded photos delivered to their phone before they leave your event.

That last part is worth repeating. We set up a QR code at the event. Guests scan it, find their photos using facial recognition, and get pictures with your event name, logo, and date as a custom overlay. That's marketing for your event that hits every attendee's camera roll and social feed — included in every booking.

We handle setup, teardown, and everything in between. Your team doesn't touch a thing.

### What Space Do You Need?

Flat outdoor space. That's it. We've set up in fairgrounds, park fields, parking lots, and downtown streets. The horses need enough room to move safely and enough space to manage foot traffic around them. We'll walk your site layout with you before the event and tell you exactly what works.

No covered arena required. No special fencing (we bring what we need). No stabling — the horses travel in our trailer and go home after.

### What Events Does This Work For?

We've done farmers markets, holiday festivals, harvest fairs, Fourth of July celebrations, grand openings, corporate family days, and community fundraisers. The format adapts to your event. For a farmers market, that might be a pony interaction pen at one end of the market. For a festival, it might be Clydesdale wagon rides running loops through the grounds.

If people are walking around with their families, horses work.

### How Much Does It Cost?

Custom quotes based on your event size, duration, and which services you want. Corporate and festival packages start at $800 for a 2-hour window. Larger events with wagon rides, multiple animal stations, and full-day coverage go up from there.

We'll give you a quote within one business day of your inquiry. No runaround, no "let's hop on a call to discuss pricing." You tell us what you need, we tell you what it costs.

### Why Organizers Rebook

Three reasons, every time.

First, the crowd response. Horses draw people in from across the event. Families that came for something else end up spending 30 minutes with the animals. Dwell time goes up. Foot traffic to nearby vendors goes up.

Second, the photos. Every guest leaves with branded photos on their phone. That means your event name and logo show up on social feeds that night. You didn't have to hire a social media team — the photo delivery does it for you.

Third, the professionalism. We show up early, we run our own operation, and we leave your site clean. No vendor management headaches. No "can someone go check on the horse guy." We handle it.

### What About Insurance and Permits?

Fully insured. We provide a Certificate of Insurance (COI) for your venue or municipality. If your event requires additional permits for animals, we'll help you navigate that. We've done this enough to know what most venues need before you have to ask.
