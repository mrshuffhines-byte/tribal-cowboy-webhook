# Tribal Cowboy — Audience Personas

*Six distinct audience segments with full behavioral profiles. Use these to write to a specific person, not a vague crowd.*

---

## Persona 1: The Affluent Idaho Family

**Name (representative):** Megan, 38
**Location:** Coeur d'Alene or Post Falls, Idaho
**Household Income:** $180,000–$350,000+
**Household:** Married with 2–3 kids, ages 4–12. Partner works in tech, healthcare, or owns a regional business.

### Who She Is
She's done the bounce house. She's done the princess party. She's looking for something that actually surprises her kids — and that she can post and feel good about. She shops premium without thinking twice when it's worth it. She books experiences over things. She follows accounts that feel intentional, not mass-produced.

### What She's Looking For
- Something her kids will talk about for weeks, not hours
- Photos she actually wants to share, not generic event photography
- An experience that feels real — not staged, not over-emceed
- A vendor she trusts completely, because she's done the research

### How She Finds Tribal Cowboy
- Instagram, probably from a tagged photo a friend posted
- Searching "pony party North Idaho" or "kids horse party Coeur d'Alene"
- A recommendation from another parent at school or the gym

### What Convinces Her
- Photo quality — candid moments with animals, not stiff posed shots
- Specificity — "Duke the Clydesdale" not "our horses"
- Social proof — saved posts, comments from other moms, real testimonials
- A clean, easy-to-navigate website with clear pricing and a visible booking path
- instant photo delivery — the instant delivery angle is a genuine differentiator for her

### What Loses Her
- Prices that feel hidden or unclear
- A generic, hard-to-navigate website
- Excessive exclamation marks and "making memories" language — she can smell the template
- No recent content — an inactive Instagram suggests the business may not be current
- Rented or borrowed animals — she notices the difference in how the content feels

### Ideal Content to Show Her
- Candid photos of kids with horses — the surprise moments, not the posed ones
- instant photo delivery explanation (how guests get their photos)
- Event recap posts with one specific moment
- Behind-the-scenes of event prep — she's buying into a process, not just a product

### Ideal CTA
"Check open dates at www.tribalcowboy.com" or "DM us to check May availability"

### Funnel Position
- Discovers via Instagram
- Builds trust through saved posts and profile browsing
- Converts via website inquiry or DM
- Advocates through tagged photos and parent-group recommendations

## Persona 2: The Corporate Event Planner

**Name (representative):** Jennifer, 44
**Location:** Spokane, WA or Coeur d'Alene, ID
**Title:** HR Director, Operations Manager, or People & Culture Lead at a company of 50–500 employees
**Budget Authority:** $5,000–$20,000 per event with approval from a director or VP

### Who She Is
She plans 4–6 large team events per year. She needs reliability more than novelty — but novel helps sell the idea internally. She's burned by vendors who overpromise and underdeliver. She reads reviews. She asks for references. She needs a clear contract and a straightforward process.

### What She's Looking For
- An activity her team actually wants to do — not another bowling alley or wine tasting
- A vendor who communicates clearly and confirms every logistic
- Branded photography that doubles as internal comms content and social proof
- Something that requires minimal coordination on her end — ideally one-vendor, full-service

### How She Finds Tribal Cowboy
- Google search: "unique corporate events North Idaho" or "team building Spokane outdoor"
- LinkedIn recommendation from another HR professional
- Referral from a venue or event coordinator who has worked with TC before
- Instagram (less likely primary — more likely confirmation research)

### What Convinces Her
- A dedicated corporate events page on the website
- Pricing transparency — at minimum a "starting at" number
- Clear description of what's included, timeline, and what she needs to provide
- A professional follow-up email within 24 hours of inquiry
- Social proof from corporate clients specifically (not just family parties)
- Custom branded overlays — this is a genuine differentiator for corporate use

### What Loses Her
- No corporate-specific content anywhere on the website or social
- A DM-only inquiry process — she needs email
- Vague pricing and no clear deliverables
- Slow response time (48+ hours)
- Content that looks like a children's party vendor rather than a corporate experience

### Ideal Content to Show Her
- Corporate event recap posts with specific outcomes (what the team did, what they left with)
- Custom branded overlay examples
- Clear "what's included" language
- Stacie's professionalism visible in the content — not just animal charm

### Ideal CTA
"Corporate events start at $XXX for two hours. Email info@tribalcowboy.com to check dates and discuss your team."

### Funnel Position
- Discovers via Google or referral
- Evaluates via website and then Instagram for social proof
- Converts via email inquiry
- Advocates by referring other HR contacts

## Persona 3: The Executive Assistant

**Name (representative):** Kim, 32
**Location:** Spokane or Boise area
**Role:** EA to a VP, C-suite, or senior director at a mid-to-large company
**Task:** Book a team event, offsite, or client appreciation event on behalf of an executive

### Who She Is
She is not the decision-maker, but she is the gatekeeper. If she can't quickly understand what Tribal Cowboy is, how it works, and how much it costs, she moves on. She needs to present this to her executive with confidence. She is looking for simplicity, speed, and social proof — in that order.

### What She's Looking For
- A clear, simple description of the experience
- A visible price range or "starting at" number
- An easy inquiry mechanism — email preferred, clear contact info
- Confirmation that this vendor is legitimate: website, Instagram presence, testimonials
- Something she can forward to her exec without embarrassment

### How She Finds Tribal Cowboy
- Google search: "corporate horse event Idaho" or "team event vendors Spokane"
- Venue or event planner referral
- Passed along by a colleague who saw TC on Instagram

### What Convinces Her
- A clean, professional website that loads fast and has a clear corporate section
- "Starting at $XXX" language anywhere visible
- A phone number or email that is clearly the primary contact
- A fast response to her inquiry — within a business day
- Client logos or notable event names if available

### What Loses Her
- A website that requires more than two clicks to find pricing or contact info
- Instagram-only presence with no website
- No response to inquiry within 48 hours
- "DM for pricing" — she cannot DM on behalf of her exec professionally
- Anything that looks or sounds like a children's birthday vendor

### Ideal Content to Show Her
- Corporate landing page with clear deliverables and pricing
- Email outreach that is professional and specific, not templated
- A one-paragraph description she can paste into a Slack message to her exec

### Ideal CTA
"Corporate inquiries: email info@tribalcowboy.com or visit www.tribalcowboy.com/corporate"

### Funnel Position
- Entry point is usually website or direct referral
- Very short evaluation window — she decides quickly
- Converts via email
- Rarely advocates publicly, but will pass the contact info along internally

## Persona 4: The Lifestyle Brand Sponsor

**Name (representative):** Cassidy, 29
**Role:** Brand Partnerships Manager or Influencer Marketing Lead at a western lifestyle, outdoor, equine, or family brand
**Company examples:** Western apparel brands, equine care companies, family outdoor gear, regional food/beverage brands, Idaho tourism board

### Who She Is
She manages a portfolio of creator partnerships. She's looking for accounts that are visually consistent, have engaged (not just large) audiences, and have a clear identity. She is tired of generic influencer pitches. She is actively looking for creators who have built something real. She has a budget and approval authority up to a certain spend threshold.

### What She's Looking For
- A creator with a distinct visual aesthetic and point of view
- Audience engagement that suggests real attention (saves, shares, comments) over vanity metrics
- A niche that doesn't feel crowded — North Idaho + Indigenous-owned + working horses is rare
- A creator who can shoot usable content without extensive direction
- Alignment between her brand's values and the creator's identity

### How She Finds Tribal Cowboy
- Searching Instagram hashtags: #IndigenousOwned, #NorthIdaho, #ClydesdaleHorse, #HorsesTok
- Creator discovery tools (Later, AspireIQ, similar)
- Referral from another brand manager who noticed @TribalCowboy
- Organic — she follows the account and reaches out

### What Convinces Her
- Consistent content quality — not just one good post, a reliable standard
- A media kit with audience demographics, engagement rate, content examples
- Previous brand work (even informal) showing she can create on-brand content
- Responsiveness and professionalism in email — she pitches a lot and respects people who respond clearly
- Indigenous identity as a genuine differentiator — she is aware of the value of authentic diverse voices and is not looking for a token partnership

### What Loses Her
- Inconsistent posting or long gaps in content
- Low-quality photos mixed in with high-quality ones — she can't predict what she'll get
- A pitch that sounds desperate or underprepared
- Audience that appears inflated or fake (sudden follower spikes, zero engagement)
- Content that doesn't have a clear POV — generic horse photos don't move her

### Ideal Content to Show Her
- Brand Authority posts (Pillar 8): media-quality photos, clear engagement in comments
- A visible, consistent aesthetic across the grid
- An About / Bio that signals creator identity, not just service listing
- A media kit available on request (reference it in bio or link)

### Ideal CTA
Content itself is the CTA. Bio: "Partnerships: info@tribalcowboy.com" — that's all that's needed.

### Funnel Position
- Discovery is organic (hashtags, referrals, creator databases)
- Evaluation happens entirely on the profile and media kit
- First contact is usually her reaching out via DM or email
- Relationship builds over 1–2 campaigns before becoming ongoing

## Persona 5: The Tourism and Venue Partner

**Name (representative):** David, 52
**Role:** Director of Tourism at Visit North Idaho, event coordinator at a resort or venue, or experience curator at a hospitality group
**Goal:** Build a portfolio of regional experiences to offer guests, visitors, and groups

### Who He Is
He is building out a menu of experiences. He needs vendors who are reliable, professional, and distinctly regional — nothing generic. He is thinking in terms of co-marketing, package building, and referral relationships. He is not usually looking to book a single event; he wants an ongoing relationship.

### What He's Looking For
- A vendor that enhances the regional experience portfolio
- Something he can describe to visitors in one sentence: "There's an Indigenous-owned horse experience in Athol" — that's easy to pitch
- Reliability: confirmed bookings, no last-minute cancellations, professional communication
- Marketing collateral he can share: good photos, a clear website page, a one-pager
- Openness to co-marketing, cross-promotion, and referral arrangements

### How He Finds Tribal Cowboy
- Regional tourism networks and business development events
- A colleague's recommendation
- Google search for regional experience vendors
- Social media — but more likely confirms credibility there rather than discovers

### What Convinces Him
- A professional website with clear service descriptions
- Photographs that look tourism-quality — he can imagine them on a brochure
- A response to his inquiry that demonstrates Stacie is easy to work with
- A co-marketing or partnership frame, not just a vendor frame

### What Loses Him
- A website that doesn't communicate clearly enough to describe to someone else
- No corporate or group booking structure
- The sense that the business is informal or unreliable
- Content that is purely social-media-native — doesn't translate to tourism use

### Ideal Content to Show Him
- North Idaho Life posts (Pillar 7): land, seasons, regional identity
- Corporate and event recap posts showing the full experience
- Any tourism-adjacent press or coverage

### Ideal CTA
"Partnership inquiries: info@tribalcowboy.com" with a dedicated partnerships page on the website.

### Funnel Position
- Aware stage can be long — these relationships often start at a networking event or referral
- He evaluates slowly and carefully
- First booking is usually a small co-promotion or referral
- Relationship grows as TC proves reliable and referrals come back positive

## Persona 6: The Organic Follower Who Becomes a Customer

**Name (representative):** Rachel, 31
**Location:** Rathdrum, Sandpoint, or Spokane Valley
**Income:** Middle-class to upper-middle-class, dual income household
**Discovery:** Stumbled onto @TribalCowboy through a share, a hashtag, or a friend's tagged photo

### Who She Is
She found TC because something in the feed caught her eye — a funny horse moment, a stunning North Idaho photo, something a friend tagged. She is not actively shopping for an event. She follows accounts that entertain, teach, or make her feel something. She will follow for months before any purchase intent surfaces.

### What She's Looking For
- Content she wants to watch — horse personalities, real moments, the land, dry humor
- A business that feels real, not performed
- Trust built slowly — she's not going to book from a single post
- Something to save and come back to when she's ready

### How She Finds Tribal Cowboy
- A friend tags a TC post with "we should do this"
- She discovers the account through #NorthIdaho or #HorsesOfInstagram
- A TikTok that performed well gets reposted or shared

### What Convinces Her (over time)
- Consistent, high-quality content she looks forward to seeing
- Comments responded to — she sees TC is a real person who engages
- A specific animal or story she's been following over time (Duke, a pony, a seasonal story)
- A post that says "we have May open" just when she's planning her daughter's birthday

### What Loses Her
- Posting gaps of more than two weeks
- Too many promotional posts in a row
- A sudden shift in tone that breaks the trust she's built
- No response when she finally does comment or DM

### Ideal Content to Show Her
- All pillars, in rotation — she's a full-diet follower
- The Animals and The Moments perform best for this persona
- Behind-the-scenes builds the intimacy that converts her from follower to buyer

### Ideal CTA
Soft: "We have a few spring dates still open — www.tribalcowboy.com" — planted naturally in a post she's already engaged with.

### Funnel Position
- Lives at the top and middle of the funnel for a long time
- Converts when the timing is right and trust is established
- Becomes an advocate — tags friends, shares posts, leaves the kind of comment that convinces other Rachel-types
