# Tribal Cowboy — Monthly Operating System

**Last updated:** 2026-03-21
**Business:** Tribal Cowboy LLC — Stacie Huffhines, Nisenan Maidu
**Design principle:** One clear month at a time. Build it, run it, learn from it, adjust.

---

## Overview

The monthly operating system answers the question: "What does running TC's content and business development look like at the month level?"

The weekly workflow (`docs/weekly-workflow.md`) handles the tactical day-to-day. This document handles the strategic month-to-month — the bigger decisions, the longer-view reviews, and the planning that cannot happen in a 15-minute daily check.

---

## Month Start Checklist

Run this in the first 2–3 days of every month. Blocks 1–5 take approximately 90 minutes total.

### Block 1: Set the Month Theme and Priority (10 minutes)
Before building content, decide what this month is about.

**Ask:**
- What is the dominant season or seasonal moment? (See seasonal calendar section below)
- What is TC's biggest business priority this month? (Filling bookings? Building creator income? Corporate outreach? Growing TikTok?)
- What happened last month that informs this month? (Did something perform unusually well? Did a channel underperform? Did a business goal shift?)
- Is there a specific campaign, event, or milestone this month? (A big event, a booking push, a partnership launch)

**Output:** One paragraph or bullet list that defines the month's theme and top priority. Write it at the top of the month's content calendar file.

**Example:**
> "March 2026 — Spring is opening. Priority: get bookings for May–June filled while beginning UGC outreach to equine brands. Theme content: spring on the land, herd personality as the season changes, behind-the-scenes of opening the event season. One corporate proposal to complete."

### Block 2: Build the Content Calendar (30 minutes)
Open or create `/TribalCowboy-Social/content/calendar/YYYY-MM.md` for the current month.

**Content volume targets:**
- Instagram feed: 4–5 posts per week (18–22 per month)
- Instagram Stories: daily or near-daily
- Instagram Reels: 2–3 per week minimum
- TikTok Business: 4–5 per week
- TikTok Personal: 3–5 per week
- Facebook: 3–4 per week
- Blog: 1–2 posts per month

**Calendar structure:** Fill in by week, not by day. Assign each post slot a pillar, a rough topic, and a format. Do not write captions in the calendar — that lives in the drafts folder.

**Pillar rotation check:** Scan the calendar before finalizing. Are all 7 pillars represented? Is any pillar overrepresented? Are Events and Booking posts no more than 1 in 5?

**Pillars:**
1. The Animals
2. The Moments
3. The Work
4. Indigenous Identity
5. Events and Booking
6. Education
7. North Idaho Life

### Block 3: Note Seasonal and Operational Context (10 minutes)
- What events, holidays, or regional moments are happening this month that content should respond to?
- What events TC is running this month that will generate content?
- What is coming in 60 days that needs content now? (Teaser posts, booking pushes, event announcements)

**Add these to the calendar** as anchor posts or note them at the top of the calendar file.

### Block 4: Set Creator Income Priority for the Month (15 minutes)
- Review `docs/creator-income-roadmap.md` — what tier is TC currently working on?
- What is the specific creator income action for this month? (Draft UGC pitches? Follow up on open conversations? Build the media kit? Set up affiliate links?)
- Assign a specific deliverable with a due date by the end of the month

**Monthly creator income actions by stage:**
- **Early stage (now):** Send 5–10 UGC pitches; set up 3 affiliate links; begin media kit
- **Building stage:** Follow up on Tier 1 relationships; pitch tourism board content partnership; complete media kit v1
- **Growing stage:** Maintain active brand partnerships; open Tier 2 pitch conversations; update media kit with new data

### Block 5: Corporate Lead Check (10 minutes)
- Any open corporate leads from last month? What is their status? What needs to happen?
- Any new corporate outreach planned this month? Who are the 1–2 targets?
- Any venue partnership outreach needed?
- Is the website's corporate events page current?

## Content Production Rhythm

This is how content actually gets made — the production schedule that supports the posting calendar.

### Weekly Production Targets
- **2–3 pieces of evergreen content** per week: posts that do not expire, can be used anytime, always relevant (horse personality, behind-the-scenes, education)
- **1–2 pieces of timely content** per week: tied to a current moment, season, event, or trend
- **1 long-form piece** every 2 weeks: a blog post, an in-depth carousel, or a multi-part series

### Content Batching
When TC runs an event, batch content production:
- Shoot stills and video during the event
- Debrief immediately after: what moments stood out, what was said, what was funny or unexpected
- Produce 3–5 pieces of content from a single event: event recap, animal personality clip, behind-the-scenes setup, instant photo delivery moment, guest reaction

This is how TC gets content efficiency without manufacturing fake moments.

### Draft-to-Post Pipeline
```
Idea captured (content queue note)
   ↓
Draft written (saved to /content/drafts/ as YYYY-MM-DD-platform-topic.md)
   ↓
Review (caption, tone check, hashtags, CTA)
   ↓
Approve (Stacie confirms — can be a quick self-review)
   ↓
Schedule (native scheduler or Buffer/Later)
   ↓
Live (post goes up)
   ↓
First-hour response (respond to early comments within 60 minutes)
   ↓
Performance noted (Friday review)
```

### Approval Workflow Details
- **Standard posts:** Stacie reviews caption + image before scheduling. Fast review — 2–3 minutes per post is enough.
- **Corporate-facing or partnership content:** Slightly longer review — confirm nothing was said that could create a conflict or misrepresentation.
- **Sponsored or branded content:** Full review + confirm disclosure is correct (use #ad or #sponsored clearly per FTC requirements)
- **Anything about Indigenous identity:** Stacie's own voice only — no drafts from a third party should go out in this category without her full re-write

## Monthly Reviews

### Performance Check Protocol

**When:** During the first week of the new month (reviewing the month that just ended)
**Time:** 30–45 minutes
**Where to find data:** Instagram Insights, TikTok Analytics, Facebook Insights (native apps)

**Metrics to track** (simple platform-native, no complicated dashboards needed):

| Platform | Metric 1 | Metric 2 | Metric 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Follower change (net) | Top post by reach | Profile visits |
| Instagram | Avg Reel plays | Story views (avg) | Link in bio clicks |
| TikTok Business | Follower change (net) | Top video by views | Profile visits |
| TikTok Personal | Follower change (net) | Top video by shares | |
| Facebook | Page reach (total) | Top post by engagement | Event RSVPs |

**Save to:** `/TribalCowboy-Social/insights/YYYY-MM-insights.md` — a simple monthly note file with these numbers and any patterns you noticed.

**Pattern questions to ask:**
- Which pillar performed best this month?
- Which format (Reel, still, carousel, TikTok) drove the most reach?
- Did any post drive profile visits or follows — and if so, what was it about that post?
- Did any post underperform — and why? (Wrong time, weak hook, off-pillar?)

### Creator and Monetization Review Protocol

**When:** Mid-month and end of month
**Time:** 20 minutes

**Questions:**
- What creator income actions were taken this month? (Pitches sent, conversations started, content delivered)
- Did any UGC work or partnerships close? What was the outcome?
- What affiliate revenue, if any, was generated?
- What is the status of open creator income conversations?
- What is the one creator income priority for next month?

**Track in:** `/TribalCowboy-Social/insights/creator-income-tracker.md` — a running log of pitches, conversations, status, and revenue.

**Tracker format:**
```
[Date] | [Brand] | [Type: UGC / Sponsored / Affiliate / Partnership] | [Status: Pitched / Negotiating / Active / Closed / Declined] | [Value: $0 / $XXX] | [Notes]
```

### Partnership Pipeline Review

**When:** End of month
**Time:** 15 minutes

- List all active partnership conversations (brand deals, tourism, venue partnerships)
- For each: what is the status? What is the next action? Is this still worth pursuing?
- Any new partnership opportunities identified this month to add to the pipeline?
- Any to close or deprioritize?

### Corporate Lead Check

**When:** Mid-month and end of month
**Time:** 15 minutes

- List all corporate leads — new this month plus any from prior months still open
- Status of each: new inquiry / proposal sent / deposit received / completed / declined
- Any corporate outreach planned for next month?
- Any completed events to follow up for testimonials or annual rebooking conversations?

### Website Content Review

**When:** End of month
**Time:** 20 minutes

- Were the planned 1–2 blog posts published?
- Are all service pages current (pricing ranges, seasonal availability)?
- Is the gallery updated with recent event photos?
- Is the media kit current (if it exists)?
- Is the contact/inquiry form working?
- Any SEO or content opportunities identified from this month's social content?

## Seasonal Calendar Integration

Content and business priorities shift by season. Plan 60 days ahead.

### Spring (March–May)
**Business:** Booking season opens. Fill May–August event calendar.
**Content priority:** Spring on the land, herd energy as season changes, pony party booking content, school program positioning
**Creator income priority:** Outreach to equine care and western apparel brands (spring product cycles open now)
**Corporate priority:** HR teams planning summer events — begin outreach in March for June–July events
**60-day look-ahead (March):** Memorial Day weekend pony parties and corporate events need content teasers by late April

### Summer (June–August)
**Business:** Peak event season. Execute well, document everything.
**Content priority:** Real event recaps, the photo delivery system in action, guest moments, behind-the-scenes of large groups
**Creator income priority:** Deliver any active brand partnerships with summer content; begin fall pitch cycle
**Corporate priority:** Annual event companies — note who books, follow up in October for next year
**60-day look-ahead (June):** Back-to-school programs and fall festival content needs to be planned by late July

### Fall (September–November)
**Business:** Harvest events, school programs, early holiday booking
**Content priority:** North Idaho autumn (visually exceptional), harvest festival content, holiday wagon ride teaser
**Creator income priority:** Begin pitching tourism boards for next year's content budgets (they open Q4)
**Corporate priority:** Holiday event companies booking December events — outreach starts in September
**60-day look-ahead (September):** December holiday wagon rides need content and booking push by late October

### Winter (December–February)
**Business:** Holiday events, then off-season
**Content priority:** Holiday wagon rides, winter barn content, behind-the-scenes of horse care in cold, spring booking teaser
**Creator income priority:** Plan and pitch for spring brand partnerships; complete or update media kit
**Corporate priority:** Annual company event planners start planning in January for spring — be present
**60-day look-ahead (December):** Spring booking season content and outreach needs to start in February

## How to Adjust Next Month Based on What Happened This Month

This is the most important loop in the system. Here is how to use the data.

### If a platform underperformed
- Is the content format right for that platform?
- Is the posting frequency consistent enough?
- Did TC engage with comments and drive early algorithm signals?
- Pull the best post from that platform and note what it had that the others did not

### If a content pillar consistently underperforms
- Is it a format issue (wrong format for the platform) or a content issue (the pillar is not resonating)?
- Try a different format for that pillar before abandoning it
- If education posts underperform as carousels, try them as Reels

### If a creator income pitch got no response
- Was the pitch to the right person (brand manager, not a generic contact form)?
- Was the angle specific enough to TC's differentiators?
- Try a different brand category next month

### If a booking lead came in from social
- Which post drove it? What pillar, format, topic?
- Make more content like that

### If corporate outreach got no response
- Was it to the right decision-maker?
- Did the message lead with the right framing (outcome, not features)?
- Try a different company category next month

### If something performed unexpectedly well
- Do not assume it is a fluke — try again with a similar topic or format
- Note the specific element that worked (hook, topic, tone, visual, timing)
- Build that element into the next month's content plan
