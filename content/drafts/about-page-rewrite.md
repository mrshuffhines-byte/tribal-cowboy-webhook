## About Tribal Cowboy

Tribal Cowboy is an Indigenous-owned equine experience company based in Athol, Idaho — founded and led by Stacie Huffhines, Nisenan Maidu.

We bring horses to events, schools, corporate activations, and families across North Idaho and Eastern Washington. Ponies, Clydesdales, wagon rides, photo experiences, and educational programs — all of it fully managed by people who have been doing this for years and take it seriously.

---

## Who We Are

This is a family business in the real sense of the word.

Stacie is Nisenan Maidu — her people are of the Pacific Northwest, connected to this land long before Idaho was Idaho. She grew up with horses. She built this business from the ground up, and she runs it with the values she was raised with: show up, do the work, take care of people.

Her husband is a Marine Corps veteran and former law enforcement officer. He has served this country and this community. That's not a marketing angle — it's just who this family is.

They are raising their family in North Idaho because they believe in this place. In the land, in the communities, in the people here who still know their neighbors and look out for each other.

---

## Why This Business Exists — Billy

Stacie had a brother named Billy.

Billy had Down syndrome and a love for people, music, and a laugh that could change the temperature of a room. He passed away in 2025. He is the reason this business has a mission that goes beyond events.

Tribal Cowboy is building a fully accessible therapy ranch — level walking areas, wagon ramps for wheelchairs, safe and unhurried interaction with horses for individuals with Down syndrome and other special needs, and real respite time for the caregivers who never get a break.

The goal is to be fully running by 2027.

Every corporate partnership, every sponsored appearance, every booking contributes to that ranch. That's what Stacie is building. That's why she shows up.

## What We Believe

We believe horses are one of the most honest things on earth. They don't perform. They don't pretend. Children who are afraid of everything will walk straight up to one of our ponies, and something shifts.

We believe in North Idaho — the land, the seasons, the people who have built lives here and intend to stay.

We believe a business can be rooted in Indigenous identity and also be deeply, genuinely part of this American West community. Those things have always gone together. We are proof of it.

## What We Do

- **Pony visits and rides** for birthday parties, school programs, and community events
- **Clydesdale carriage and wagon experiences** for festivals, corporate events, weddings, and grand openings
- **Horse photography experiences** — live animal props for professional photo sessions
- **Educational school programs** — horsemanship and Pacific Northwest Indigenous horse history
- **Corporate and brand activations** — sponsored appearances with branded photo overlays

We serve North Idaho and Eastern Washington. Based in Athol — Coeur d'Alene, Hayden, Spokane, and surrounding areas are our home territory.
