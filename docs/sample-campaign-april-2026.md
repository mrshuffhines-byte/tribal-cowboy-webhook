# Waking Up the Herd — April 2026 Campaign

---

## Campaign Overview

**Name:** Waking Up the Herd
**Theme:** Spring arrival. The horses are back, the season is open, and North Idaho is waking up.
**Dates:** April 1–30, 2026
**Primary Objective:** Build authority and open booking season
**Secondary Objectives:** Signal creator readiness for brand partnerships, build corporate prospect credibility, attract lifestyle brand interest
**Target Audiences:**
- Affluent families planning spring/summer events in North Idaho and Eastern Washington
- Corporate event planners scouting distinctive outdoor experiences
- Lifestyle and western brand sponsors seeking authentic creator content
- Organic followers who have been watching through the off-season

**Why this campaign, why now:** Spring is when families start planning. It's also when corporate event budgets open. And it's when Tribal Cowboy has the most visual content — animals emerging, land greening, early morning light. This campaign does four things at once without looking like it's trying: it warms up the audience, signals the season is open, builds the kind of trust corporate buyers need before they reach out, and produces content that sponsors want their names next to.

---

## Campaign Theme and Message

**What this campaign says:**

Tribal Cowboy is not a vendor you call when you need horses. It's a place — a real working herd, real land, a real person behind it who knows these animals by name and has been doing this before it was a content niche.

The "Waking Up the Herd" theme works on two levels: the literal spring return of the horses after winter, and the broader idea that something worth paying attention to is coming back into full motion. It creates anticipation without hype.

**What feeling it creates:**

Grounded. Real. A little bit of longing for the outdoors. The feeling you get when you see someone who is genuinely good at something and you want to be near it. Not "I want to go to this party" — more like "I want to be part of whatever this is."

**What the audience walks away knowing:**

1. Tribal Cowboy has a real herd with real names and personalities
2. This business is run by one woman who knows horses, knows her land, and takes her work seriously
3. The season is open and there are ways to be part of it
4. This is not a rental. This is not a pop-up. This is rooted.

**Corporate buyers walk away knowing:**

This operation is professional, visually credible, and has an audience that is warm and engaged — not just a follower count.

**Brand sponsors walk away knowing:**

The creator behind this account produces content that looks like editorial, not advertising. The audience trusts her. The content is consistent. This is a real partnership opportunity.

## Campaign Success Signals

### What Does Success Look Like Without Formal Analytics

Success in April is not a number. It's a pattern.

**Signs the campaign is working:**

- DMs increase by Week 3. Not necessarily booking inquiries — just people responding. "I love Duke." "This is beautiful." "Where is this?" People who were silently watching are now engaging.
- Comments on the herd introduction posts have names in them. "Duke!" or "I love Biscuit" means the animals are becoming characters to the audience.
- Story polls get responses. Even 10–15 responses on a poll is an engaged audience, not a passive one.
- Someone books who says they've been following for a while. That's the campaign working exactly as intended.
- A brand or business reaches out in April or May and mentions seeing the content. They may not say "the campaign" but they saw something that made them reach out.
- The corporate Facebook post (Apr 25) gets shared or saved by someone who looks like a business owner or event planner.

**Signs something needs to change:**

- Week 1 and 2 posts get low engagement and Week 4's booking CTA falls flat. If nobody warmed up to the herd, the CTA has no foundation. Go back and post more specific, personality-driven herd content before pushing booking again.
- TikTok hooks aren't holding viewers past 3 seconds. If the watch time is low, the hooks aren't specific enough. Get more specific, more surprising, or more immediately visual.
- No DMs by Week 2. The content is being seen but not responded to. Check: is the caption creating a reason to respond? Is there a question, a poll, a moment of connection? Add more of those.
- The comment section is quiet. Not bad comments — just quiet. This means the content is fine but not prompting conversation. Add a direct question to every caption in Week 3 and see what happens.
- Story views drop week over week. The story content is not delivering enough variety or value to keep people tapping through daily. Vary the format — mix video, photo, poll, quiz, and text slides across the week.
