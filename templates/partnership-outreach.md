## Tone Rules

Read these before writing any outreach message.

- Write peer-to-peer. You are not asking for a favor — you are offering a business arrangement with mutual value.
- Be specific about what you want and what you're offering. Vague pitches go straight to the archive.
- Name exactly why their brand fits TC's audience or vice versa. Show you looked at what they do.
- One compliment maximum — and only if it's genuine and specific. No "I love your brand."
- Never open with "I would love to collaborate." That's the kiss of death.
- If TC has numbers or social proof, use them. If not, lead with reach and specificity of the audience.
- Short is better. If a partner needs to scroll to find the ask, you've already lost them.

---

## What to Include

- Brief, sharp brand description (2 sentences max — who TC is and what makes it different)
- Audience description (who follows TC, what they care about, what region)
- Specific collaboration idea — not "we could work together" but "here's the content I'd create"
- Clear ask or proposed next step
- Easy reply path — email, DM, or a simple yes/no question to move the conversation forward

## What to Never Include

- "Would love to collaborate" without saying exactly what that means
- Excessive compliments or sycophantic openers
- Long backstories before the ask
- Desperation signals: "even something small would be great," "whatever works for you"
- Unprofessional formatting — walls of text, no line breaks, emoji overload

## Collaboration Angle Options

Use one per pitch. Don't pitch multiple angles in the same message — pick the most relevant and commit to it.

| Angle | What It Means | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Sponsored post** | TC creates content featuring the brand's product. Agreed deliverables, clear compensation. | Consumer brands with outdoor/western/family positioning |
| **UGC content** | TC creates authentic-looking content the brand can use in their own channels — no TC posting required. | Brands with strong paid social budgets |
| **Event co-activation** | Brand shows up at a TC event (physically or through product placement). Content captured at the event. | Local/regional brands, gear companies, food/beverage brands |
| **Ambassador** | Ongoing relationship. TC uses and features the brand across multiple posts over time. | Brands TC actually uses and believes in |
| **Affiliate** | TC earns commission on sales driven through a link or code. Low barrier to start. | Brands with existing affiliate infrastructure |
| **Content swap** | TC creates content for the brand; the brand creates content featuring TC. No money exchanged. | Complementary brands with similar audience size |
