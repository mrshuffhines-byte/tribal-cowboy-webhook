# Tribal Cowboy — Channel Strategy

**Last updated:** 2026-03-21
**Business:** Tribal Cowboy LLC — Stacie Huffhines, Nisenan Maidu
**Priority order:** Creator income > brand deals > partnerships > corporate > premium inquiries > bookings

---

## Channel Overview

Tribal Cowboy operates across six channels. Each one has a distinct job. They are not interchangeable. Do not post the same content the same way on every platform — it flattens the brand and signals algorithm weakness.

| Channel | Primary Job | Secondary Job |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Brand authority | Sponsorship credibility |
| Facebook | Community + events | Local booking pipeline |
| TikTok Business | Creator credibility | Brand deal positioning |
| TikTok Personal | Audience growth | Stacie's voice / relatability |
| Website / Blog | SEO + trust hub | Long-form credibility anchor |
| DMs + Comments | Relationship layer | Conversion and routing |

## 1. Instagram (@TribalCowboy) — Primary Brand Authority Channel

### Role
Instagram is the portfolio. It's where potential sponsors, corporate clients, press, and premium guests go to decide whether Tribal Cowboy is legitimate. Every post needs to hold up as a brand piece, not just content. This is the permanent record.

### Primary Audience
- Women 28–55 in North Idaho and Eastern Washington
- Corporate event planners and HR managers
- Western lifestyle enthusiasts
- Brand partnerships and PR managers searching for Indigenous creators
- Photography and event industry peers

### Content Types
- High-quality stills: horses, landscape, event moments, close-up details
- Reels: 15–45 seconds, story-driven, real moments with narrative captions
- Carousels: education, behind-the-scenes, event recaps (5–10 slides)
- Stories: daily life, polls, countdowns, DM prompts
- Collabs: guest posts with vendors, event venues, regional brands

### Tone
Warm, specific, grounded. Captions tell a moment, not a feature list. First or third person only. One exclamation mark maximum per post. No urgency language.

### Posting Frequency
- Feed posts: 4–5 per week
- Stories: daily or near-daily
- Reels: 2–3 per week minimum (Reels drive discovery)

### What to Post
- Millie, Abby, and the herd — personality-driven content, not generic "pretty horse"
- Guest reaction moments (with permission)
- The work: grooming, setup, hauling, early mornings
- the photo delivery system in action — the delivery experience, not just the photo props
- Indigenous identity moments handled with care and specificity
- Seasonal content tied to North Idaho: snow, harvest, spring mud, summer light
- Event recaps with real specifics (company name if permitted, guest count, what happened)
- Creator-positioned content: Stacie's perspective, her decisions, her animals

### What Never to Post
- Stock-looking flatlay content with no personality
- Generic "Book now!" promotional posts more than once every 5 posts
- Low-light, blurry, or visually inconsistent images
- Anything that sounds like a press release in the caption
- Posts without a caption — captions build authority on Instagram
- Content that mimics other accounts rather than documenting TC's real life

### CTA Strategy
- Most posts: no hard CTA — let the content do the work
- Event or service posts: one soft CTA — "Link in bio" or "DM us to check dates"
- Stories: swipe up / link sticker for booking or website
- Never: countdown urgency, "limited spots," artificial scarcity language

### Growth Tactics
- Collab posts with North Idaho businesses, event venues, western brands
- Tag relevant accounts in posts (without over-tagging — 3 max)
- Consistent Reels cadence — the algorithm rewards it
- Respond to every comment in the first hour after posting
- Use 5–10 hashtags from the hashtag bank — mix core with reach tags
- Save location tags to every post (Athol, ID; North Idaho; Coeur d'Alene area)
- Go live quarterly — behind-barn moments, Q&A

### Monetization Connection
- Instagram grid is the primary asset in the media kit
- Engagement rate on Instagram is what brand deal partners check first
- Creator Fund and sponsored content deals typically originate here
- Link in bio routes to website booking page and/or affiliate links

### Website Connection
- Every blog post gets at least one Instagram post
- Instagram Reels can embed in website blog for SEO
- Bio link routes to website — update seasonally

## 2. Facebook — Community and Event Channel

### Role
Facebook is for the local community. People in North Idaho and Eastern Washington use Facebook to find events, share with neighbors, and look up businesses before they call. It's also where older demographics (parents booking pony parties, HR managers, event coordinators) spend time. Facebook is not for content experimentation — it's for reliability and community trust.

### Primary Audience
- Local families in North Idaho and Eastern Washington
- Event planners and community organizations
- Parents looking for kids' activity vendors
- Corporate admin assistants and HR coordinators
- Local Facebook Groups members (Coeur d'Alene events, North Idaho community groups)

### Content Types
- Event announcements and updates
- Behind-the-scenes longer-form stories
- Shared Instagram posts (with minor caption edits for platform tone)
- Facebook Events for every bookable event TC hosts
- Community engagement: questions, polls, local tags
- Photo albums from events (Facebook still handles photo albums well)

### Tone
Slightly warmer and more community-forward than Instagram. More complete information — people on Facebook want to know logistics. Still no corporate language. Still no fluff.

### Posting Frequency
- Feed posts: 3–4 per week
- Facebook Events: create for every public event, at least 2 weeks in advance
- Shared posts from Instagram: acceptable, but write a new caption don't just copy-paste

### What to Post
- Full event details: date, location, what to expect, how to book
- Community milestone posts (anniversaries, new animals in the herd, seasonal openings)
- Photo albums from recent events (tag guests with permission)
- Shared local coverage or press mentions
- Behind-the-scenes longer than Instagram would support
- Booking reminders when availability is genuinely limited

### What Never to Post
- TikTok-native content with watermarks — Facebook algorithm suppresses it
- More than 2–3 hashtags per post (Facebook hashtags add minimal value)
- Reposts without any customization — rewrite the caption for this audience
- Political commentary or anything that could fragment the community following

### CTA Strategy
- Events: "RSVP via Facebook Events" or "Check the link below to book"
- General posts: "Comment below if you want more info" — drives engagement
- Never: urgency pressure, "DM fast before it's gone"

### Growth Tactics
- Join and participate in North Idaho event and community Facebook Groups
- Share posts to relevant groups (when group rules allow)
- Create and promote Facebook Events — they get organic reach
- Tag venues and partners in event posts
- Boost high-performing posts with $10–20 for local event reach

### Monetization Connection
- Facebook is the secondary lead source for corporate and group bookings
- Corporate coordinators often find TC via Facebook search before calling
- Not a primary creator income channel — focus there is Instagram and TikTok

### Website Connection
- All event posts link to the website booking page
- Blog content shared on Facebook extends website SEO reach

## 3. TikTok Business (@TribalCowboy) — Brand Authority + Creator Credibility

### Role
TikTok Business is where Tribal Cowboy positions itself as a professional, bookable operation. This account is polished enough to show corporate clients and brand partners, but native enough to perform algorithmically. The goal is not virality for its own sake — it's consistent authority-building content that makes TC look like a real media brand, not just someone posting horse videos.

### Primary Audience
- Horse and western lifestyle enthusiasts
- Event industry professionals
- Brand deal decision-makers searching for creator partners
- Media buyers looking for authentic Indigenous creators
- Potential corporate event clients (they're on TikTok now)

### Content Types
- Short-form event footage: 15–30 second clips with text overlay or voiceover
- "Day in the life" series content
- Horse personality clips — Millie, Abby, Jolene, Monster, Master Chief, herd moments
- Educational content: what instant photo delivery does, what a Clydesdale wagon ride is like
- Behind-the-scenes event setup
- Trending format adoption when authentic to TC

### Tone
Direct, punchy, hook in the first line. Talk-to-camera energy even without showing face. Confident without being loud. No hashtag overload in caption — put them in first comment if needed.

### Posting Frequency
- 4–5 posts per week minimum (TikTok rewards volume more than any other platform)
- Trending formats should go up within 24–48 hours of spotting the trend

### What to Post
- Any 15–30 second moment with a clear visual hook
- Text-on-screen stories with B-roll
- "What I didn't expect about running a horse experience business" type formats
- Reaction content (guests seeing Clydesdales for the first time)
- Indigenous-owned business identity content — authentic, not trending

### What Never to Post
- Low-effort reposts of Instagram content without TikTok-native editing
- Anything that looks like a Facebook post with a border
- Content that feels like an ad
- Anything relying on sound trends that will be dated in 48 hours
- Long captions — TikTok captions should be under 150 characters on the business account

### CTA Strategy
- Most posts: no explicit CTA — TikTok audiences are discovery-mode, not conversion-mode
- Series or recurring content: "Follow for part 2"
- Event content: "Link in bio to book" only when it's a high-intent moment

### Growth Tactics
- Consistent posting cadence — the algorithm penalizes gaps
- Use 4–6 hashtags from TikTok hashtag bank
- Stitch and Duet relevant content from western lifestyle or equine space
- Comment on trending equine or North Idaho content to build community presence
- TikTok Creator Marketplace profile — needs to be active and complete

### Monetization Connection
- Primary platform for brand deal discovery by media buyers
- Creator Fund eligibility requires consistent posting and follower thresholds
- TikTok is where UGC package clients often find creators first
- Sponsored content disclosures: always use #ad or #sponsored clearly

### Website Connection
- TikTok bio links to website
- High-performing TikTok content can embed in blog posts for dwell time

## 4. TikTok Personal (Stacie's Creator Voice) — Relatability + Growth

This is Stacie's personal creator presence — separate from the business account. The personal account can take risks the business account cannot. It's where the Indigenous identity, the behind-the-scenes frustrations, the real opinions, and the unfiltered moments live. It feeds audience trust back to the business account and makes Stacie a recognizable creator, not just a brand logo.

### Primary Audience
- Indigenous creators and followers
- Horse girls and western women across age ranges
- Small business owners in the equine or event space
- Authenticity-seeking audiences tired of polished content

### Content Types
- Personal opinion and commentary — horse industry, small business, Indigenous creator experience
- Unfiltered moments: things that went wrong, what nobody tells you, real costs
- Creator journey content: building TC, what it's like running this business
- Cultural identity content: Nisenan Maidu context, what it means to run an Indigenous business
- Trend participation that wouldn't fit the business account
- Collabs with other Indigenous creators

### Tone
Unfiltered but purposeful. This is not a dump account — it's a voice account. Still no AI-speak. Still no corporate tone. More edge is allowed here.

### Posting Frequency
- 3–5 posts per week
- Does not need to match business account schedule

### What to Post
- "Things I wish someone told me before starting a horse experience business"
- Real opinions on the equine industry, event industry
- Indigenous creator perspective content — not performance, real
- Moments where TC's real personality shows through

### What Never to Post
- Content that contradicts TC's brand positioning
- Complaints about specific clients or vendors
- Anything that would embarrass a future corporate partner

### Monetization Connection
- Personal account grows Stacie's creator profile independent of the business

## 5. Website / Blog (www.tribalcowboy.com) — SEO and Trust Hub

### Role
The website is the only channel TC fully controls. It doesn't have an algorithm. It doesn't change its rules. Everything else on social media points here eventually. The website does two jobs: (1) confirms legitimacy for people who found TC on social media, and (2) attracts new visitors through search who never heard of TC before.

### Primary Audience
- Search-intent visitors: people Googling "pony party North Idaho," "Clydesdale wagon rides Idaho," "horse photography Coeur d'Alene"
- Social media visitors arriving to book or learn more
- Corporate clients doing due diligence
- Press and media looking for contact and background

### Content Types
- Service pages: one page per service, optimized for local search
- Blog posts: 500–1,000 words, specific topics with local SEO value
- About page: Stacie's story, the herd, the Indigenous identity — handled with honesty
- Gallery: event photos, horse portraits, instant photo delivery examples
- Press / Media page: coverage, mentions, media kit download link
- FAQ: what to expect, pricing ranges, booking process

### Blog Topics to Develop
- "What to expect at a pony party in North Idaho" (local search + education)
- "How instant photo delivery works at equine events" (product SEO)
- "Clydesdale wagon rides: what makes them different from pony rides"
- "Planning a corporate team event in North Idaho" (corporate SEO)
- "Indigenous-owned businesses in North Idaho" (identity + PR)
- "Horse photography tips for outdoor events" (photographer SEO crossover)

### Posting Frequency
- New blog post: 1–2 per month minimum
- Service page updates: seasonal (spring booking season, fall wrap-up)
- Gallery updates: after every major event

### What to Post
- Long-form educational content that social media cannot hold
- Booking information with clear pricing ranges (don't hide prices entirely)
- Real photography — not stock photos

### What Never to Post
- Stock photography
- Generic copy that could apply to any event company
- Outdated pricing or service information

### Monetization Connection
- SEO traffic = organic booking leads without ad spend
- Blog posts can include affiliate links (western gear, horse care products)
- Media kit hosted and downloadable here
- Press page builds brand deal credibility

## 6. DMs and Comments — Relationship and Conversion Layer

### Role
This is where interest becomes relationship, and relationship becomes revenue. DMs and comments are not administrative tasks — they are the most valuable touchpoint in the customer journey. How TC responds to a comment or DM in the first hour after posting directly affects algorithm performance and conversion rate.

See the full routing rules in `docs/engagement-routing-rules.md`.

### Response Speed Expectations
- Instagram comments: within 1 hour of posting, within 4 hours otherwise
- Instagram DMs: within 4 hours during business hours
- Facebook comments: within 8 hours
- Facebook DMs: within 8 hours
- Story replies: within 24 hours

### Tone in All Responses
- Warm but not performative
- Specific — reference something from their message
- Never templated-sounding
- Never desperate or pushy

## Cross-Platform Repurposing Rules

Repurposing is smart. But lazy repurposing hurts the brand. Follow these rules.

### What Can Be Repurposed and How

| Source | Can Go To | What Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram Reel | TikTok Business | Remove watermark, rewrite caption for TikTok tone, adjust audio if needed |
| Instagram still | Facebook | Rewrite caption for community tone, limit hashtags to 2–3 |
| TikTok Business clip | Instagram Reel | Remove TikTok watermark, add longer caption, update hashtags |
| Blog post | Instagram Carousel | Pull 5–7 key points into carousel slides, add brand visuals |
| Blog post | Facebook | Share link with 2-sentence intro caption |
| Event recap photos | All platforms | Rewrite caption for each platform's tone |

### What Never Gets Repurposed Directly
- TikTok Personal content onto the business account without deliberate editing
- Facebook-formatted content onto TikTok (text-heavy, no hook structure)
- Low-resolution screenshots of other people's content
- Stories repurposed as feed posts — they're different format types

### Watermark Rule
Never post TikTok-watermarked content to Instagram or Facebook. Download via SnapTik or similar before cross-posting.

## Content Sequencing

Order matters. Here is how to think about it.

### Within a Single Piece of Content
1. TikTok Business first (if it's a video — TikTok rewards early posting)
2. Instagram Reel or feed post second (within 24 hours)
3. Facebook third (same day or next day — include more context)
4. Blog if the topic has search value (publish within the same week)

### Within a Week
- Never post the same content pillar two days in a row
- Alternate: Animals → Moments → Work → Indigenous Identity → Events → Education → North Idaho Life
- Events and booking content: maximum 1 in every 5 posts

### For Campaigns or Events
1. Teaser post 2 weeks out (IG + FB)
2. Behind-the-scenes content the week before (TikTok)
3. Day-of story content (IG Stories)
4. Recap content within 48 hours (all platforms)
5. Long-tail blog post within the same week

## Platform-Exclusive Content Recommendations

Some content should stay on one platform only. It performs better there and maintains platform-specific value.

| Content | Platform Only | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Long-horizon cultural identity posts | Instagram | Depth of caption, audience receptivity |
| Unfiltered personal opinions | TikTok Personal | Tone mismatch with Instagram brand aesthetic |
| Multi-photo event albums | Facebook | Still performs best there |
| Detailed booking logistics | Website | Search intent audience |
| Creator journey commentary | TikTok Personal | Format-native, wrong tone for IG grid |
| Press and media info | Website | Professional credibility asset |
| Trend-reactive content | TikTok Business | Speed and format alignment |
