Step 4 — Emergency Services
911
If any injury, fire, or threat to life
🐴
Animal Distress
  1. Stop rides immediately — handler signals pause to all staff
  2. Remove distressed animal from activation zone — lead calmly to trailer
  3. Provide water, shade, and rest — do not return without full assessment
  4. Temp policy: no animal works above 90°F or below 20°F ambient
  5. Rotate replacement pony if available and ready
  6. Contact SAVE Veterinary if any medical concern — (208) 946-1998
  7. Document in Incident Log — time, animal, trigger observed
🚧
Barrier / Perimeter Breach
  1. Handler verbally redirects the guest immediately
  2. Pause rides — announce brief hold
  3. Secure the animal — move to far side of panel if needed
  4. Re-seat and confirm panel perimeter integrity
  5. Resume only after full perimeter check
  6. If repeat breach: guest removed from activation area
🩹
Guest Injury
  1. Secure all animals — rides pause immediately
  2. Clear the immediate area — ask bystanders to step back
  3. Administer first aid if trained and appropriate
  4. Call 911 if injury is beyond minor (fall, bite, head contact)
  5. Notify host manager on-site — do not leave them uninformed
  6. Do not resume rides until area is fully secure and cleared
  7. Document: guest name (if provided), time, what occurred, witness names
  8. Written incident report to host within 24 hours
🌩
Weather Hold
  1. Pause rides at first sign of lightning or sustained high wind
  2. Move animals to trailer for shelter — do not leave in open
  3. Notify host manager — announce hold to guests
  4. Resume when safe — minimum 30 min after last lightning
  5. Heat hold: rides paused if ambient temp exceeds 90°F — animals moved to shade with continuous water access
  6. Cold hold: rides paused if temp drops below 20°F
  7. If event cannot safely resume: full pack-out protocol (below)
🚨
Emergency Evacuation
  1. Call out "Pack out" to all Tribal Cowboy staff
  2. Clear guest queue — direct people away from activation zone
  3. All animals led to and loaded into trailer — target: 15 minutes
  4. Remove panels and fencing — leave clear path for emergency vehicles
  5. Stacie notifies host manager immediately with reason and timeline
  6. Written debrief to host within 24 hours
♿
ADA & Accessibility
  1. Clydesdale photo station is fully accessible — no riding required
  2. Clear path maintained from event entry to photo station at all times
  3. Horses trained alongside wheelchairs, power chairs, and service animals
  4. Handler adjusts interaction for guests with reactive movement or sound
  5. No guest is turned away from the photo experience for mobility reasons
Pre-Event Briefing Checklist
  • Host manager contact confirmed and on-site
  • Trailer parking and access path confirmed — clear turnaround
  • Power connection located (if needed for fans)
  • Perimeter panels installed — all entry/exit points confirmed
  • Shade structure (10×20 tent + canopies) set up over rest area
  • Water tank filled — animals watered before first rides
  • Diaper bags on all working animals
  • Rotation schedule confirmed (3 ponies — 45 min on / 20–30 min rest; 4 hr max per animal per event)
  • Ground surface inspected — no glass, metal, holes, or slick conditions
  • Safety signage posted at queue entry — rules and rider requirements visible
  • All Tribal Cowboy staff briefed — roles assigned
  • Host staff briefed — 10-min walk-through complete
  • EAP copy left with host day-of manager
  • SAVE Vet on standby: (208) 946-1998 (24-hr large animal line)
