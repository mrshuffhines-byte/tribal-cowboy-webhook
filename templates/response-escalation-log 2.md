**Tribal Cowboy LLC — Sensitive Inbound Message Tracking**

## How to Use This Log

This log exists for inbound messages that cannot be handled with a standard response. If a message requires judgment, involves a complaint, raises a legal or ethical concern, or falls outside the normal inquiry or partnership path — it goes here before any response is sent.

**Process:**
1. Receive message that doesn't fit standard routing
2. Open this log and create a new entry (copy the entry template below)
3. Fill in all fields before taking any action
4. Determine resolution action — respond, hold, escalate, or flag
5. Log the action taken and outcome
6. Flag for monthly review if unresolved

**Golden rule:** Do not respond to a sensitive or unusual message before logging it here. Logging first forces a pause. That pause prevents reactive responses that create bigger problems.

## Escalation Categories

Use these categories when classifying the escalation reason. More than one may apply.

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Complaint — service** | Guest or client unhappy with an experience, event, or service outcome |
| **Complaint — communication** | Person frustrated by a slow response, miscommunication, or perceived slight |
| **Pricing dispute** | Someone is challenging TC's rates, requesting a refund, or disputing a charge |
| **Booking conflict** | Double booking, cancellation dispute, or event date disagreement |
| **Legal or liability concern** | Message references injury, legal action, or liability. Stop. Log. Do not respond without guidance. |
| **Harassment or threatening message** | Any message that is aggressive, threatening, or abusive. Do not engage. Log and block if needed. |
| **Misrepresentation concern** | Someone claiming to be press, a brand, or an organization that doesn't check out |
| **Copyright or content dispute** | Someone claiming TC used their content, or TC believes their content was used without permission |
| **Creator / partner concern** | A brand deal or partnership that has gone sideways or raised a concern mid-execution |
| **Sensitive identity or cultural concern** | A message that involves TC's Indigenous identity in a way that requires careful response |
| **Other** | Anything that doesn't fit above but needs human review before responding |

## Resolution Status Options

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Logged, not yet acted on |
| **Responded** | A response was sent — details in log entry |
| **Resolved** | Issue is closed to satisfaction on both sides |
| **No action needed** | After review, no response or action was required |
| **Flagged for legal review** | Do not respond. This is in legal or external review. |

## Notes on Handling Specific Escalation Types

### Complaints (service or communication)
Acknowledge first. Don't defend before you understand. Ask a clarifying question if the situation isn't clear. If TC made an error, own it plainly — no corporate non-apology language. Never promise something that can't be delivered.

### Pricing disputes and refund requests
Do not offer refunds reactively. Log the message, understand the full context, then respond with TC's actual policy. If no formal refund policy exists, handle case by case — but make a decision and document it.

### Legal or liability concerns
**Stop. Do not respond.** Log the full message verbatim. Flag for legal review. No response goes out until there is clarity on the legal exposure. Even a casual "I'm so sorry that happened" can be used in a dispute.

### Harassment or threatening messages
Do not engage. Log the message and sender details in full. Block the sender. If threats are serious, contact local law enforcement. There is no correct response to a threatening message — silence and documentation is correct.

### Sensitive identity or cultural concerns
Read twice before responding once. If a message touches TC's Indigenous identity in a way that feels off — whether it's a well-meaning but clumsy question, an appropriative request, or something disrespectful — take time before responding. The response matters and should reflect TC's dignity, not a defensive or reactive posture.
