# Media Kit Outline
**Tribal Cowboy LLC — Creator & Partnership Media Kit**

This document is the structural guide and draft content for TC's media kit. The media kit itself should be designed as a PDF (1–4 pages) or a well-formatted web page. This outline provides the copy structure and guidance for building and updating it.

---

## When to Send the Media Kit

- When a brand or potential partner asks "can you tell us more about your audience?"
- When submitting to a brand campaign or creator marketplace
- When pitching a corporate partnership or sponsored event
- When reaching out to press or media contacts
- When a creator platform or agency requests credentials

**Never send the full media kit on the first cold message.** Lead with a one-paragraph pitch. Offer to send the kit as the next step. This filters serious partners from tire-kickers.

---

## How to Customize Before Sending

Before sending the media kit to any specific partner, update:
- The "collaboration formats" section to lead with the format most relevant to that partner
- Any audience notes that are specifically relevant to that brand's customer
- Remove sections that aren't relevant to the type of deal (e.g., skip the corporate event section when pitching a consumer brand deal)
- Add any recent performance metrics, events, or press that are relevant to this partner's interests

---

## How to Keep It Current

Review and update the media kit:
- Monthly: platform follower counts, engagement rate, recent events
- Quarterly: collaboration examples, testimonials, notable reach moments
- Annually: full review of positioning, audience description, and rate card

## Section 1: Brand Overview

**Headline:** Tribal Cowboy LLC — Indigenous-Owned Equine Experience, North Idaho

**Brand overview paragraph (for media kit):**

Tribal Cowboy is an Indigenous-owned equine experience business founded and operated by Stacie Huffhines, a Nisenan Maidu woman, in Athol, Idaho. Tribal Cowboy brings Clydesdale wagon rides, horse photography events, and pony experiences to families, corporate groups, and community organizations across North Idaho and Eastern Washington. The business operates at the intersection of western heritage, Indigenous identity, and modern event experience — a combination that is genuinely rare in the Pacific Northwest and increasingly sought out by brands looking to partner with authentic, regionally grounded creators.

**Premium positioning note (internal guidance):** TC is not a party vendor. It is a premium, values-driven brand with a specific audience and a clear visual identity. The media kit should read accordingly — confident, not desperate. Do not list pricing in the media kit without a consultation gate.

## Section 5: Brand Values and Identity

**What Tribal Cowboy stands for:**

- **Indigenous ownership and identity** — TC is owned and operated by a Nisenan Maidu woman. This identity is not a marketing angle — it is the foundation of everything TC does. Brand partners must be aligned with or at minimum respectful of Indigenous values.
- **Authentic western experience** — TC's horses are real. Its events are real. The content it produces reflects real life on the land in North Idaho, not a manufactured aesthetic.
- **Premium, not mass-market** — TC is not a discount vendor. Its experiences are priced and presented for clients who value quality. Brand partnerships should reflect the same positioning.
- **Community and place** — TC is rooted in North Idaho and Eastern Washington. It is part of a specific region, a specific community, and a specific tradition. Partnerships that honor that context are the right fit.

**What TC will not represent:**
- Brands with public controversies or values conflicts
- Products that contradict a family-safe, land-honoring identity
- Anything that would require TC to perform an identity that isn't its own

## Section 7: Partnership Formats Available

TC offers the following collaboration structures. Not all are available for every partner — match the format to the relationship.

| Format | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Sponsored post** | TC creates and publishes content featuring the brand's product. Clear deliverables, agreed compensation. | Consumer brands with outdoor/western/family positioning |
| **UGC content** | TC produces content for the brand to use in their own channels. No TC posting required. | Brands with strong paid social or ad budgets |
| **Event co-activation** | Brand is present (physically or through product placement) at a TC event. Content captured and shared. | Local/regional brands, gear companies, experience-adjacent brands |
| **Ambassador relationship** | Ongoing, multi-post partnership over a defined period. TC integrates the brand naturally over time. | Brands TC uses and genuinely believes in |
| **Affiliate partnership** | TC promotes brand via tracked link or code. Earns commission on sales. | Brands with established affiliate infrastructure |
| **Content licensing** | Brand licenses existing TC photos or video for their own use. | Brands that need high-quality equine or western content |
| **Blog / SEO content** | TC creates a blog post or long-form content featuring the brand on www.tribalcowboy.com | Brands seeking SEO backlinks or longer content formats |
