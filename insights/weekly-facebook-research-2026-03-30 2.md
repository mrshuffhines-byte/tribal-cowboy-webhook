## Facebook-Specific Tactics

These patterns showed up across multiple competitors and are specific to Facebook (wouldn't work the same on Instagram):

1. **Facebook Events are underused by most competitors.** The businesses that do use Events for their bookable dates get organic reach because Facebook promotes Events to local users. Tribal Cowboy should create a Facebook Event for every public appearance, open booking weekend, or seasonal offering.

2. **The 2026 "Book Now" button.** Meta revamped how services display on Facebook pages. Businesses can now list specific services with pricing and a direct booking button. This is essentially a free booking widget.

3. **Albums still work on Facebook.** Unlike Instagram, Facebook photo albums get solid engagement — especially event recap albums where people tag themselves. After every event, posting an album of guest photos (with permission) drives shares and tags.

4. **Local community pages are the real reach play.** Pages like "North Idaho Events," "Post Falls Market," and "Coeur d'Alene Insider" are where locals actually look for things to do. Posting in these community pages (following their rules) gets more local eyes than posting on a business page alone.

5. **Review generation.** The Clydesdale and pony party businesses with the strongest presence all have active review sections. Facebook reviews feed into search results. A simple "Had a great time? Leave us a review on Facebook" follow-up after events would build this over time.

6. **Video-first.** Short-form video (Reels, 15–60 seconds) is the top organic reach format on Facebook in 2026. Text on screen, hook in first 3 seconds, no need for polish — phone footage of horses being horses outperforms produced content.

## Content Ideas for Tribal Cowboy

1. **"Meet the Herd" album series** — A Facebook album introducing each horse with name, personality, and a fun fact. "This is Millie. She's 1,800 lbs and afraid of plastic bags." Albums get shares and saves on Facebook in a way single photos don't.

2. **Spring booking announcement as a Facebook Event** — Instead of just a post saying "spring dates are open," create an actual Facebook Event called something like "Spring 2026 Booking Season — Tribal Cowboy" with dates, services, and a booking link. Let people mark "Interested" for organic reach.

3. **Behind-the-scenes morning routine Reel** — 30-second phone video: feeding, grooming, loading up the trailer. No narration needed — just text overlays like "4:45 AM / Before the party starts / This is what it takes." This hits the "The Work" content pillar and performs well as short-form video.

4. **"What's it like to get your photo back in 30 seconds?"** — A Reel showing the actual QR scan → photo delivery flow from a guest's perspective. Most people don't know instant facial recognition photo delivery exists. This is Tribal Cowboy's tech differentiator — show it off.

5. **Tag-a-local-vendor post** — "Planning a summer party in North Idaho? Here's who we love working with:" and tag The Attic, Lake City Rental, local caterers, etc. Cross-promotion builds community relationships and gets shared by the tagged businesses.

## Ad & Boost Observations

Specific Facebook ad transparency data wasn't accessible in this research cycle (Facebook's Ad Library requires direct page-by-page lookup). However, based on what's visible:

- **Most small pony party businesses are NOT running paid ads.** Their reach is organic + word of mouth + community pages. This means the paid ad space for "pony party near me" and "horse experience North Idaho" is likely uncrowded — low competition = low cost per click.
- **Clydesdale Outpost appears to invest in professional video content** that likely gets boosted — their production quality suggests a marketing budget behind it.
- **The 2026 Facebook playbook for service businesses** emphasizes boosting Reels and Event posts rather than running traditional image ads. A $10–20 boost on a well-performing Reel can reach 5,000–15,000 local users.
- **Recommended test:** Boost one "Meet the Herd" post and one event/booking post with $20 each, targeted to North Idaho + Spokane area, ages 25–55, interests in horses/family activities/outdoor events. Measure which format gets more engagement and bookings.
