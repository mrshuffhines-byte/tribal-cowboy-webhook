Based on my research across the industry, here's where I'd recommend you land. Your service includes significantly more than most carriage companies (photographer, branded overlays, QR delivery, full setup) — so you should price above the basic "horse + driver" competitors.

**Market context:**
- Basic carriage hire (1 horse, driver only, no photos): $250-$500/hr nationally
- Premium carriage hire with extras: $400-$800/hr
- Half-day (4hr) event bookings in comparable markets: $1,000-$2,000 single horse, $1,500-$2,500+ team
- Full-day typically runs 1.5-1.75x the half-day rate
- Your region (rural Idaho) will price lower than metro markets, but you're also one of the only games in town with Clydesdales — scarcity matters

**Why these numbers:**
- **Team + 12-passenger wagon at $1,800/half day ($450/hr effective):** This is your premium offering. Two Clydesdales, a big wagon, 12 seats — nothing else like it in the region. $450/hr effective rate is competitive nationally and accounts for your full-service package (photographer, branded delivery). Most events will book this.
- **Single Clydesdale options at $1,200/half day ($300/hr effective):** Competitive for a Clydesdale with full service. Lower than the team because one horse is less impressive and lower capacity, but still premium because it's a Clydesdale with a full photo package.
- **Full day at ~1.55x half day:** Standard industry discount for committing to a full day. The marginal cost to you is lower (already there, already set up) so you can pass some savings along while still making it worth the long day.
- **Mini pony wagon at $400/2hr:** In line with your pony party pricing and appropriate for the size/scope.

Adjust these up or down based on what you know about your local market and your costs (feed, transport, labor, insurance). But don't go lower than $1,000 for a half-day Clydesdale team booking — you're undervaluing the service if you do.

## SECTION 8: FAQ
**Squarespace block: Accordion / dropdown**

**Heading:** Common questions.

**Q: How much space do you need for wagon rides?**
A: It depends on the ride type. Loop rides need a cleared path — ideally a flat, firm surface (gravel, asphalt, packed dirt, or grass) with enough room for the wagon to make a circuit. For straight-line or point-to-point rides, we need a clear route between two stops. We'll help you figure out the best layout for your space when you reach out.

**Q: Can we book a wagon ride and pony interactions at the same event?**
A: Yes. We do combo setups for festivals and larger events regularly. Wagon rides running alongside a pony interaction pen is one of our most popular setups. Ask for package pricing when you contact us.

**Q: What happens if the weather is bad?**
A: Rain and mud are part of life in North Idaho. Light rain usually isn't a problem — the horses don't mind. If conditions become unsafe for the animals or guests, we'll work with you to reschedule. We don't cancel on you without a conversation first.

**Q: How many people fit on the wagon?**
A: The 12-passenger wagon (pulled by a Clydesdale team) seats 12 adults per trip. The Cinderella wagon is a smaller capacity. The air ride carriage fits up to 5 passengers. The mini pony wagon is designed for 1-2 small children per ride.

**Q: What's the difference between the Cinderella wagon and the air ride carriage?**
A: The Cinderella wagon has more of a storybook, open look — great for weddings and themed events. The air ride carriage is a smoother, more enclosed ride that seats up to 5 passengers — better for intimate events, VIP guest experiences, or events on rougher terrain. Both are pulled by a single Clydesdale.

**Q: Why is there a 4-hour minimum for Clydesdale bookings?**
A: Transporting, hitching, and managing a 1,800-lb draft horse (or two) is a serious operation. By the time we load, haul, arrive, set up, and break down, shorter bookings don't make sense for the horses or for us. The 4-hour minimum means your event gets real value and the horses get proper pacing with breaks built in.

**Q: Do you travel outside North Idaho?**
A: We're based in Athol and serve the full region — CdA, Post Falls, Hayden, Rathdrum, Spirit Lake, Spokane, Spokane Valley. We can go further; travel fees apply outside our core service area. Just ask.

**Q: Is this safe for young children?**
A: Yes. Our horses are trained for crowds and noise. The mini pony wagon is specifically designed for young kids. All rides are supervised by our trained handlers. We carry full liability insurance.
