## 10 Seasonal Content Prompts

### 1. "Spring shedding is no joke"
**Platform:** TikTok or Instagram Reel
**Pillar:** The Animals
**Angle:** Show the actual amount of hair coming off a Clydesdale in spring. Brush Millie or Abby and let the hair fly. It's funny, relatable to any horse person, and the visual of a mountain of horse hair is a scroll-stopper.
**Hook:** "This is one brushing session."

### 2. "What a Mother's Day pony party actually looks like"
**Platform:** Instagram + Facebook
**Pillar:** Events & Booking
**Angle:** Not a hard sell — paint the picture. Mom sitting in a chair watching her kid ride a pony for the first time. The branded photo she gets on her phone 30 seconds later. The moment when the Clydesdale walks up and she realizes how big they actually are. Warm, specific, visual.
**Hook:** "She didn't ask for flowers this year."
**Timing:** Post between April 14–21 for maximum booking conversion.

### 3. "Earth Day from someone whose people have been on this land"
**Platform:** Instagram
**Pillar:** Indigenous Identity
**Angle:** Stacie speaks to what Earth Day means as a Nisenan Maidu woman. Not a lecture. Not performative. Just: this land matters. These animals are part of it. This business is how she lives that.
**Date:** April 22 (already on calendar)

### 4. "What your event planner doesn't know about horse photography"
**Platform:** Instagram carousel or TikTok
**Pillar:** Education / Corporate
**Angle:** Corporate event planners don't know that branded photo overlays with facial recognition delivery exist. Educate them. Show what the finished product looks like: a guest photo with the company logo, event name, and date, delivered to their phone before they leave.
**Hook:** "Your guests will share these. That's the point."

### 5. "The wagon is ready"
**Platform:** Instagram or Facebook
**Pillar:** The Work
**Angle:** A single clean photo of the wagon — spring-dressed, parked on the property, light coming through the trees. Short caption: what it took to get it ready, what's different this year, what events are coming up. Simple. The wagon IS the product for corporate buyers.

### 6. "End-of-year school celebration? We come to you."
**Platform:** Facebook (community audience)
**Pillar:** Events & Booking
**Angle:** Teachers and PTA parents are planning May celebrations now. A direct, warm post about school programs: what kids learn, how the horses behave with big groups, how easy booking is.
**Timing:** Post April 14–18 to hit planning windows.

### 7. "Idaho in April" (property shot series)
**Platform:** Instagram
**Pillar:** North Idaho Life
**Angle:** Wide shot of the property — fence line, pasture, mountains in the distance, horses grazing. No sales angle. Just the land. These posts perform reliably and cost nothing to make.
**Hook:** "Not a bad office."

### 8. "First spring ride"
**Platform:** TikTok
**Pillar:** The Moments
**Angle:** If there's a first spring event or even just Stacie riding one of the horses for the first time this season — film it. The "first" framing creates natural interest. First ride of the year, first wagon pull, first time the horses see the new pasture gate.

### 9. "What goes in the trailer"
**Platform:** TikTok or Instagram Reel
**Pillar:** The Work
**Angle:** Pack for an event on camera. Show everything that goes in: harness, grooming tools, photo equipment, QR code signs, branded overlay printer/setup, safety equipment, water, hay. People have no idea what it takes. This is behind-the-scenes content that builds credibility with corporate buyers and shares well with horse lovers.
**Hook:** "Everything we bring to your event."

### 10. "Why we don't rent horses"
**Platform:** Instagram or Facebook
**Pillar:** Education / The Animals
**Angle:** Address a common question: do you rent the horses from somewhere? No. These are TC's horses — raised, trained, groomed, loved. That's why they're gentle. That's why they know what they're doing at events. This builds trust and differentiates from businesses that do use rented animals.
**Hook:** "Millie has been with us since she was [age]. She knows the routine better than I do."
