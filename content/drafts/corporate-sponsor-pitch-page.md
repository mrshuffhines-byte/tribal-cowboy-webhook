## Space and Logistics Requirements

- **Outdoor space:** Minimum 30 x 30 feet for pony setup; more for Clydesdale carriage
- **Surface:** Grass, packed dirt, gravel, or pavement (no hot asphalt in summer heat)
- **Parking:** Trailer access required near setup area
- **Power/water:** Not required for standard appearances; helpful for longer events
- **Duration:** Minimum 2 hours; half-day and full-day rates available

We handle everything. You just need the space.

## A Note on Who We Are

Tribal Cowboy is a family business. Stacie Huffhines is Nisenan Maidu — Indigenous-owned from the ground up. Her husband is a Marine Corps veteran and former law enforcement officer. They built this business in Athol, Idaho and have been bringing horses to North Idaho events, schools, and families for years.

The business exists in part to honor Stacie's brother Billy, who had Down syndrome and passed away in 2025. The long-term goal is a fully accessible therapy ranch — with wagon ramps for wheelchairs, safe interaction spaces, and respite programs for caregivers. Every appearance moves that mission forward.

When companies sponsor Tribal Cowboy, they're investing in that ranch. We say it plainly and we mean it.
