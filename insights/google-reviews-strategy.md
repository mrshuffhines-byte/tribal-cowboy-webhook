# Google Reviews Strategy — Tribal Cowboy
## April 2026

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS

You have zero Google reviews visible right now. A parent searching "pony party North Idaho" or an event planner googling "horse entertainment Coeur d'Alene" sees a business with no social proof. Even 10 reviews with a 4.8+ star rating changes the entire picture. Google also ranks businesses with more reviews higher in local search results.

---

## STEP 1: Set Up Google Business Profile (if not already done)

Since you travel to events (no storefront), set up a **service-area business** profile:

1. Go to business.google.com and create or claim your listing
2. Business name: **Tribal Cowboy**
3. Category: **"Event Planner"** (primary) — add secondary categories: "Pony Ride Service," "Photographer," "Party Entertainment Service"
4. **Do NOT show your home address publicly** — set it as service-area only
5. Add service areas: Athol, Coeur d'Alene, Hayden, Post Falls, Rathdrum, Spirit Lake, Sandpoint, Spokane, Spokane Valley
6. Add photos: horses, events, the wagon, the property. Google listings with photos get 42% more direction requests
7. Add your website, phone, and hours

---

## STEP 2: Get Your Direct Review Link

Once your profile is live, get your short review link:

1. Search for your business on Google
2. Click "Ask for reviews" or go to your Google Business dashboard
3. Copy the short link — it looks like: g.page/tribalcowboy/review
4. Save this link. You'll use it everywhere.

---

## STEP 3: Ask for Reviews (Timing + Template)

**When to ask:** Within 24-48 hours after the event. After 72 hours, response rates drop hard.

**How to ask:** Text message is the highest-converting method. Email works too. Don't ask at the event — let the experience settle first.

**Template (text message):**

> Hey [Name]! Thanks for having Tribal Cowboy at [event name]. If the horses made it special, we'd love a quick Google review — takes about 60 seconds and means a lot to a small business: [REVIEW LINK]

**Template (email, for event planners/corporate):**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for bringing Tribal Cowboy in for [event]. We loved being part of it.
>
> If you have 60 seconds, a Google review would help other organizers find us. Here's the link: [REVIEW LINK]
>
> Either way, we'd love to work with you again. Thanks for the opportunity.
>
> Stacie
> Tribal Cowboy
> (208) 261-2092

**Follow-up:** If no response in 3-5 days, one follow-up is fine. After that, let it go.

**Never do:**
- Offer discounts or free photos for reviews (violates Google policy)
- Ask only happy customers (Google can detect this)
- Write reviews for people or ask them to say specific things

---

## STEP 4: Targets

| Milestone | What it unlocks |
|-----------|----------------|
| 5 reviews | Google shows your star rating in search |
| 10-15 reviews | Baseline credibility for most customers |
| 20+ reviews | Competitive in local search rankings |
| 50+ reviews | Strong local SEO signal, top-3 map pack potential |

**Goal: 15 reviews by end of summer 2026.** That's roughly one review per event. Very doable.

---

## STEP 5: Display Reviews on Your Website

Squarespace doesn't have a built-in Google Reviews widget. Options:

**Option A: EmbedSocial (recommended)**
- Free plan available
- Auto-updates when new reviews come in
- Clean widget that matches your site design
- Add via Code Block in Squarespace (requires Business plan)

**Option B: Manual quotes**
- Until you have enough reviews for a widget, manually copy your best 3-5 reviews into a testimonial section on the homepage and service pages
- Update quarterly

**Where to put them:**
- Homepage (below the hero or near the mission section)
- Pony Parties page
- For Event Planners page
- Corporate Events page

---

## STEP 6: Make It a Habit

After every event:
1. Send the review request text within 24 hours
2. Respond to every Google review (positive or negative) within 48 hours — Google rewards active profiles
3. Once a month, check your Google Business insights to see how many people found you through search
