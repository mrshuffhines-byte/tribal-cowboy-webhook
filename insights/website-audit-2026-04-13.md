# Tribal Cowboy Website Audit — April 13, 2026

Full UX, copy, conversion, SEO, and credibility review of tribalcowboy.com with a prioritized action plan for increasing profitable local bookings — especially from event organizers (festivals, farmers markets, corporate events).

---

## 1. Quick Snapshot

- **No clear path for event organizers.** The site speaks mostly to families and individual bookings. A festival director, farmers market manager, or corporate event planner has to piece together what you offer them, how much it costs, and why it's worth it. This is the single biggest missed revenue opportunity on the site.
- **The About page is your strongest asset — and it's buried.** Billy's story, Stacie's background, the dream of the ranch — that's the kind of content that makes people choose you over a cheaper option. But a busy parent or event planner will never scroll that far unless you surface pieces of it earlier.
- **Pricing is visible (good) but inconsistent.** Pony parties show clear tiered pricing. Photography props says "Contact Us" for every tier. Corporate events returned a 404. Inconsistency makes people feel like they're going to get a runaround.
- **Same-day photo delivery is undersold.** This is a genuine differentiator — facial recognition QR delivery is not something any local competitor offers. It's mentioned, but it's not demonstrated. A 15-second visual walkthrough or a before/after (scan QR → photos on your phone) would do more than any paragraph of text.
- **No Google reviews, no real event photos from festivals or corporate gigs, no case studies.** Trust signals are thin for the higher-dollar bookings you want more of.

## 2. Detailed Findings by Section

### A. Overall Site

**What's working:**
- Clean, modern design. Not cluttered. Loads reasonably fast.
- The "Not a petting zoo. Not a rental. A real working herd." line is strong positioning — sets you apart immediately.
- Pricing on pony parties is transparent and builds trust.
- The footer with "Indigenous Owned • Nisenan Maidu" is understated and dignified — exactly right.
- Service area listed clearly (Athol, Hayden, Spirit Lake, CdA, Rathdrum, Post Falls, Spokane, Spokane Valley).

**What needs work:**
- **No dedicated page or section for event organizers** (festival directors, market managers, corporate planners). These are your highest-dollar, most repeatable clients and the site doesn't speak to them directly.
- **Corporate Events page returns a 404.** That's a dead end for anyone clicking through from your services menu or Google.
- **Carriage Rides page also 404s.** Two broken service pages means lost bookings and wasted ad spend if you're driving traffic.
- **No testimonials on the homepage.** The pony parties page has three good ones, but the homepage — where most visitors land — has zero social proof from real people.
- **No Google review widget or link.** If you have Google reviews (and you should be actively collecting them), they should be visible on the homepage and every service page.
- **Navigation is cluttered.** The Services dropdown has 8 items (Corporate Events, Horse Photography Props, Pony Parties, Carriage Rides, School Programs & Community Events, Brand Partnerships, Portrait Sessions, Photography). Some of these overlap (Portrait Sessions vs. Photography vs. Horse Photography Props). A first-time visitor doesn't know which one to click.

---

### B. Home Page Hero

**Current hero:**
- Headline: "We bring the horses."
- Subhead: "Pony parties, wagon rides, horse photography, and corporate events across North Idaho and Eastern Washington — with same-day photo delivery built into every booking."
- CTAs: "See All Services" | "Check Availability"

**Assessment:**
The headline is catchy but vague. "We bring the horses" doesn't tell a first-time visitor what they're looking at. A parent planning a birthday party and a festival director scouting vendors have very different needs — and neither one sees their specific situation reflected here.

The subhead is doing the heavy lifting but it reads like a list, not a hook. It's also long enough that on mobile, most of it probably falls below the fold.

"See All Services" is a weak primary CTA — it delays the decision. "Check Availability" is better but links to a mailto, which is fine, though a form would convert higher (lower friction than composing an email).

**Missing from the hero:**
- The word "Idaho" or "North Idaho" in the headline itself (SEO and instant geographic clarity)
- Any mention of Indigenous-owned (a genuine differentiator, especially for corporate/government bookings)
- A visual demonstration of the photo delivery — even a small phone mockup showing the QR scan

---

### C. Navigation & Footer

**Navigation issues:**
- 8 service items in one dropdown is too many. Visitors don't read menus — they scan. Group them: "Family Events" (pony parties, carriage rides), "Business & Community" (corporate events, school programs, festivals), "Photography" (props, portraits, sessions).
- "Brand Partnerships" in the main nav is premature unless you're actively getting inbound partnership inquiries. It can live on the About page or as a secondary link.
- No "For Event Planners" or "Festivals & Markets" link. This is the audience you said you want more of — give them a front door.

**Footer:**
- Clean and functional. Contact info, social links, and the Indigenous-owned line are all there.
- Consider adding a one-line CTA in the footer: "Planning a festival or corporate event? Let's talk." with a direct link to a contact form or email.

### D. Pony Parties Page

**Strengths:**
- "Small Horses. Big Reactions." is a great headline. Specific, visual, human.
- Clear tiered pricing ($300 / $450 / $600) with add-ons. This is best-in-class for a local service business.
- The 4-step "How It Works" section is exactly what a busy parent needs.
- Three real testimonials — these are doing serious conversion work.
- FAQ section covers the right questions (duration, cost, age range, photo delivery).

**Improvements:**
- The final CTA says "Ready to Make Some Memories?" — this is a cliché your own brand guide bans. Replace it.
- No photos visible in the fetch (may be present visually but not in the markup as alt text — check that every image has descriptive alt text for SEO and accessibility).
- Add a "What parents ask most" mini-FAQ higher on the page — before pricing. Parents want to know: Is it safe? How much space do you need? Do you stay the whole time? What if it rains?
- Add a note about weather/cancellation policy. Parents worry about this and if they can't find the answer, some will bounce rather than email.

---

### E. Horse Photography Props Page

**Strengths:**
- "Real horses. For your shoot." is clear and strong.
- Four use cases (real estate, mini sessions, video/advertising, creative projects) are well-defined.
- Good positioning as a professional service, not a novelty.

**Improvements:**
- **Pricing is hidden behind "Contact Us" for every single tier.** This is the opposite of the pony parties page and it will cost you inquiries. Photographers and real estate agents are comparing options quickly. If they can't see a starting price, many will move on. Even "Starting at $XXX" is better than nothing.
- No testimonials from photographers or agents who've used the service.
- No sample gallery or portfolio link. A photographer hiring horse props wants to see what the final shots look like.
- The page title says "Horse Photography Props" but the service is really "horses for your photo or video shoot." Consider whether "Horse Photography Props" is how your customers would search for this, or if they'd search "rent horses for photoshoot North Idaho" or "horse props for real estate photos."

### F. Corporate Events Page — 404 ERROR

This page is broken. Anyone clicking "Corporate Events" from your navigation hits a dead end. This is your most critical fix because:
- Corporate events start at $800+ and are your highest-margin service
- Festival and market organizers would logically click here
- If you're running any ads or have any backlinks pointing to this URL, they're wasted

**Immediate action:** Get this page live, even if it's minimal. A headline, 3 bullets on what's included, starting pricing, and a CTA to email or fill out a form.

### G. School Programs & Community Events Page

**Strengths:**
- 500+ students served is a good proof point.
- Clear program formats (half-day, full-day, community events).
- Discounts for Title I schools and 501(c)(3)s — smart and community-minded.

**Improvements:**
- No testimonials from teachers, principals, or program directors.
- No photos of actual school programs.
- "Pricing depends on your group size, program length, travel distance" is honest but vague. A school administrator with a fixed budget needs at least a "starting at" number to know if this is even feasible before they email.

---

### H. About Page

**This is the emotional core of the site and it's excellent.** Billy's story, the dream of the ranch, Stacie's identity as Nisenan Maidu — this is content that makes people root for the business and choose it over a faceless competitor.

**Improvements:**
- Surface the Billy story (or a one-line teaser) on the homepage. Something like: "Every booking helps build a permanent ranch for people with Down syndrome to ride, drive, and connect with horses." That single line changes the emotional weight of every service page.
- The "Book an event. Help build the dream." CTA at the bottom is strong. Make sure it links directly to a booking action, not just the services overview.
- Add a photo of Stacie with the horses if not already present (the fetch can't confirm visuals, but this matters enormously for trust).

---

### I. Contact Page

**Current state:** Simple form (name, email, message) + phone + email + scheduling link.

**Improvements:**
- **Add service-specific fields to the form.** At minimum: Event type (dropdown: birthday party, corporate event, festival/market, photography, school program, other), preferred date, estimated guest count, location. This saves you a round of back-and-forth emails and qualifies leads before they hit your inbox.
- **Add a "For Event Planners" callout** near the form: "Planning a festival, market, or corporate event? Include your event dates and expected attendance and we'll send you a custom quote within one business day."
- **The scheduling link (tribalcowboy.as.me)** is mentioned but not prominently featured. If this is a real booking calendar, it should be a primary CTA button, not a footer link.
- "Let's talk horses" as a headline is warm and on-brand, but adding "or check your date" would make the action clearer.

## 3. Prioritized 10-Step Action Plan

Ranked from highest to lowest impact on getting more profitable local bookings, especially from event organizers.

### Step 1: Fix the Broken Pages (Corporate Events + Carriage Rides)
**Impact: Critical / Effort: Low**

Two service pages return 404 errors. Corporate Events is your highest-dollar service. Fix these today. Even a placeholder page with a headline, 3 bullets, starting price, and a "Request a Quote" button is better than a dead link.

### Step 2: Create a "For Event Planners" Page or Section
**Impact: Very High / Effort: Medium**

Build a dedicated page (or a prominent homepage section) that speaks directly to festival directors, farmers market managers, and corporate event planners. Include:
- What you bring (horses, wagon, handlers, photography, branded overlays)
- Event types you serve (festivals, farmers markets, corporate family days, holiday events, grand openings)
- Starting pricing for large events
- What makes you different from a petting zoo or pony rental (you handle everything, instant branded photos, Indigenous-owned)
- A downloadable one-sheet PDF they can share with their event committee
- 2-3 testimonials or mini case studies from past events
- A direct CTA: "Tell us about your event" with a tailored form

### Step 3: Add Testimonials and Social Proof to the Homepage
**Impact: High / Effort: Low**

Pull your best 3-4 testimonials to the homepage. Include the person's role if possible ("Festival Director, Athol Harvest Fest" carries more weight than just a first name). If you have Google reviews, embed a widget or screenshot showing your rating and review count.

### Step 4: Upgrade the Contact Form to Qualify Leads
**Impact: High / Effort: Low-Medium**

Replace the basic name/email/message form with a short intake form:
- Your name
- Email
- Phone (optional)
- Event type (dropdown)
- Preferred date(s)
- Estimated guest count
- Location / venue
- Anything else we should know (open text)

This reduces your back-and-forth emails by at least one round per inquiry and helps you prioritize high-value leads.

### Step 5: Simplify Navigation
**Impact: Medium-High / Effort: Low**

Restructure the Services dropdown from 8 items to 3-4 groups:
- **Parties & Family Events** (pony parties, carriage rides)
- **Festivals, Markets & Corporate** (large events, corporate, community)
- **Photography** (photography props, portrait sessions)
- **School Programs**

Add a top-level "For Event Planners" link outside the dropdown — make it visible and obvious.

### Step 6: Rewrite the Homepage Hero
**Impact: Medium-High / Effort: Low**

Make the headline geographically specific, service-clear, and welcoming to both families and event organizers. (See example rewrite below.)

### Step 7: Surface the Mission on the Homepage
**Impact: Medium / Effort: Low**

Add a short section (3-4 sentences max) on the homepage that teases the Billy story and the ranch dream. This emotional hook converts fence-sitters and gives event organizers a reason to choose you that goes beyond price. Link to the full About page.

### Step 8: Show Pricing on the Photography Props Page
**Impact: Medium / Effort: Low**

Add "starting at" pricing to the photography props page. You don't need to lock in exact numbers — even ranges help. "Single horse, up to 2 hours: starting at $250" is infinitely better than "Contact Us" repeated three times.

### Step 9: Build an SEO Content Strategy
**Impact: Medium (compounds over time) / Effort: Medium-High**

Your site has almost no blog or long-form content. This means you're invisible on Google for dozens of searches your ideal customers are making. Priority content pieces:

**For families:**
1. "Horse Birthday Parties in North Idaho: What to Expect and How to Book" (targets: horse birthday party Coeur d'Alene, pony party North Idaho)
2. "Pony Rides in North Idaho: Ages, Safety, and What's Included" (targets: pony rides near me, pony rides Coeur d'Alene)
3. "Clydesdale Wagon Rides in Idaho: Everything You Need to Know" (targets: wagon rides Athol Idaho, Clydesdale rides near me)

**For event organizers:**
4. "How Pony Rides Boost Family Attendance at Farmers Markets" (positions you as an expert vendor for market managers)
5. "Adding Horses to Your Festival: What Organizers Need to Know" (targets: festival entertainment ideas, festival vendor North Idaho)
6. "Corporate Family Day Ideas That Actually Work" (targets: corporate event entertainment Idaho, team building Spokane)
7. "Why Wagon Rides Increase Dwell Time at Holiday Events" (speaks directly to event ROI)

**For photographers:**
8. "How to Use Real Horses in Your Next Photo Session: A North Idaho Guide" (targets: horse photography props, equine photo session Idaho)

**For the brand:**
9. "What Indigenous-Owned Means for Tribal Cowboy" (builds authority, attracts values-aligned corporate clients)
10. "Behind the Scenes: What It Takes to Bring Horses to Your Event" (builds trust, shows professionalism)

### Step 10: Improve Accessibility and Technical Basics
**Impact: Low-Medium / Effort: Low-Medium**

Based on what's visible in the page content:
- Ensure every image has descriptive alt text (e.g., "Clydesdale horse standing next to a young girl at a birthday party in North Idaho" not just "horse" or blank).
- Check color contrast on all text, especially over images or colored backgrounds.
- Make sure the phone number and email are clickable (tel: and mailto: links) on mobile.
- Add structured data (LocalBusiness schema) so Google shows your address, phone, hours, and reviews in search results.
- Set up proper page titles for SEO. Each page should follow the pattern: "[Service] | Tribal Cowboy — North Idaho Horse Events & Photography"
  - Home: "Tribal Cowboy — Horse Events, Pony Parties & Equine Photography | North Idaho"
  - Pony Parties: "Pony Parties & Horse Birthday Parties | Tribal Cowboy — North Idaho"
  - Photography: "Horse Photography Props & Equine Sessions | Tribal Cowboy — North Idaho"
  - For Event Planners: "Festival, Market & Corporate Event Entertainment | Tribal Cowboy — North Idaho"

## 4. Example Rewrites

### A. New Homepage Hero Section

**Current:**
> Headline: "We bring the horses."
> Subhead: "Pony parties, wagon rides, horse photography, and corporate events across North Idaho and Eastern Washington — with same-day photo delivery built into every booking."
> CTAs: "See All Services" | "Check Availability"

**Proposed:**

> **Headline:** "Real horses. Real photos. Right here in North Idaho."
>
> **Subhead:** "Pony parties, wagon rides, and equine photography for families — plus full-service horse experiences for festivals, markets, and corporate events. Indigenous-owned. Every guest goes home with photos on their phone."
>
> **Primary CTA:** "Check Your Date" (links to contact form with date field)
> **Secondary CTA:** "Planning a Festival or Corporate Event?" (links to the For Event Planners page)

**Why this works better:**
- "Real horses. Real photos. Right here in North Idaho." is geographically specific (SEO), establishes credibility (real, not rented), and names the photo delivery angle in three words.
- The subhead now speaks to both audiences in one breath: families first, then event organizers.
- "Indigenous-owned" appears above the fold without being a gimmick — it's stated as fact.
- "Check Your Date" is a stronger CTA than "Check Availability" because it implies a specific action and feels faster.
- The second CTA gives event organizers their own clear door in.

## Summary

The bones of this site are solid. The design is clean, the voice is mostly right, and the pony parties page is genuinely well-built. The biggest gaps are structural, not aesthetic: broken pages, no path for event organizers, thin social proof on the homepage, and zero blog content for SEO.

The single highest-ROI move is fixing the Corporate Events 404 and building a "For Event Planners" page. That's the door your most profitable customers are looking for — and right now it doesn't exist.
