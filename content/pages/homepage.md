---

## WHAT'S CHANGING AND WHY

The current homepage has solid bones — clean design, good positioning line ("Not a petting zoo"), and clear service area. But the audit flagged several gaps:

1. **Hero is vague.** "We bring the horses" doesn't tell a first-time visitor what this business does or where it operates. No geographic anchor, no audience clarity.
2. **Zero testimonials.** The homepage has no social proof. The pony parties page has great ones — the homepage has none.
3. **No path for event organizers.** A festival director or corporate planner lands here and sees a site aimed at families. No door for them.
4. **The Billy story / mission is buried on the About page.** This is the emotional core of the business and it's invisible unless someone clicks through 2-3 pages deep.
5. **Photo delivery is mentioned but not demonstrated.** Same-day branded QR delivery is a genuine differentiator — it needs more than a sentence.
6. **Services section links to mailto for 3 of 5 services.** That's friction. Every service should link to its own page now that we've built them.

This rewrite addresses all six.

Tribal Cowboy is Indigenous-owned (Nisenan Maidu), based in Athol, Idaho, and serves all of North Idaho and Eastern Washington.

**CTA:** Read Our Full Story → /about

### Squarespace Setup Notes:
- This is the emotional anchor of the homepage. The audit flagged this as the most powerful content on the entire site — and it was completely buried on the About page.

- The image matters here. This should feel like a real family, not a stock photo. Stacie with Billy and the horses would be ideal.
