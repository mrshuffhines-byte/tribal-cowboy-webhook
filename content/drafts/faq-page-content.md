### Booking & Availability

**How far in advance should I book?**
Spring and summer dates fill fast — most weekends book 4–8 weeks out. For large events, school programs, or summer parties, the earlier the better. Off-season (fall and winter) usually has more flexibility. Check availability at tribalcowboy.as.me or email info@tribalcowboy.com.

**Do you require a deposit?**
Yes. A 25% deposit holds your date. The remaining balance is due before or at the event. We accept most payment methods — confirm at booking.

**Can I book last minute?**
Sometimes. If we have an opening, we'll do our best to make it work. Email info@tribalcowboy.com or DM us on Instagram @TribalCowboy with your date and we'll check.

**What happens if it rains?**
Light rain is usually fine — horses work in the rain and so do we. For heavy weather, lightning, or unsafe conditions we'll work with you to reschedule. We don't cancel without good reason.

### Logistics & Space

**What areas do you serve?**
We're based in Athol, Idaho and serve North Idaho and Eastern Washington — including Coeur d'Alene, Hayden, Spirit Lake, Rathdrum, Post Falls, Spokane, and Spokane Valley. Travel fees may apply for locations outside this area. Email us and we'll let you know.

**How much space is needed for pony rides?**
For pony rides, plan for roughly 30 feet of open space in any direction — enough room for the pony to walk a circle comfortably. For wagon rides, you'll need a wider path and clearance for the pony and wagon to turn. We'll confirm space requirements when you book.

**What surfaces work?**
Grass and packed dirt are ideal. Gravel works. Pavement is fine for short visits. Bare asphalt in heat is not suitable — it's hard on hooves. If you're unsure about your venue, send us the address and we'll advise.

**Can you set up indoors?**
No. All of our services require outdoor space. Covered outdoor areas (pavilions, open barns) are great.

**Do you need electricity or water on-site?**
No electricity needed. For longer events (3+ hours), access to water for the animals is helpful but not required — we come prepared.

### Safety

**Are pony rides safe for toddlers?**
Yes. Our ponies are trained for events with young children. A handler walks with every rider. We do not leave a child unattended on an animal, ever.

**What's the minimum age for riding?**
Children who can sit upright independently — typically 2 years and older — can ride with a handler walking alongside. For very young children, a parent can walk with them during the ride. We use our judgment at the event based on the child's size and comfort.

**Are the horses trained for crowds and noise?**
Yes. Our horses and ponies are specifically conditioned for event environments — music, crowds, balloons, bouncy castles, all of it. We don't bring animals that aren't ready for that setting.

### Pricing

**How much do pony rides cost in North Idaho?**
Pony visits start at $300 for 2 hours. Pony-drawn wagon rides start at $550 for 2 hours. Additional time is available. See the full pricing table on our [Services page](/services).

**How much does a Clydesdale event cost?**
Clydesdale carriage experiences start at $800 for a standard 4-person wagon. The Cinderella carriage option starts at $1,100. Pricing is based on duration and event type. Contact us for a custom quote.

### Services

**What is the horse photography experience?**
We bring horses and decorated wagons to your location as live props for a professional photo session. You or your photographer shoot — we handle the animals. Our Clydesdales stand over 1,800 pounds and are stunning on camera. Photo sessions start at $250 for 1 hour.

**Can I hire Tribal Cowboy for a school visit?**
Yes. We bring horses to schools, community centers, libraries, and tribal programs for hands-on educational visits. Stacie teaches the history of horses in the Pacific Northwest alongside real horsemanship. Programs can align with curriculum goals or run as open experiential time with the animals. Contact us at info@tribalcowboy.com to discuss your program.

**Do you travel to Spokane?**
Yes. We regularly serve Spokane and Spokane Valley. Travel fees may apply. Email us with your event details and we'll put together a quote.

**Is Tribal Cowboy Indigenous-owned?**
Yes. Tribal Cowboy is owned and led by Stacie Huffhines, Nisenan Maidu. That identity is the foundation of this business — the values, the relationship with animals, and the commitment to community.
