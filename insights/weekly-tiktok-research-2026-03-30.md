# Weekly TikTok Research — March 30, 2026

## Top Finds This Week

- **"Slow content" is the dominant format trend in March 2026.** TikTok viewers are trading rapid-fire edits for videos that take time to show a full process. Day-in-the-life, grooming sequences, event setup walkthroughs — these are outperforming flashy edits. This plays directly to Tribal Cowboy's real-work content pillar.
- **Completion rate is now the #1 algorithm signal**, surpassing likes and follows. TikTok's viral threshold sits around 70% watch-through. Short (15–30 sec), tight videos with a strong hook and satisfying payoff get pushed hardest. Implication: open with the Clydesdale walking toward camera, end with the kid's face.
- **@michaelaredeker continues dominating Clydesdale TikTok** with Roman riding and bridleless draft horse content. The takeaway isn't trick riding — it's that "big horse doing something unexpected" stops the scroll every single time.
- **Megan Moroney is fueling country TikTok this month.** "6 Months Later" hit #1 on country radio, "Beautiful Things" is climbing Billboard, and her Ed Sheeran duet "I Only Miss You" is everywhere. Perfect for montage-style videos of guest reactions or behind-the-scenes moments.
- **Harry Styles' "Aperture" is the go-to sound for cinematic day-in-the-life content.** Album dropped March 6, giving 11+ potential trending tracks. The slow-build energy pairs well with sunrise-at-the-barn, grooming, and trailer-loading footage.

- Businesses like Ponies For Hire ($425/hr for two ponies + pygmy goats), Horse N Round Fun (Ohio), Sunset Trails Stables, and Caribou Creek Outfitters are active in the market but have minimal TikTok presence

## Hook Breakdown

**Top 5 hook formats from this week's research:**

1. **The Question Loop** — "What happens when a 4-year-old meets an 1,800-lb horse for the first time?" Opens a curiosity gap the viewer has to close. 63% of top-performing TikToks use a question or curiosity hook in the first 3 seconds.

2. **The Size Reveal** — Camera starts on something normal-sized (a fence post, a person, a saddle), then pans or pulls back to reveal the Clydesdale's full scale. The brain can't help but compare. Built-in share trigger.

3. **The Counter-Narrative** — "Everyone told me a Clydesdale couldn't be a party horse." Then show the Clydesdale at a birthday party being gentle and perfect. Pattern interrupt: viewer expects one thing, gets another.

4. **The Process Hook** — "Here's what it actually takes to bring horses to your event." Then show 5 AM wake-up, grooming, trailer loading, setup. "Slow content" format. Real work is interesting when people have never seen it.

5. **The Text Overlay Reveal** — Black screen or static shot with text: "She said she wanted a horse for her birthday." Cut to the Clydesdale walking up the driveway. Text overlays drive completion rate because people read faster than they watch — they stay to see if the video matches the promise.

**Key stat:** TikTok has a hard checkpoint at 3 seconds. If viewers leave before that, the video stops getting pushed. Every hook has to earn those first 3 seconds.

## Content Ideas for Tribal Cowboy

**1. "People don't realize how big our Clydesdales actually are."**
Hook: Open on Stacie's hand next to Millie's hoof. Pan up slowly. Let the size speak.
Format: 15–20 seconds, trending sound or original audio.
Why: Size-comparison is the #1 performing format in the Clydesdale niche. Simple, repeatable, shareable.

**2. "You scan a QR code. Your photos just appear."**
Hook: Text overlay: "We don't do event photography like anyone else." Show a guest scanning the code, their phone lighting up with branded photos.
Format: 15 seconds, punchy.
Why: Nobody in this space has facial recognition photo delivery. This is genuinely novel and people will share it out of disbelief.

**3. "Your party starts at 2 PM. Our day starts at 5 AM."**
Hook: Text on screen with the contrast. Show the hauling, grooming, trailer loading, early morning coffee, arrival, setup.
Format: 30–45 seconds, day-in-the-life. Use "Aperture" by Harry Styles.
Why: "Slow content" is trending. Nobody in the pony party space is showing the work behind the party.

**4. "She said she wanted a horse for her birthday."**
Hook: Text on black screen. Cut to Clydesdale walking up a suburban driveway. Kid's reaction.
Format: 20 seconds. Megan Moroney's "Beautiful Things" underneath.
Why: Kid-reaction + horse + surprise = shared by parents and horse lovers alike.

**5. "I'm Nisenan Maidu. I own a horse business in Idaho. Here's what that looks like."**
Hook: Stacie straight to camera, 3-second identity statement. Cut to horses, land, events.
Format: 30–45 seconds, talk-to-camera energy. Original audio.
