## What's Working in Competitors' Content

### Formats
- **Carousels** dominate — most of the higher-engagement posts are Sidecar (multi-image)
- Single horse portrait / cute animal reveal posts also perform
- Text-only or generic "book us" posts get near-zero engagement

### Caption patterns that pull engagement
- Personal, story-forward: "Last weekend we celebrated Lizzie turning 9..." — names the kid, tells the story
- New animal reveals: "Our newest addition, 'Q-Tip'" — curiosity hook
- Guest POV: The highest-engagement post was a parent posting ABOUT a competitor's pony party — not the business posting about itself
- Short + specific beats long + generic

### What nobody is doing
- **Branded photo overlays** — zero competitors mention custom event branding on photos
- **Facial recognition / instant delivery** — The system is completely unmentioned
- **Draft horses / Clydesdales** — everyone is running mini horses or Shetlands; nobody is bringing a 1,800-lb horse to a birthday party
- **Indigenous-owned identity** — completely absent from this space

## Strategic Takeaways for Tribal Cowboy

1. **The differentiation post writes itself** — nobody in this hashtag is doing custom branded photo overlays or instant facial recognition delivery. A clear "here's what we do differently" post targeting this hashtag would stand out immediately.

2. **Name the kids** (with permission) — competitor posts that name the birthday child outperform generic "we did a party" posts. "Lizzie turning 9" gets more engagement than "birthday celebrations."

3. **Carousel = better reach** — nearly all higher-engagement posts use multiple images. Single-photo posts consistently underperform.

4. **The Clydesdale is the hook** — nobody else in this space has a draft horse. Millie and Abby are the differentiators. Lead with them more.

5. **Post volume in this hashtag is low** — #ponyparty only returned 24 posts in recent days. This is a low-competition hashtag worth owning consistently.
