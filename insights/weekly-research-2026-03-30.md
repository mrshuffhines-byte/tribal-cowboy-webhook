## Content Ideas for Tribal Cowboy

1. **"What 1,800 pounds looks like next to a 4-year-old"** — Side-by-side or video of a kid meeting Millie or Abby for the first time. Clydesdale Outpost proves that size-comparison content is a scroll-stopper. Keep the caption grounded: the kid's name (with permission), the horse's name, what actually happened. No "magical moment" language — the photo does that work.

2. **"Your photos found you before you got to the car"** — A Reel or carousel explaining the facial recognition photo delivery. Show the QR code scan, the phone loading, the reaction. This is Tribal Cowboy's biggest differentiator and nobody else is talking about it. Hook: "You didn't even know we took this one."

3. **"5 AM haul day"** — Behind-the-scenes Reel of loading the trailer, the drive, the setup. The work content pillar is underused across the entire pony party industry. Everyone shows the party — almost nobody shows the labor. This is free authenticity.

4. **"She said she wasn't a horse person"** — Guest reaction compilation. Every pony party and horse experience account that does well leans on genuine human reactions. Collect 5–10 seconds of adults saying "oh my god" when they see the Clydesdales up close. Set it to simple music. No text overlay needed.

5. **"What people ask me when I say I'm Nisenan Maidu and I run a horse business"** — A talking-head Reel or text-over-video that addresses real questions Stacie gets. Not educational in a lecture way — just honest, warm, a little funny. This fills the Indigenous identity pillar without making it a sales angle. Nobody in this space is doing this kind of content.

## Trending Hooks & Formats

**Formats getting traction right now:**

- **"Get ready with me" barn edition** — Morning routine Reels showing feeding, grooming, tacking up. The equestrian community loves these and they consistently hit the Explore page.
- **Horse personality POV** — Filming from the horse's perspective or narrating what the horse is "thinking." Humor content that performs well across horse accounts.
- **Before/after event setup** — Time-lapse or side-by-side of empty field → full event setup. Shows professionalism and effort.
- **Sound-on moments** — Clydesdale hoofbeats, horse breathing, kids laughing. ASMR-adjacent content is trending in animal accounts.
- **Carousel storytelling** — 5–7 slides telling the story of one event, one guest, or one day. Carousels still get the highest save rates on Instagram.
- **User-generated content reposts** — Sharing guest photos/videos with credit. Low effort, high trust, and the algorithm rewards the engagement from tagged users.

**Caption structures that are working:**

- Open with a specific detail, not a generic statement ("Millie stood completely still for 11 minutes while a 3-year-old braided her mane" beats "Our horses are so gentle with kids")
- One-line hooks that create curiosity: "This wasn't supposed to happen." / "I almost cancelled this one." / "Nobody warned her about the size."
- End with a soft CTA or question, not a sales push: "Have you ever been around a draft horse?" / "Tag someone who needs this in their life."
