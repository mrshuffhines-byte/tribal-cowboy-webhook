## Template 1: The Moment (Guest Reaction)

**When to use:** After an event, when you have a great photo or clip of a guest with a horse.
**Format:** Single image, carousel, or Reel (guest reaction compilations perform especially well as Reels)

```
[Set the scene in one sentence — who, what, where]

[The moment — what happened, what they said, or what their face did]

[One line that brings it home — why this matters or what it felt like]

[Optional: soft CTA — "Check availability at www.tribalcowboy.com"]

#TribalCowboy #NorthIdaho [3-5 more relevant tags]
```

**Example:**
```
A little girl in a sparkly birthday crown walked up to Millie and stopped dead in her tracks.

She whispered "he's SO big" and then immediately asked if she could come back tomorrow.

This is why we do this.

#TribalCowboy #PonyParty #ClydesdaleHorse #NorthIdaho #HorsesOfInstagram #HorseExperience
```

**Reel variation — reaction compilation:**
Collect 5–10 seconds of genuine reactions from multiple guests. Adults saying "oh my god" when they see the Clydesdales. Kids frozen in place. Set to simple music or no music. No text overlay needed — the faces are the content.

## Template 3: Size Comparison (NEW — research-driven)

**When to use:** Any time a human is next to a Clydesdale. This is the highest-performing format for draft horse accounts.
**Format:** Reel (preferred) or single image. Video of Clydesdales moving — running, pulling, walking toward camera — consistently outperforms static photos.

```
[A specific measurement or comparison — height, weight, hoof size, something concrete]

[What it's actually like to be near that much horse — one real detail]

[Optional: a question to the audience]

#TribalCowboy #ClydesdaleHorse #DraftHorseLove [3-5 more tags]
```

**Example:**
```
Millie is 17.2 hands and somewhere north of 1,800 pounds.

She also stands completely still while a kid half her leg height tries to braid her feathers.

People ask if the big ones are gentle. Gentle isn't the word. Patient in a way that doesn't make sense for something that size.

#TribalCowboy #ClydesdaleHorse #DraftHorseLove #HorseExperience #ClydesdalesOfInstagram #NorthIdaho #HorsesOfIdaho
```

**Why this works:** Clydesdale Outpost (114K followers) built their entire brand on this format. The size difference between human and horse is a scroll-stopper every time.

```

[What's happening — specific, not generic]

[Why it matters or what it's for]

[Optional: a wink at the audience — "worth it every time" or similar]

#TribalCowboy #BehindTheScenes [3-5 tags]
```

**Example:**
```
5am. Event load-out. The trailer doesn't care that it's cold.

Horses braided, equipment loaded, branded overlays packed.

The family won't see any of this — they'll just see their kids' faces when the ponies step off the trailer.

That's the whole point.

#TribalCowboy #NorthIdaho #HorseLife #BehindTheScenes #EquestrianLife #HorsesOfIdaho
```

**Reel variation — ASMR grooming:**
Close-up grooming footage. Brush strokes, hoof picking, mane braiding. All real audio — no music. The sounds are the hook. ASMR-adjacent animal content is trending across equestrian Instagram. Also strong for equine care brand partnerships.

## Caption Hook Formulas (Research-Driven)

These structures are working across equestrian Instagram right now:

1. **Specific detail opener:** "Millie stood completely still for 11 minutes while a 3-year-old braided her mane."
2. **Curiosity hook:** "This wasn't supposed to happen." / "I almost cancelled this one." / "Nobody warned her about the size."
3. **Time stamp opener:** "5am. Nobody asked for the day to start yet."
4. **Contrast hook:** "1,800 pounds. Completely silent."
5. **Direct address:** "You didn't even know we took this one."
6. **Soft question close:** "Have you ever been around a draft horse?" / "Tag someone who needs this."

**Avoid:** Starting with "At Tribal Cowboy..." / Starting with a question / Starting with an emoji / Generic openers like "Happy Monday"

## Format Priority (Spring 2026)

Based on current Instagram algorithm and competitor performance:

1. **Reels** — highest reach, especially with real audio (hoofbeats, grooming sounds, kid reactions)
2. **Carousels** — highest save rates, best for educational and "what you get" content
3. **Single image** — still works for strong standalone photos (North Idaho landscapes, horse portraits)
4. **Stories** — minimum 3x/week for engagement, polls, behind-the-scenes quick captures

Shift toward 60% Reels / 25% Carousels / 15% Single Image for feed posts.
