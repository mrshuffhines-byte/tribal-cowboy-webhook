# Regional Competitor Research — North Idaho / Eastern Washington
**Searches run:** #northidahohorses, #horsedrawncarriage, #wagonride, #spokanehorses, #idahoeventphotography
**Posts analyzed:** 109 across 5 searches
**Date:** March 21, 2026

---

## The Main Finding

**There is no direct competitor in North Idaho or Eastern Washington offering horse experiences as an event service.**

No one in this market is doing:
- Horse photography with branded overlays at events
- Clydesdale or draft horse wagon rides for hire
- Pony parties as a mobile event business
- Any combination of the above

The market is open.

## Strategic Conclusions

### 1. Tribal Cowboy owns this market by default
No one else in North Idaho or Eastern Washington is running horse experiences as a mobile event business. Every booking you get, you get because you're the only one showing up.

### 2. The photography service is the most uncontested differentiator
@avalanche_creek_photography_ does horse portraits. Nobody does branded photo overlays at events in this region. This is completely uncontested.

### 3. The Clydesdale is a geographic monopoly
There is no other Clydesdale available for hire at events within a 100+ mile radius based on this data. Draft horses pull attention. Nobody else has one in this market.

### 4. Regional hashtags are unclaimed
#northidahohorses gets almost no business use. If Tribal Cowboy posts consistently to that tag, you own it within weeks.

### 5. The only risk is invisibility — not competition
The gap isn't competitors — it's awareness. People in this region who want a horse experience at an event don't know Tribal Cowboy exists yet. Social content that shows up in regional hashtags and tags local venues/event planners is the growth lever.
