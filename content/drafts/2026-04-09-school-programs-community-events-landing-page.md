## Section 1: How It Works (Educational Process)

**Section Eyebrow:** THE EXPERIENCE

**Section Heading:** Here's How a School Program or Community Event Actually Works

### Step 1: You Tell Us Your Goals
Email or call with group size, grade level, what you're hoping to teach or celebrate, and your preferred date. Do you want animal care basics? Local Indigenous history? General farm experience? We customize. We confirm dates and send a program outline within 24 hours.

### Step 2: We Prepare the Space & Animals
We scout your location (school grounds, local park, venue property) or arrange for your group to visit our property. We set up safe interaction areas, prepare calm animals, and brief our handlers on your group's age and learning goals.

### Step 3: Kids Learn Through Interaction
Groups of 15–30 work best. Kids meet, pet, groom, and feed animals while learning. Our handlers answer questions and guide observation. Age-appropriate lessons on animal behavior, care, biology, or land stewardship are woven in naturally—no feels-like-school format.

### Step 4: Hands-On Activity Rotations (Optional)
Larger groups rotate through activity stations: grooming, feeding, photo time, questions with the handler. Keeps energy high and gives every kid genuine interaction time.

### Step 5: Debrief & Documentation
We stay to answer final questions. If your school or organization wants photos or learning materials captured, we deliver digital assets same day. Teachers often use the experience as a springboard for classroom follow-up work.

## Section 7: FAQ (Schools & Organizations)

**Q: How do you customize for different grade levels?**
A: K–2 is sensory and observation-based (touching, watching, basic facts). Grades 3–5 add animal care and biology basics. Middle school can explore behavior, STEM observation, or Indigenous land stewardship. We adjust language, complexity, and activities to match age.

**Q: What if we're a homeschool co-op or small group?**
A: Groups of 15–25 work great. We can do smaller or larger with adjusted pricing. Co-ops often do group rates and share the cost.

**Q: Can we visit your property instead of you coming to us?**
A: Yes. If your group wants to visit our location, we can arrange that. Brings kids to the animals' home environment, which some teachers love for the learning angle.

**Q: Do all kids have to touch the horses?**
A: No. Some kids are nervous or have sensory sensitivities. We create a space where kids can observe, ask questions, and participate at their own pace. Nervous kids often warm up. Some stay as observers, and that's okay.

**Q: Can we take photos?**
A: Yes. Families can take photos. If you want professional documentation, we can capture candid shots and provide digital files or prints for newsletters/yearbooks.

**Q: What if kids have allergies or fears?**
A: Tell us upfront. We'll work with the group to make sure every kid has a positive experience—whether that's interaction or supported observation. We've worked with kids who are allergic, anxious, or have had bad experiences. We take it seriously.

**Q: How do we book?**
A: Email info@tribalcowboy.com with your school/organization name, group size, grade level, preferred date, and any learning goals. We respond with options and pricing within 24 hours. Schools and nonprofits may qualify for adjusted rates.
