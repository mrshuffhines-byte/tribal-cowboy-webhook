## What is Alt Text (and Why It Matters)

**Alt text** is a brief description of an image. It appears when:
- An image doesn't load
- A screen reader reads your page (for blind/visually impaired visitors)
- Google Images indexes your photos

Good alt text helps:
1. **People with disabilities** — they can understand your content via screen reader
2. **Google** — it understands what the image is and indexes it for image search
3. **Accessibility compliance** — you avoid legal liability under accessibility laws

---

## Current Status

Most of your homepage images likely have **no alt text** or generic alt text like "image" or "photo." This is a quick win to fix.

---

## How to Add Alt Text in Squarespace

1. **Edit your page**
2. **Right-click on an image** (or click the image and look for an edit button)
3. Look for **"Alt Text"** or **"Description"** field
4. Enter the recommended text (see examples below)
5. **Save**

Repeat for all images on your site.

## Alt Text Best Practices

### DO:
✅ Describe what's actually in the image  
✅ Be specific and concise (10–15 words max)  
✅ Include relevant keywords naturally (horse, pony, North Idaho, Tribal Cowboy, etc.)  
✅ Describe the activity/action (petting, posing, riding, brushing)  
✅ Include people when relevant (family, kids, business team)  

### DON'T:
❌ Write "image" or "photo" (not descriptive)  
❌ Start with "An image of" or "A photo of" (redundant)  
❌ Make it too long (Google stops reading after ~125 characters)  
❌ Stuff keywords ("Pony party North Idaho horse birthday event pony petting horse North Idaho")  
❌ Leave it blank

## How This Helps Your SEO

1. **Image search traffic:** When someone searches "pony party North Idaho" on Google Images, your images show up (with good alt text)
2. **Organic traffic:** Alt text helps Google understand your pages better, improving overall rankings
3. **Accessibility:** You meet WCAG standards (Americans with Disabilities Act compliance)
4. **User experience:** Visitors see descriptions if images don't load

**Expected impact:** +5–10% image search traffic over 2–3 months as Google re-indexes your pages.

## Tools to Check Your Alt Text

### Free browser check:
1. Install **WAVE** (Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool) browser extension
2. Run it on your homepage
3. It'll show you every image missing alt text
4. Fix the ones it flags

### Free online check:
Go to **https://www.webaim.org/articles/alttext/** for detailed guidance.
