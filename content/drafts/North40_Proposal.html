Common Questions
Before You Ask
The Five Questions Every Decision-Maker Has
Q1 — Liability
What if a guest gets injured?
Tribal Cowboy carries $2M per-occurrence general liability insurance with a dedicated animal liability endorsement. Your company is named as additionally insured on the COI before the event date. Lead-line only riding, fully enclosed panels, and a minimum of two handlers per pony mean the activation is built around preventing injury. We have operated at schools, community events, and public venues without incident.
Q2 — Animal Safety
What if an animal behaves unpredictably?
Our animals are trained for public activations — not borrowed for the day. They're accustomed to crowds, children, noise, and unfamiliar settings. Our handlers read animal behavior continuously and remove any animal showing stress before it becomes a problem. Guests interact from one side of a fixed panel system; the animals are never loose in the activation area at any point.
Q3 — Permits & Compliance
Do we need permits or approvals?
Requirements vary by city and county. Tribal Cowboy handles all permit research and coordination — you don't manage this. We've navigated event permitting across multiple Idaho and Washington jurisdictions. If a permit is needed, we get it. If a health department notification is required, we handle that too. No action required from your team.
Q4 — Cleanup & Sanitation
What about animal waste and cleanup?
Entirely on us. Tribal Cowboy brings dedicated waste management supplies, cleanup materials, and sanitation equipment. The activation area is actively maintained throughout the event — not just at the end. The lot is fully cleared and restored within 90 minutes of close. We leave the parking lot the way we found it.
Q5 — Operations & Parking
How does this affect our lot and daily operations?
The activation uses approximately 6–8 standard parking spaces (40' × 60' footprint). Tribal Cowboy walks the site in advance with your store manager to confirm placement that keeps all traffic lanes, fire access, and store entrances clear. Setup happens before open, teardown is complete before close of business, and guest flow within the activation is managed entirely by our staff. Your team does not coordinate the event — you host it.
