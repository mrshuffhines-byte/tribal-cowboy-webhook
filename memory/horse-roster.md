## Content Rules for Writing About the Horses

- Always use first names — "Abby" not "our Clydesdale"
- Specific beats general — "Abby, a 7-year-old bay Clydesdale" beats "a beautiful horse"
- Personality and quirks make content shareable — use them when known
- Never describe the horses as props or backdrops — they are participants
- Weight/size specifics add credibility: Clydesdales typically 1,800–2,200 lbs, use if relevant
- Minis have their own distinct personalities and content angle — lean into size contrast humor when appropriate
