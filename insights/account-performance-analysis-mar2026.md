## Trends — What's Working

### 1. Clydesdales in dramatic seasonal settings = your highest engagement content
The #1 post is the clearest signal. Clydesdale + snow + simple honest line = 509 likes. No event, no sell, no elaborate story. Just Millie in the winter and two sentences. **Do this every season. Every weather event. Every sunrise.**

### 2. Question posts drive massive relative engagement
"Which animal would you interact with?" was #1 on Facebook and top 5 on Instagram. You get more comments per post when you ask one specific question. The format works for any content: "Which horse caught your eye first?" (22L 2C), "What would you do first on the ranch?" (19L 3C). **Every post should end with a question.**

### 3. Humor about the animals converts well
"Instructions unclear: I bought the spicy striped version" (26L 6C) and the farrier/Christmas trim video (29L, top Facebook) both got strong comments relative to your average. Horses being ridiculous is content. Lean into it.

### 4. Seasonal booking content with clear visuals and pricing drives shares
The Harvest Wagon Mini announcement (Sep 29) was the #2 Facebook post by far — 20 likes AND 5 shares. Shares mean strangers are sending this to people who might book. The formula: seasonal angle + real visual + clear price + easy CTA. **The mini session format is your best booking conversion vehicle.**

### 5. Real emotional moments build loyalty
Posts about Billy (Down Syndrome, both years), Stripes passing, and the "Core memory" Clydesdale + child moment all over-indexed on comments. This audience cares about you, not just the horses. Personal content keeps the followers you have.

### 6. Collaborative content with other local businesses multiplies reach
The Star Dust Ranch holiday video (376L 5C) was the #2 all-time. You reached their audience. Every collab post performs above average. This is an underused lever.

### 7. Video beats image for comments; image beats video for raw likes
- Top likes: Single image (509L)
- Top comments: Video posts dominate
- Best of both: Carousels (Sidecar) are severely underused — only 6 of 50 posts — but when used, they perform in the top half consistently

## Trends — What's Not Working

### Generic promotional posts with no visual hook
Posts that list services without a strong image get 5-10 likes regardless of how well-written they are. Text-forward booking posts with no visual don't pull.

### Posting without a question at the end
The lowest-engagement posts consistently have no question or interactive element. The caption does the work but the audience has nowhere to go.

### Repetitive booking push without the experience shown
Multiple posts pushing the same event (Harvest Wagon had 4-5 posts) see diminishing returns after the first announcement. First post = announcement + visual = strong. Repeat posts = reminder links = near zero engagement.

## Facebook-Specific Findings

Facebook's audience is smaller (244 likes) but highly engaged and more likely to share. This platform responds better to:
- Conversational, personal tone over promotional
- Question posts and polls
- Booking announcements with clear pricing (they share these to friends)
- Community content (local event involvement, school programs)

Facebook underperforms when:
- Posts are identical cross-posts from Instagram
- Caption is too short with no story ("Throwback to St. Patrick's Day 🍀🐴" = 1 like)

**What's strong:** Clear pricing, clear service area, booking form, professional positioning, "Horses That Show Up For You" tagline.

**What's missing:**
- No testimonials/reviews visible on booking page (significant gap for first-time visitors)
